# Morning Wire Editorial Policy

Morning Wire should feel like a concise editor prepared the morning briefing, not like a model filled six HTML files.

## Publishing Bar

An edition is publishable only when it satisfies all of these conditions:

- The edition date matches the current America/New_York date for the scheduled run.
- Each beat has source-backed stories from credible sources.
- The hub lede synthesizes the day instead of repeating every section card.
- Ticker values are consistent across every page and across the duplicated ticker loop.
- No story is based on a source URL that was guessed, fabricated, or not actually retrieved.
- No major claim appears without an attribution link nearby.

## Source Preference

Use primary sources when possible: company releases, government data, central banks, court filings, scientific journals, and official statements. Use high-quality secondary reporting for context and verification: Reuters, AP, Bloomberg, Financial Times, Wall Street Journal, CNBC, The Verge, TechCrunch, CoinDesk, Nature, Science, STAT, Al Jazeera, BBC, and similar outlets.

Avoid relying on low-quality aggregators, SEO content farms, anonymous reposts, or thin market blogs unless they are only used to support a minor noncritical detail.

## Beat Standards

- Tech & AI: model releases, major platform changes, enterprise adoption, regulation, infrastructure, chips, developer tools.
- Crypto: BTC/ETH moves, ETFs, stablecoins, DeFi infrastructure, exchange/regulatory events, major on-chain incidents.
- Markets: equities, macro data, rates, commodities, bonds, earnings, central banks.
- World: geopolitics, elections, diplomacy, conflict, humanitarian developments.
- Science & Health: peer-reviewed research, public health, space, climate, biotech, medical policy.

## Voice

The voice should be clear, specific, and restrained. Prefer short explanatory sentences. Do not pad. Do not use "could be a game changer" unless the source-backed stakes are genuinely clear.

## Email Standard

The morning email should be a readable digest, not the full website copied into an inbox. It should include the hub lede, the five lead cards, source links, and a link back to the live site.
