Wall Street's Best Day Since May as Iran Peace Hopes Ignite Rally

Markets erupted Tuesday with explosive gains as reports of potential diplomatic off-ramps in the Middle East conflict sent equities soaring. The Dow surged 1,125 points—a commanding 2.49% jump—while the S&P 500 climbed 2.91% and the tech-heavy Nasdaq catapulted 3.83% higher. The rally caps an explosive final day of Q1, demonstrating just how much geopolitical risk premium has weighed on valuations.

Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian signaled openness to de-escalation, and Trump administration statements suggesting a potential resolution within two to three weeks ignited investor optimism. As Q2 opens Wednesday, that momentum persists: S&P 500 futures advance 0.84%, Nasdaq futures up 1.12%, and Dow futures gaining 0.79%. Technology stocks lead the charge, with investors rotating back into growth equities after a brutal quarter marked by heightened geopolitical uncertainty. The breakthrough in sentiment marks a turning point—if fragile—for risk assets.

Market participants are carefully calibrating their positioning between the hope for reduced military tensions and the reality that crude prices remain historically elevated. Any further escalation could quickly reverse these gains, underscoring the persistent tail risk that will likely define Q2 trading dynamics.

Energy Crisis

Oil's Record 60% March Surge Reshapes Global Markets

Brent crude's astonishing 60% rally in March—lifting the benchmark to $110.69 per barrel—reflects a fundamental reshaping of global energy markets. The Strait of Hormuz disruption has knocked approximately 15 million barrels per day offline, creating the tightest supply conditions since the 1970s oil embargoes. Analysts now warn that Brent could average $125 per barrel throughout April, with potential spikes toward $150 if regional tensions intensify.

This price trajectory carries profound implications for inflation, central bank policy, and consumer purchasing power. Energy costs ripple through every asset class, and sustained elevated crude prices could become the key variable constraining equity valuations despite improved geopolitical sentiment.

US Gas Prices Hit $4/Gallon — Highest Since 2022

The national average for unleaded gasoline has crossed the $4.00 per gallon threshold for the first time since 2022, translating to tangible pain at the pump for American consumers. The surge reflects both crude oil's rally and reduced refinery capacity amid the regional conflict. Consumer sentiment surveys already show significant pressure on discretionary spending, and a sustained period above $4 could dampen economic growth.

Trump Tells Allies: 'Get Your Own Oil'

The administration's latest policy statement—that allied nations should secure their own energy supplies rather than rely on U.S. coordination—adds another layer of uncertainty to global energy security architecture. The message underscores a shift toward energy nationalism even as the Strait of Hormuz remains contested. Markets are pricing in a more fragmented, potentially higher-cost energy regime.

J.P. Morgan Still Sees $60 Brent Long-Term

Despite the headline price surge, major financial institutions maintain conviction that current levels are unsustainable if Middle East tensions resolve. J.P. Morgan's commodities research team pegs long-term fair value for Brent at approximately $60 per barrel, implying substantial downside risk if geopolitical risks ease. This perspective is crucial context for investors positioning for Q2—the current rally may be pricing peak crude, not a new equilibrium.

Equities & Indices

Tech Leads Q2 Charge as Optimism Returns

Technology stocks, battered throughout Q1 by geopolitical uncertainty and rising rates, reclaimed their leadership position Tuesday and into early Wednesday trading. Semiconductor, software, and high-growth names all showed outsized strength, suggesting that investors are rotating aggressively back into the growth narrative. If geopolitical risks continue easing, expect further leadership from the high-beta tech cohort.

Rate Pressure Easing Supports Risk Assets

Treasury yields retreated across the curve Tuesday, with the 10-year note pulling back as market participants reduced inflation expectations tied to the potential Middle East resolution. Lower rates provide critical support for equity valuations, particularly for long-duration growth assets. This technical backdrop—combined with geopolitical optimism—creates favorable conditions for equities to sustain higher levels.

Q1 Ends on High Note After Brutal Quarter

Despite the explosive close, Q1 2026 will be remembered for its volatility. Geopolitical shocks, rate uncertainty, and recession fears dominated early-quarter trading. Yet the final week's rally underscores how quickly sentiment can shift when geopolitical risk premia begin to decompress. Investors should remember that this strength comes after a quarter of significant drawdowns—positioning matters heading into the more uncertain months ahead.

Trade & Policy

Liberation Day Tariff Fallout Continues

Three months past the April 2 tariff rollout that markets dubbed "Liberation Day," questions persist about Supreme Court challenges and the viability of trade deal renegotiations. The tariff regime has created winners in domestic manufacturing and losers in import-dependent sectors, and any resolution to the Middle East conflict could accelerate discussions around trade normalization. Policy uncertainty remains elevated on this front.

IPO Pipeline Building as Markets Stabilize

As equity markets stabilize and risk sentiment improves, venture capital firms are accelerating companies toward public markets. The IPO pipeline is visibly building, particularly in artificial intelligence and adjacent sectors where mega-deal activity continues robust. A successful Q2 for equities could trigger a wave of late-stage company debuts in the second half of 2026.

Global Demand

IEA Cuts Global Oil Demand Growth Forecast

The International Energy Agency trimmed its global oil demand growth forecast by 210,000 barrels per day to 640,000 barrels daily, citing both the elevated price environment and deteriorating macro outlook. Higher crude prices typically suppress demand, particularly in emerging markets where affordability matters. This demand destruction could eventually cap oil price appreciation, though near-term supply constraints may dominate price discovery.

Gold Holds Near Record at $4,699

Gold remains bid near all-time highs despite the geopolitical optimism, as safe-haven demand continues elevated. Investors maintain substantial positioning in precious metals as insurance against both currency debasement and unresolved tail risks. The persistence of gold strength even amid equity rally suggests markets are not fully convinced that geopolitical risks have truly been neutralized.
