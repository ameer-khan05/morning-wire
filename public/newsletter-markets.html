Wall Street Posts Worst Day Since Iran War as Oil Surge Crushes Sentiment

Markets tumble amid geopolitical tensions and energy crisis fears

Stock markets recorded their biggest single-session sell-off since the February 28 outbreak of hostilities, with broad-based weakness across all major indices. The Nasdaq officially entered correction territory, falling more than 10% below its October record, while the Dow Jones neared correction status at 9% below all-time highs.

The S&P 500 is now heading for its fifth consecutive weekly decline—the longest losing streak since 2022. Technology stocks led the decline as investors fled risk assets amid surging oil prices and mounting inflation concerns. Treasury yields rallied on safe-haven demand.

Sources: CNBC | NYT | Bloomberg

The Week's Major Stories

Oil Surges Past $105 as Ceasefire Hopes Evaporate

Brent crude gained 2.5% to over $110 at peak, with recent volatility spanning the $99-$106 range. The closure of the Iran Strait of Hormuz is choking global oil supply, with projections indicating a plunge of 8 million barrels per day. Energy prices remain highly sensitive to any developments in the geopolitical crisis.

Sources: Fortune | IEA Oil Market Report

ECB President Warns on Inflation

European Central Bank leadership is raising alarms about mounting inflation concerns as the oil shock works through the broader economy. The surge in energy costs threatens to reignite price pressures across Europe, complicating monetary policy decisions in the months ahead.

Source: TheStreet

Gold Holds Near Record $4,429 Despite Pullback

The precious metal continues to benefit from safe-haven demand amid geopolitical uncertainty, though it pulled back $111 on March 26 due to profit-taking. Gold remains near historic highs, reflecting investor concerns about inflation and market stability.

Sources: Fortune | CBS News

Southeast Asia Faces Energy Crisis from Hormuz Closure

Nations across Southeast Asia, almost entirely dependent on foreign energy supplies, are running critically low on reserves. The closure of a key shipping strait has triggered an urgent energy crisis in the region, with severe implications for economic growth and manufacturing output.

Source: CNBC

Asian Markets Fall on March 27 After Wall Street Rout

The worst trading day since the start of the Iran conflict rippled across global markets on Thursday, with sharp losses extending into Asia on Friday morning. Regional indices declined sharply as investors grappled with the implications of surging energy prices and reduced risk appetite.

Sources: Various global markets

Trump Extends Iran Energy Strike Deadline

The administration has extended the deadline to April 6, providing a narrow 10-day window for diplomatic resolution. U.S. stock index futures rose Friday morning, recovering some of Thursday's losses on hopes that the extended timeline might allow for negotiated settlement.

Sources: NPR | CNBC

US-China Trade Tensions Escalate

Rising trade disputes between Washington and Beijing add another layer of concern for markets already reeling from geopolitical tensions and oil shocks. China's escalating response in the trade dispute compounds the bearish sentiment weighing on equity prices.

Source: Bloomberg

India Markets Crash

India's Sensex index fell 829 points while the Nifty dropped below 23,100 on March 27, reflecting the global contagion from Wall Street's sharp losses. The Indian market decline signals that emerging markets are not immune to the current risk-off environment.

Source: International News & Views
