Lead Story

Dow Plummets 606 Points as Oil Surge and Rate Fears Spark Worst Session in Over a Year

Blue-chip stocks faced their worst session in over a year as the Dow fell 605.78 points (-1.53%). A sharp tech divergence emerged: NVIDIA surged past $1,000 while the broader market sold off on fears that the Iran conflict will keep oil prices elevated and inflation sticky. The S&P 500 fell 0.74% and the Nasdaq held up relatively better at -0.39%, propped up by mega-cap tech names.

Macro & Economic Data

Key Data Point: OECD Revises US Inflation Forecast Sharply Higher

The OECD now forecasts US all-items inflation at 4.2% for 2026 — up sharply from the prior 2.8% projection and well above the Fed's 2.7% estimate. The primary driver: the Iran war's impact on global energy markets. This revision is rattling bond markets and equity valuations.

Initial Jobless Claims Hold Steady at 210K

Weekly jobless claims came in at 210,000, right in line with expectations. The labor market remains resilient despite inflationary headwinds, giving the Fed little reason to cut rates anytime soon. The disconnect between a strong jobs picture and elevated inflation is the central tension in markets right now.

All Eyes on PCE Data Next Week

Market participants are bracing for next week's Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) price index — the Fed's preferred inflation gauge. A hot reading could push rate cut expectations further out and trigger another leg down in equities. A tame reading would be a major relief.

Suspicious Oil Trades Minutes Before Trump's Iran Post

The BBC reports that millions of dollars in oil futures trades were placed just minutes before President Trump posted about Iran negotiations and a "significant" offer related to the Strait of Hormuz. The timing raises serious questions about information leaks and market manipulation at the highest levels.

Oil Jumps 5% to $94.80 on Strait of Hormuz Tensions

WTI crude surged nearly 5% to $94.80/barrel as Strait of Hormuz tensions intensified. The spike is the primary driver behind today's risk-off trade, as higher energy costs flow directly into inflation expectations and corporate margin compression — particularly for transport, industrials, and consumer staples.

What to Watch
