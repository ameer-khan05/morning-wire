Lead Story

S&P 500 Posts Longest Winning Streak Since October as Ceasefire Rally Extends
Equities extended gains into a seventh straight day, with the S&P 500 up nearly 3.7% for the week—tracking for its best week since November. The Dow turned positive for the year, flipping its 2026 performance to +0.25% YTD. The rally was driven by optimism around the US-Iran ceasefire agreement, though cracks in the truce are showing as Israel intensified bombing in Lebanon and Iran restricted Hormuz tanker passage. The breadth of the rally suggests institutional conviction: advancing stocks outnumbered decliners by a 3:1 margin on the New York Stock Exchange.
Sources: Yahoo Finance, CNBC, Bloomberg

Oil & Energy

Oil Volatile Near $97 as Strait of Hormuz Remains Partially Closed
WTI hit $101.28 on April 9 before falling back to $98.76. Brent crude settled at $96.51. The International Energy Agency called this the most significant supply shock in oil market history. The Strait of Hormuz—through which roughly 20% of global oil exports flows—remains partially closed, keeping markets in a persistent state of uncertainty.
Sources: EIA, CNBC
Saudi Arabia Production Damage
Attacks reduced production capacity by 600,000 barrels per day. Pipeline throughput was cut by 700,000 bpd. These losses represent among the largest supply disruptions in the region's modern history.
Source: EIA
Q1 2026: Historic Price Surge
Brent crude went from $61/barrel in early January to $118/barrel at its peak—the largest quarterly price increase on an inflation-adjusted basis since 1988. This shock is rippling through energy markets and broader inflation expectations.
Source: EIA
IEA Price Forecast for 2026
Brent is expected to peak at $115/barrel in Q2 2026 before easing below $90 in Q4, assuming gradual stabilization in Middle East tensions. This forecast carries substantial downside risk if geopolitical conditions deteriorate.
Source: EIA
U.S. Gasoline & Diesel at Multi-Year Highs
U.S. unleaded gasoline averages $3.99/gallon; diesel at $5.40/gallon. These are the highest prices in real terms in more than two years, creating headwinds for consumer spending and corporate logistics costs.
Source: EIA
Gulf Production Shutdowns Widening
7.5 million barrels per day of crude production were shut in across Gulf states in March. This is forecast to rise to 9.1 million bpd in April as damage assessments continue and repair work ramps up.
Source: EIA

Inflation & Fed

CPI Report Due Today at 8:30 AM EDT
Economists expect headline CPI of 3.3% annual, 0.9% monthly, with core CPI at 2.7%. The report will be closely watched given the recent spike in oil prices. A beat on inflation could further temper rate cut expectations and support the Treasury market.
Source: Dow Jones
Rate Cut Expectations Shifting
Hot inflation data could complicate the Fed's ability to cut rates later this year. Markets have been pricing in fewer cuts as oil-driven inflation concerns mount. The Fed's next meeting is May 6–7, with no decision expected until June at the earliest.
Source: Yahoo Finance
Treasury Market Holding Steady
The 10-year Treasury yield is flat at 4.29%, suggesting the market is in a wait-and-see mode ahead of today's CPI print. Volatility in long rates could spike depending on the inflation data.
Source: CNBC
