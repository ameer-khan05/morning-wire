S&P 500 Futures Rally to Start Q2 — Eyes 6,600 After Monday's Surge

U.S. equity markets are kicking off the second quarter with sharp gains as S&P futures climb 0.46% in premarket trading. The rally builds on Monday's 1,100-point Dow surge—the best day since May—driven by de-escalation hopes between the U.S. and Iran following weekend diplomatic signals. Optimism around geopolitical risk reversal is lifting indices toward the 6,600 level, signaling renewed appetite for equities after a turbulent first quarter.

Source: Yahoo Finance, CNBC, Stock Market Watch

Q1 Wrap: S&P Down 4.6% After Five Consecutive Weekly Declines

The first quarter ended on a sour note with the S&P 500 closing March down 4.6% following its worst five-week losing streak since early 2022. Energy volatility and inflation concerns dominated the period, with elevated oil prices above $100/barrel defining market sentiment throughout. The rocky quarter underscores how quickly sentiment can shift, especially when geopolitical tensions and commodity shocks intersect with lingering Fed uncertainty.

Source: Yahoo Finance

Oil Above $103/Barrel as Strait of Hormuz Shipping Stalls

West Texas Intermediate crude surged above $103 per barrel as geopolitical tensions constricted global oil flows. The Strait of Hormuz, critical to world energy supplies, saw shipping grind to a near standstill with approximately 150 vessels idled amid heightened shipping insurance costs and Iran tanker strike concerns near Qatar. The disruption highlights how quickly supply-side shocks can inflate energy costs and ripple through equity and commodity markets.

Source: CNBC, Al Jazeera

Fed's Powell Says Rates On Hold — December Rate Cut Now in Focus

Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell signaled confidence in current policy settings, stating that monetary policy is "in a good place" and there is no near-term urgency to adjust rates. However, Powell's remarks shifted market expectations for rate cuts further out to December, pushing near-term easing hopes off the table. The Fed also raised its inflation forecast to 2.7%—up from a prior 2.4% estimate—a modest upward revision that underscores lingering price pressures despite cooling headline inflation.

Source: Schwab

Tech Led Late-March Recovery — Nasdaq Surges 3.83% on Final Day of Q1

Technology stocks staged a powerful rally in the final trading session of March, lifting the Nasdaq 3.83% and providing much-needed relief for a sector that had weathered significant volatility throughout the quarter. The jump signals renewed appetite for growth and mega-cap tech names as investors rotate back into secular winners following geopolitical de-escalation signals. The strong finish sets the tone for April and suggests momentum-driven positioning may favor equities over the coming weeks.

Source: Yahoo Finance
