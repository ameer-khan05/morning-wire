Lead Story

Oil Tops $100 as Iran War Enters Fifth Week—Markets Reel

Geopolitical tensions have triggered a sharp market correction as energy prices spike on Middle East instability. Brent crude surged 2% to $107 per barrel Friday, while WTI pushed above $100, following remarks by the Trump administration signaling an intent to control Iran's oil supplies "indefinitely." The Strait of Hormuz remains a flashpoint, with up to 20 million barrels per day—the largest oil supply disruption since the 1970s—at risk from continued hostilities.

The geopolitical shock reverberated across equities. The Dow fell 1.73%, the S&P 500 dropped 1.67%, and the tech-heavy Nasdaq slid 2.15% on Friday. Energy stocks were the rare bright spot, with oil majors benefiting from higher crude prices. Investors rotated defensively into gold, which jumped 1.14% to $4,576 per ounce as flight-to-safety demand intensified. The broader market selloff signals investor anxiety over potential stagflation—a combination of geopolitical disruption, energy inflation, and slowing growth.
