Dow Enters Correction Territory as Iran Conflict Rattles Markets

The Dow Jones Industrial Average plunged 793 points on Friday, officially entering correction territory as the US-Israel war with Iran continues to destabilize global energy markets. The S&P 500 posted its fifth consecutive weekly decline, dropping 2.1% for the week. Moody's AI Recession Model Hits 49% Probability

Moody's proprietary AI recession model is flashing warning lights with a 49% probability reading for February—before the Iran conflict cut off crude supply and oil surged to nearly $120. With new geopolitical pressures mounting, the current real probability is likely significantly higher, adding to recession concerns already weighing on investor sentiment.

Oil Surges Past $107 — Strait of Hormuz Closure Disrupts Global Supply

Brent crude crude reached $107.81/barrel, up $34 year-over-year, as geopolitical tensions choke off a critical shipping route. Global oil supply is projected to plunge 8 million barrels per day, with higher prices eroding demand across the entire product spectrum. However, J.P. Morgan maintains that if the conflict resolves, Brent could average around $60/barrel for the remainder of 2026—a level that would ease inflationary pressures on the broader economy.

Source: Fortune - Price of Oil

Gold Rallies to $4,430 as Investors Seek Safety

Gold prices have surged to $4,430/oz, up $1,346 from a year ago, as investors flee to safe-haven assets amid heightened geopolitical risk and market volatility. The precious metal is hovering near record levels, reflecting the elevated risk environment and appetite for portfolio insurance during periods of uncertainty.

Source: Fortune - Current Price of Gold

Google TurboQuant Sends Memory Chip Stocks Tumbling

Google unveiled TurboQuant, a breakthrough compression technology that allows AI models to run in a fraction of the memory previously required. The announcement sent semiconductor stocks lower, particularly memory suppliers like Micron, as markets price in reduced demand for high-capacity memory chips. The innovation could reshape the competitive dynamics of the AI infrastructure build-out.

Tech Stocks Lead Weekly Losses — Nasdaq Down 2.15%

The Nasdaq composite declined 2.15% for the week as rising energy costs and geopolitical uncertainty weighed heavily on growth stocks. Technology shares, which typically benefit from low inflation and stable interest rates, are now facing headwinds from both elevated oil prices and the prospect of the Federal Reserve maintaining higher rates for longer.

Rate Cut Hopes Fade as Oil Inflation Pressures Mount

Fed rate cut expectations have been sharply reduced, with markets now pricing in only a single rate cut for the year—down significantly from earlier estimates. Higher oil prices are driving fresh inflationary pressures that could force the Federal Reserve to maintain its current restrictive stance longer than previously anticipated, keeping borrowing costs elevated for consumers and businesses.
