Wall Street Posts Best Week Since November on Ceasefire Rally
Despite a modest Friday pullback, the S&P 500 surged 3.6% for the week, Nasdaq rose 4.7%, and Dow gained 3%. The week was powered by the US-Iran ceasefire announced April 7, which sparked a massive relief rally — the Dow jumped 1,300 points that day alone, its best since April 2025. Semiconductor stocks including Nvidia and Broadcom led Nasdaq gains.
Source: CNBC

Market Stories

Oil Volatile Near $98 as Strait of Hormuz Remains Effectively Closed
Brent crude at $97.78, up $31 from a year ago. Despite ceasefire, Iran has not fully reopened the Strait of Hormuz — only 2 ships passed through on April 11, none carrying oil. Saudi Arabia reports attacks reduced production capacity by 600K bpd and East-West Pipeline throughput by 700K bpd.
Source: Fortune
Gold Stabilizes at $4,749 After Volatile Week
Gold maintained its third consecutive weekly gain of ~2%, settling at $4,749/oz. Prices have been driven by geopolitical uncertainty and dollar weakness as US-Iran ceasefire talks weaken the greenback.
Source: Sunday Guardian Live
Consumer Sentiment Plunges as Inflation Expectations Surge
Consumer sentiment slumped 10.7% in April. Year-ahead inflation expectations surged to 4.8% from 3.8% in March, the highest in years. Rising oil prices and geopolitical uncertainty are weighing heavily on consumers.
Source: Yahoo Finance
Dow Surges 1,300 Points on Ceasefire Day — Best Day Since April 2025
On April 7, the Dow jumped 1,300 points after the US-Iran ceasefire was announced, with oil crashing and risk assets surging. It was the best single-day performance since April 2025.
Source: CNBC
Oil Prices Surged Sharply in Q1 2026, EIA Reports
The US Energy Information Administration confirmed crude oil and petroleum prices increased sharply in Q1, driven by the Middle East conflict and Strait of Hormuz disruptions. Brent is up 49% YoY.
Source: US Energy Information Administration
AI Stocks Continue to Drive Market Returns
Nvidia remains Wall Street's most discussed stock. The Motley Fool predicts two AI stocks will finish 2026 higher. Semiconductor stocks led Nasdaq's 4.7% weekly gain.
Source: Yahoo Finance
Markets Eye Weekend Peace Talks for Direction
Futures inched down Friday evening as traders positioned ahead of Saturday's US-Iran talks in Islamabad. The ceasefire's durability and Hormuz reopening remain the key catalysts for next week.
Source: Yahoo Finance
Russell 2000 Posts Strong Weekly Performance
The Russell 2000 ended at 2,636.31, also posting strong weekly gains alongside the broader market rally. The small-cap index benefited from the ceasefire relief and technology sector strength throughout the week.
