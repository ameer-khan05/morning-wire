Dow Enters Correction Territory as Iran War Rattles Markets

The Dow Jones Industrial Average lost 793.47 points (1.73%) to close at 45,166.64, officially entering correction territory—a decline of 10% or more from recent highs. The S&P 500 dropped 1.67% to 6,368.85 and the Nasdaq fell 2.15% to 20,948.36 in a broad-based selloff that underscores investor anxiety.

All three major averages have fallen more than 7% month-to-date as the US-Israel war on Iran, which began February 28, continues to roil global markets. The ongoing conflict has destabilized oil markets and rattled investor confidence in the stability of supply chains and energy costs.

Brent crude topped $110 after incidents in the Strait of Hormuz exacerbated energy supply concerns. About a fifth of global oil normally transits the waterway, but that critical chokepoint is now largely blocked. Energy prices are not the only concern—the combination of military conflict, inflation fears, and trade uncertainty has created a toxic cocktail for equities.

Sources: TheStreet, CNBC

In Depth

Oil Tops $110 as Strait of Hormuz Chokes Global Supply

Brent crude reached $107.81 per barrel on March 27 after gaining $1.96, having touched $110+ intraday as energy markets reacted to escalating Middle East tensions. About 20% of the world's oil normally passes through the Strait of Hormuz, but most ships have been blocked during the Iran war, creating supply bottlenecks that reverberate through the global economy.

The International Energy Agency member countries agreed on March 11 to release an unprecedented 400 million barrels from emergency reserves in an effort to stabilize prices, but oil remains roughly $34 higher than a year ago. This represents a significant structural shift that threatens inflation and economic growth.

Source: Fortune

Fed's Next Move May Be a Rate Hike

Markets are now pricing in the Federal Reserve's next move as a potential rate hike rather than a cut—a dramatic reversal from earlier 2026 when rate cuts were widely anticipated. The shift in expectations reflects growing concern that surging oil prices will fuel inflation despite slowing economic growth.

The combination of war-driven energy costs and trade tariffs is creating a stagflationary environment: slow growth paired with rising prices. This scenario is particularly challenging for the Fed, which cannot easily accommodate both conditions with a single policy response. Some analysts worry this could be the beginning of a prolonged period of economic discomfort.

Source: CNBC

IEA Releases Unprecedented 400M Barrels from Strategic Reserves

In response to the Iran conflict's impact on global oil supplies, IEA member countries agreed on March 11 to make available 400 million barrels of oil from emergency reserves—the largest coordinated release in the organization's history. The strategic move aims to mitigate the economic impact of Middle East supply disruptions and prevent energy costs from spiraling further out of control.

Despite this unprecedented intervention, oil prices have continued climbing, suggesting that market participants believe the supply disruption could persist longer than the IEA's reserves can offset. This underscores the severity of the current energy crisis.

Source: International Energy Agency

Unity Software Surges 13.5% on Strong Q1 Guidance

In a rare bright spot on Friday, Unity Software bucked the broader market decline, closing at $19.45, up 13.54% for the day. The gaming and AI software company announced that preliminary Q1 2026 revenue would exceed prior guidance, with particular excitement around its AI-related Vector guidance for future products.

The strong performance highlights how selective sectors—particularly those tied to artificial intelligence—continue to draw investor money even as traditional equities struggle. However, the broader market's weakness suggests this enthusiasm is limited to a narrow cohort of mega-cap and cutting-edge tech names.

Source: The Motley Fool

Gold Surges to $4,524 as Investors Flee to Safety

Gold climbed 2.62% to $4,524.30 on Friday, continuing its role as the ultimate safe haven amid war fears, inflation concerns, and equity selloffs. The precious metal has been one of the best-performing assets of 2026 as geopolitical risk escalates and real yields become more attractive.

The surge in gold prices reflects a fundamental shift in investor psychology from risk-on to risk-off. This move toward traditional hedges signals deep uncertainty about both the trajectory of the conflict and the economic impacts it will generate in the months ahead.

Source: Sunday Guardian Live

Markets Yo-Yo on War Headlines—Volatility Here to Stay

Wall Street has been whipsawing on war headlines as investors struggle to price in the conflict's trajectory and economic impact. On March 25, stocks rose and oil prices eased after Trump suggested possible negotiations with Iran, only to reverse sharply on March 27 as the conflict widened and the blockade of the Strait of Hormuz intensified.

This pattern of rallies and selloffs is likely to persist as long as the war remains in an acute phase. Market participants will continue to react to headlines about negotiations, military operations, and energy supply disruptions. Strategic investors should prepare for continued volatility.
