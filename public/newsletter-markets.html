Lead Story

S&P 500 Hits Record as Nasdaq Posts Longest Winning Streak Since 2009

Wall Street reached new heights Thursday as the S&P 500 closed at a record 7,041.28, gaining 0.26%, while the Nasdaq surged to 24,102.70 for its 12th consecutive positive session — the longest daily winning streak since July 2009. The Dow Jones added 115 points to close at 48,578.72.

The rally was fueled by President Trump's announcement of a 10-day Israel-Lebanon ceasefire, which eased geopolitical risk premiums that have weighed on markets for weeks. This week alone, the S&P 500 and Nasdaq have risen 3.3% and 5.2% respectively, while the Dow has advanced more than 1%.

Leading the Dow's gains were Verizon (+3.93%), Cisco Systems (+2.56%), and IBM (+2.41%), while Boeing (-2.09%), Merck (-2.08%), and Johnson & Johnson (-1.77%) lagged.

Source: TheStreet | CNBC
Energy & Commodities

Oil Surges Past $94 as Iran Doubts Resurface and Inventories Plunge

WTI crude oil rose as much as 4% to above $95 per barrel as doubts resurfaced over ongoing US-Iran negotiations, with peace talks now focused on securing a temporary memorandum to avoid renewed conflict. Adding fuel to the fire: US crude inventories fell by 9.13 million barrels last week, far exceeding analysts' expectations for a modest 154,000-barrel build. Brent crude reached $97.06, about $31 above its price a year ago.

Source: Fortune | Trading Economics

Strait of Hormuz Remains the Market's Biggest Wild Card

Physical crude oil prices have surged to record levels near $150/bbl in spot markets — far above futures prices — as the Strait of Hormuz supply disruption continues to ripple through global energy markets. The IEA forecasts a drop in consumption this year as the conflict pushes up fuel costs, while some analysts warn oil could spike to $200 if infrastructure damage worsens.

Source: deVere Group | IEA

Gold Holds Above $4,800 as Safe-Haven Demand Persists

Gold edged higher to $4,821 per ounce, up 0.63% on the day and $1,489 higher than one year ago. The precious metal continues to benefit from safe-haven demand amid Middle East tensions, persistent inflation fears, and central bank buying. The ceasefire announcement provided a slight headwind, but analysts note structural demand remains robust.

Source: Fortune
Earnings & Corporate

Abbott Labs Falls 5% on Flu-Season Revenue Miss

Abbott Laboratories shares dropped 5% after reporting results that fell short of expectations due to a weaker-than-anticipated flu season. The diagnostics and medical device maker saw lower demand for respiratory testing kits, dragging overall revenue below analyst estimates despite strength in other business segments.

Source: Motley Fool

Charles Schwab Plunges 10% Despite Record Revenue

Schwab shares fell sharply despite posting record revenue numbers, as investors focused on margin compression and slower-than-expected client asset growth. The disconnect between top-line strength and stock performance highlights the market's increasingly exacting standards during earnings season.

Source: Motley Fool
Economic Data

Unemployment Claims Drop to 207,000 — Labor Market Stays Resilient

Weekly jobless claims fell by 11,000 to 207,000, indicating continued strength in the labor market despite elevated oil prices and geopolitical uncertainty. The reading came in below the consensus estimate, reinforcing the narrative that the U.S. economy remains remarkably resilient even as energy costs weigh on consumer spending.

Source: CNBC

IMF Releases World Economic Outlook: "Global Economy in the Shadow of War"

The International Monetary Fund published its April 2026 World Economic Outlook, titled "Global Economy in the Shadow of War," warning that the Iran conflict and energy price spikes are the dominant risks to global growth. The report downgraded growth forecasts for oil-importing nations while noting windfall gains for energy exporters.

Source: IMF
