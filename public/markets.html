The Anchor

Futures edge up as Vance boards a plane. Apple's CEO shock weighs on tech.

Tuesday morning's pre-market tone turned cautiously optimistic the moment reports confirmed Vice President Vance had departed for Islamabad. S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 futures both added 0.5%, and WTI crude gave back $1.49 from Monday's 6.5% surge, settling near $87.80. The dollar index eased 0.30% to 99.98 — the first full-point retreat since the ceasefire collapsed Friday. VIX pulled back 9.25% from its Monday spike, suggesting traders are pricing at least a non-zero probability of a peace framework before Wednesday's deadline.

The other headline dragging on sentiment was Apple's Monday evening announcement that CEO Tim Cook will step down September 1. AAPL slipped less than 1% in after-hours, a relatively muted reaction — but the story introduces management uncertainty into the most-owned stock on earth ahead of a pivotal earnings week. March retail sales data is due this morning, with economists watching for signs that consumer spending held through the geopolitical volatility. And the first wave of Q1 earnings — 3M, GE Aerospace, RTX, and UnitedHealth — will set the industrial and healthcare tone before the mega-cap tech season opens Wednesday.

Futures · Peace talks +0.50% S&P 500 · Nasdaq · pre-market

Futures add 0.5% as Vance heads to Pakistan — oil retreats from Monday's high.

S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 futures each gained 0.5% in early Tuesday trading as reports confirmed Vice President Vance had boarded a flight to Islamabad for a second round of US-Iran peace talks. WTI crude slipped 1.67% to $87.80 from Monday's $89.29 peak. Gold eased 0.37% below $4,800. The dollar index fell 0.30%, and Treasury 10-year yields dipped 2 basis points to 4.34%.

Apple · CEO shock AAPL Cook steps down · Ternus inherits

AAPL slips as Cook's departure adds uncertainty to the world's most-owned stock.

Apple stock fell less than 1% in after-hours and continued lower in pre-market trading following Monday's announcement that Tim Cook will step down September 1. The muted reaction reflects both confidence in incoming CEO John Ternus and anxiety about Apple's lagging AI strategy. The transition comes just weeks before Apple is expected to detail its next generation of Apple Intelligence features — now Ternus's first major product moment as CEO-designate.

Tesla Q1 earnings Wednesday: Street expects $0.37 EPS, delivery miss already known.

The most consequential number won't be EPS — it will be any disclosure on Terafab, the planned one-terawatt AI compute facility excluded from Tesla's $20B+ capex guidance. WTI fell 1.67% from Monday's $89.29 peak — giving back a fraction of the weekend's 6.5% surge — as peace-talk optimism modestly eased the Hormuz risk premium. Brent retreated to $94.52.
