The peace dividend evaporates.

Friday's relief rally unwound almost entirely on Monday. WTI crude jumped 6.5% as Hormuz shut again. The Nasdaq's 13-day winning streak — the longest since 1992 — ended. The small-cap Russell 2000 bucked the tape, scoring a new all-time closing high. Earnings season begins now.

At a glance · Apr 20 13 days Nasdaq win streak · ended today
The Anchor

One ship, one seizure, and the peace premium is gone.

Markets opened Monday staring at two pieces of weekend news neither wanted: the US Navy seized an Iranian cargo vessel, and Iran responded by shutting the Strait of Hormuz again. WTI futures responded immediately, adding 6.5% to $89.29 — reversing most of Friday's ceasefire-driven collapse in a single session. Brent punched back above $96. The dollar strengthened as safe-haven bids returned. Gold, the other traditional refuge, fell 0.51% as oil inflation fears competed with the flight-to-quality impulse.

The Nasdaq's 13-day winning streak — the longest in 34 years — ended with a modest 0.26% decline. The S&P 500 slipped 0.24%, the Dow was essentially flat. The outlier was small caps: the Russell 2000 gained 0.58% to 2,792.96, a new all-time closing record. Domestically oriented small-cap businesses are less exposed to oil prices and geopolitical supply chains — and Monday's divergence may be the first hint that investors are beginning to rotate out of large-cap tech and into something less correlated to Hormuz.

Markets · 20 April
Oil · Hormuz +6.49% WTI · Hormuz closure

WTI surges 6.5% as Iran shuts Hormuz again.

West Texas Intermediate futures climbed from $83.92 to $89.29 in Monday's session after Tehran restricted ship passage in response to the USS Spruance's seizure of the Touska. Brent rose from $90.38 to $96.12. The move erases most of Friday's ceasefire-driven oil collapse and pushes April's average crude print well above the Fed's comfort zone.

Source: TheStreet
Streak · Nasdaq 13 days Longest since 1992 · ended

Nasdaq's 13-day winning streak snaps — the longest since 1992.

The composite declined 0.26% to 24,404.39 Monday after the geopolitical jolt. The streak, which began April 2, had been powered by AI earnings optimism, the ceasefire rally, and a dovish Fed signal. The question now is whether Monday's pullback is a healthy pause or the start of something larger ahead of a high-stakes earnings week.

Source: CNBC
Small Caps ATH Russell 2000 · 2,792.96

Russell 2000 scores a new all-time closing high at 2,792.96.

While large-cap tech slipped, small-cap domestic stocks rallied 0.58% to a record close — the first time the Russell has outperformed on a risk-off day in months. Domestically oriented businesses face less direct Hormuz exposure, and some rotation out of stretched large-cap valuations may be accelerating.

Source: Motley Fool
Earnings · Tesla Apr 22 Q1 2026 · Terafab watch

Tesla reports Q1 on Tuesday with delivery miss and Terafab in focus.

Tesla delivered 358,023 vehicles in Q1 — down 14% sequentially, 3.4% below consensus. Street expects $0.37 EPS on $22.71B revenue. The more consequential disclosure is any update on Terafab, the one-terawatt AI compute facility excluded from Tesla's $20B+ official capex guidance — a number that, if confirmed, dwarfs any prior corporate capex commitment.

Source: IG
Earnings · Mag-7 4 reports Alphabet · Meta · MSFT · AMZN

Alphabet, Meta, Microsoft, and Amazon report Wednesday through Friday.

Every transcript will be searched for the same signal: whether AI infrastructure spending is producing revenue fast enough to justify the decade's largest corporate capex cycle. Goldman Sachs estimates the combined AI capex of the Magnificent Seven will exceed $350 billion in 2026 — a figure the market has priced in optimistically and will now demand to see validated.

Source: CNBC
Fed · Inflation Worsened April forecast · oil shock

Fed's April inflation forecast worsens as oil spike undoes March progress.

The Motley Fool reports that the Federal Reserve's April inflation forecast has been revised higher following Monday's oil surge, pushing the probability of a June rate cut materially lower. March CPI came in at 3.3%; if oil holds above $89, the April print could reverse the modest progress made when the Strait briefly reopened Friday.
