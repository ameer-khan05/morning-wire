The desalination plant strike drives Brent higher for a reason beyond the Hormuz transit premium that characterized prior sessions. Hormuz-specific risk is priced into Brent but not WTI; both benchmarks surging together reflects a broader reassessment. Striking civilian-adjacent infrastructure broadens the retaliatory spectrum: Iran could characterize Gulf-state desalination facilities as legitimate targets in response, with consequences extending far beyond oil transit. That forward risk is what Friday's move begins to price. The "full-scale offensive" warning reinforces the Monday open as a binary: if Iranian attacks scale materially over the weekend, Brent could open significantly above $88.
