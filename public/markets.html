The Dow Jones Industrial Average surged 923 points Thursday to close at 50,841 вЂ” a 1.86% gain that marks the single largest point and percentage advance in the 104-day US-Iran conflict era вЂ” while the S&P 500 gained 1.75% and the Nasdaq Composite advanced 2.54%, as President Trump's claim that the war had ended and his cancellation of further planned strikes shifted market sentiment from partial recovery to full resolution-trade positioning. The two-day swing вЂ” Wednesday's conflict-peak selloff followed by Thursday's deal-announcement rally вЂ” illustrates the market's operating structure during the 104-day conflict: Iran news drives violent intraday and session-to-session moves in both directions, with each escalation or de-escalation producing multi-percentage-point moves in hours. Thursday's 923-point Dow gain is larger than Wednesday's ~900-point decline, reflecting that the market is pricing asymmetrically вЂ” the upside of a deal resolution (new records, rate-cut repricing, risk-premium removal) is larger than the downside of continued conflict (which is already priced to a significant degree).
