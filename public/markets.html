Markets · Deep Dive · Equities Reopen · Tech Rally · SK Hynix $29B · SpaceX Nasdaq-100

US equity markets reopened Monday with a tech-led rally — Nasdaq futures up 1%, S&P 500 up 0.4%, Dow flat — as the AI trade returned after its late-June slump, anchored by SK Hynix's $29 billion Nasdaq listing and SpaceX's forced Nasdaq-100 inclusion, while gold rose to $4,155, oil climbed to $69, and Iran's Day 3 funeral kept geopolitical risk priced into commodities.

The Monday open resolved the NFP split verdict in the Nasdaq's favor. When the full institutional participant set returned from the Independence Day holiday and found Bitcoin 4.44% higher than Thursday's close, the AI trade being confirmed by Nvidia supplier Hon Hai's strong quarterly results, and a $29 billion AI memory chip listing opening on the exchange, the macro-bullish read won the session. The Dow's all-time high at 52,900 was not just defended — it was modestly extended, with the index adding 0.02% to 52,891 in intraday trading. The Nasdaq's 1% advance is the session's signal: the late-June slump in chip stocks that drove the Nasdaq to its 0.80% Thursday decline was a positioning-driven dip in a structurally intact AI trade, not the start of a demand-driven reversal. The SK Hynix listing — $29 billion of institutional capital committed to AI memory infrastructure on the same morning — is the capital-market confirmation of that reading.

US equity markets reopened Monday with a tech-led advance — the Nasdaq gaining 1% to 26,091, the S&P 500 up 0.4% to 7,513, and the Dow adding modestly to 52,891 — as the AI trade returned after its late-June slump in semiconductor and AI stocks, catalyzed by Nvidia supplier Hon Hai's stronger-than-expected quarterly sales and the concurrent capital-market signal from SK Hynix's $29 billion Nasdaq listing. The Thursday NFP split verdict — Dow bullish on rate-hold, Nasdaq bearish on demand weakness — resolved on Monday in the Nasdaq's favor: the demand-weakness reading of the 57,000 jobs miss did not have the institutional backing that the rate-hold reading did, and the AI trade's Monday reentry was the market's mechanism for expressing that verdict.

The Hon Hai result is the specific catalyst that made the Nasdaq's 1% Monday advance possible. Nvidia's supply chain is the leading indicator of AI infrastructure spending — if demand for Nvidia's chips is falling, the entire AI trade is at risk; if demand is holding, the late-June semiconductor selloff was a positioning event rather than a demand signal. Hon Hai's stronger quarterly result says demand is holding. That single data point — Nvidia's biggest supplier reporting better-than-expected numbers — was enough to flip the market's read of the late-June chip slump from "demand is softening" to "positioning was crowded and has been cleared." With that flip confirmed, the Nasdaq +1% is the natural consequence of institutions who were underweight tech after the slump re-establishing positions on the Monday open.

South Korea's SK Hynix launched a $29 billion Nasdaq listing Monday — selling 17.79 million new depositary receipt shares in what ranks as the largest US share sale ever by a foreign company — riding demand for AI memory chips, specifically High-Bandwidth Memory used in Nvidia's AI accelerators and throughout the AI training infrastructure stack. SpaceX will officially join the Nasdaq-100 on Tuesday, triggering mandatory purchases by all Nasdaq-100 tracking funds, most notably the $496 billion QQQ, whose managers must buy SPCX shares at any price before Tuesday's open regardless of valuation preference. SpaceX shares were trading near $163.75, up 1.08% ahead of the inclusion, following the company's June 12 IPO at $161 that closed +19% on its debut.

The SK Hynix listing is the most informative signal about institutional AI conviction that Monday produced. In May, when SK Hynix signaled it might slow AI memory production, global markets reacted with a Kospi index plunge measuring how deeply AI memory demand had been embedded in institutional equity valuations. That the same company is now raising $29 billion in a full US public listing — the largest foreign IPO in US history — one month after that sentiment reversal is not a routine capital-markets event. It is institutional investors, with full knowledge of the May selloff and its cause, committing $29 billion to the same thesis they had to revise in May. The revision's conclusion: AI memory demand is intact, the May signal was management hedging rather than a demand read, and $29 billion is the appropriate commitment to that conclusion.

Gold rose to $4,155 (▲0.97%) and WTI crude climbed to $69 (▲2.83%) as Iran's Day 3 funeral — the largest of the five-day ceremony, with crowds chanting "death to America" and "death to Israel" through a 10-kilometer Tehran procession — kept geopolitical risk priced into commodities. The 10-year Treasury edged up 3 basis points to 4.28% in a mild risk-on tick consistent with the equity market's tech-led advance. OPEC+ approved a modest collective quota increase at its July meeting, reflecting member confidence in stabilizing Middle East conditions — but with Katz's threat against Mojtaba Khamenei still active and the burial set for July 9, oil's geopolitical premium remains live through the end of the funeral period.

The gold and oil moves tell two compatible stories. Gold at $4,155 reflects the combined support of the rate-hold narrative (falling real yields support gold) and geopolitical uncertainty (Iran's funeral period, Lebanon ceasefire implementation gap, Katz threat). WTI at $69 reflects the OPEC+ quota increase moderating supply tightness while the Iran premium remains in the price — $67.10 was Thursday's level when the funeral had just begun and the crowds were smaller; $69 is Monday's level after Day 3's largest-yet procession with explicit anti-Western crowd chants. The $1.90/barrel increase reflects the premium for the possibility that the Katz threat materializes during the funeral window. The July 9 burial and Doha resumption are the next oil price events: if both occur without escalation, the funeral premium unwinds and oil retraces toward the pre-funeral range.
