Records in every direction.

The Dow jumped 1,000 points. The S&P 500 closed above 7,100 for the first time. The Nasdaq is on a 12-day streak — the longest since 2009. And crude just printed its biggest one-day drop since the invasion.

At a glance · Apr 17 7,141 S&P 500 · first close > 7,100
The Anchor

Two narratives, one tape: peace dividend meets sticky inflation.

Thursday's close was historic by any measure. Dow +1,000. S&P 500's first settlement above 7,100. Nasdaq a fresh record. Russell 2000 finally joining the party after a month of large-cap leadership. Under the hood, the mechanism was simple: oil broke, so did tail-risk pricing — VIX collapsed 8%, ten-year yields eased three basis points, and equities did the rest.

The problem is March CPI. Headline printed 3.3%, up from 2.4% in February, as crude's brief dash above $118 fed through. The Fed minutes released last week show staff openly worried about a "protracted" Hormuz episode keeping inflation elevated well into Q4. If the truce sticks — and that's the biggest if of the year — that path clears. If it doesn't, the Nasdaq's streak ends fast.

Commodities −9.8% WTI · single-session drop

Crude collapses as Hormuz reopens.

WTI for May delivery fell 9.8% to $85.37 per barrel. Brent June dropped 9.1% to $90.38. The move wipes out the vast majority of the war-premium that had built since early March and resets the Fed's 2026 inflation trajectory.

Russell 2000 leads for the first time in a month.

Small caps outperforming on a peace-dividend tape is the textbook signal that participation is finally broadening. Financials, regionals, and domestic industrials are where the flows showed up first.

Streak 12 days Nasdaq · longest since 2009

Nasdaq extends to 12 consecutive gains.

Inflation 3.3% March CPI · YoY

March CPI re-accelerates on oil — but the door has opened.

Headline inflation jumped from 2.4% in February as crude briefly hit $118 a barrel. With WTI back below $86 today, the April print should reverse meaningfully — restoring the Fed's room to cut in June or July.
