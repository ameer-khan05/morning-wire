Friday close: Brent $88.10 (▲4.59%), WTI $82.47 (▲4.46%). The UST 10-year yield held at 4.545% (▲8 bp Friday), with oil-inflation expectations continuing to override safe-haven Treasury demand. The September Fed rate path remains the unknown variable.

Bandar Abbas entering the US target set changes the oil market's risk calculus. Prior targets — Ahvaz, Khuzestan, oil infrastructure, water stations — were inland; Bandar Abbas is where the Hormuz strait meets Iran's primary port, naval base, and commercial shipping hub. US operations in the vicinity of Bandar Abbas create a different maritime risk environment than operations 500 kilometers inland. War-risk insurance premiums, already at conflict highs following last Saturday's tanker kills, may reprice again as underwriters assess what operations near Bandar Abbas mean for Hormuz transit exposure. If the oil market reads Bandar Abbas as a step toward interdicting Iran's maritime resupply, the $90 touch may prove a floor rather than a spike.
