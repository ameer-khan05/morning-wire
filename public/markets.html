The Anchor

Futures rise as Trump's open-ended ceasefire replaces the Wednesday deadline.

The market's biggest near-term risk — a ceasefire expiry with no deal — was formally removed Tuesday evening when Trump announced an indefinite extension, conditioning the truce on Iran submitting a "unified proposal." S&P 500 and Nasdaq futures each added 0.5–0.7% in Wednesday pre-market as investors processed the development. The VIX declined 7.38% on Tuesday, building on Monday's 9.25% pullback from the conflict-driven spike. Bond yields edged up 2 basis points to 4.36% — a mild safe-haven sell-off. Gold held above $4,800 but dipped slightly from Monday's peak. The dollar index retreated 0.53% to 99.45, its biggest single-day drop in two weeks.

The extension does not remove the blockade. WTI crude held near $89–90 as traders acknowledged that the Hormuz risk premium has not been resolved — merely deferred. The key event today is Tesla Q1 earnings at 5:30 PM ET; anything Musk says about Terafab, the planned one-terawatt AI compute facility, will be dissected word by word. Then ECB rate decision Thursday — still expected to cut 25 basis points despite the oil-driven inflation noise. And the Big Tech calendar officially extended by a week: Alphabet and Meta report April 29 alongside Microsoft; Amazon follows April 30.

Futures add 0.5–0.7% as Trump's indefinite ceasefire extension removes the Wednesday deadline risk.

Source: Schwab
Oil · Blockade premium $89.44 WTI · blockade holds · Hormuz watch

WTI holds near $89 — blockade keeps Hormuz premium alive despite ceasefire extension.

WTI crude rose 1.87% to $89.44 and Brent climbed to $95.95 as traders noted the ceasefire extension did nothing to resolve the underlying Hormuz risk. The US naval blockade of Iranian ports remains in force. Iran has allowed neutral-flag commercial tankers through the Strait while blocking US-flagged vessels. Oil analysts estimate that WTI could retrace to $83–85 if a full ceasefire extension is agreed with blockade removal — a scenario that is not currently on the table.

Source: Trading Economics · CNBC
Tesla · Earnings 5:30 PM Q1 2026 · $0.37 EPS est. · Terafab

Tesla Q1 earnings at 5:30 PM ET: $0.37 EPS and $22.71B revenue expected — Terafab is the real watch.

Street consensus is $0.37 EPS on $22.71B revenue; Q1 deliveries of 358,023 — down 14% sequentially — are already known. The number that will actually move the stock is any disclosure on Terafab, the planned one-terawatt AI compute facility absent from Tesla's official $20B+ capex guidance. If Musk provides a credible Terafab update tonight, analysts say it would force a complete re-evaluation of Tesla's classification between automaker and AI infrastructure company.

ECB · Thursday −25 bps Rate decision · oil inflation signal

ECB rate decision Thursday — 25 bps cut expected despite oil's lingering inflation pressure.

The European Central Bank is expected to cut rates 25 basis points on Thursday despite oil's conflict-driven surge keeping headline inflation elevated. Markets are pricing a 93% probability of a cut. ECB President Lagarde faces a difficult communication challenge: the blockade-driven oil premium is supply-side inflation — not demand-driven — making it largely outside monetary policy's reach. The question is whether Lagarde's press conference signals any pause consideration for the June meeting.

Source: CNBC

Bonds · Gold 4.36% UST 10Y · safe-haven demand fades

Treasury yields edge up as safe-haven bid softens; gold holds above $4,800 on residual uncertainty.

The 10-year Treasury yield rose 2 basis points to 4.36% on Tuesday as the ceasefire extension reduced the flight-to-quality bid that had pushed yields lower during the peak conflict anxiety. Gold held at $4,812, up 0.44%, but well below Monday's intraday spike — residual uncertainty over the blockade resolution keeps safe-haven demand from fully unwinding. The VIX at 13.80 reflects a market that is calmer than last week but not fully relaxed.
