Lead Story

Depression May Start With an Energy Problem in Brain Cells

Researchers have discovered that depression may be linked to unusual changes in how brain and blood cells generate energy — a breakthrough that could help scientists detect the condition earlier and develop more targeted treatments. The finding shifts the understanding of depression from purely chemical imbalance theories to a more nuanced view involving cellular energy metabolism. This could open pathways for entirely new classes of antidepressants targeting mitochondrial function.

Source: ScienceDaily

Probiotic Bacteria Engineered as Tumor-Seeking Drug Factories

Scientists have engineered probiotic bacteria to act as tumor-seeking drug factories that infiltrated tumors and produced a cancer-fighting drug right where it was needed in mice. This innovative approach to drug delivery could revolutionize cancer treatment by delivering targeted therapy directly to tumors while minimizing side effects on healthy tissue. The approach represents a convergence of synthetic biology and oncology.

Source: ScienceDaily

Deepfake X-Rays Now Fool Both Doctors and AI

Deepfake X-rays created by AI are now convincing enough to fool both human radiologists and AI diagnostic models. Radiologists had limited success identifying fake images, raising serious concerns about the integrity of medical imaging and potential for insurance fraud or misdiagnosis. The finding highlights the urgent need for verification tools in medical AI.

Source: ScienceDaily

Long COVID Clue Found Deep in the Immune System

Long COVID remains a frustrating medical mystery, affecting up to 1 in 10 people long after the initial infection fades. Scientists have now uncovered a crucial clue hidden deep within the immune system that may explain persistent symptoms. The discovery could lead to both diagnostic tests and potential treatments for the millions still suffering from the condition worldwide.

Source: ScienceDaily
4

Vitamin B3 May Shut Down Key Driver of Fatty Liver Disease

A key genetic driver of fatty liver disease has been identified as microRNA-93, and researchers found that vitamin B3 can effectively shut it down. The discovery offers a potentially simple and accessible treatment pathway for a condition that affects millions worldwide and is closely linked to obesity and metabolic syndrome.

Source: ScienceDaily
5

Cold Weather Linked to Far More Deaths Than Heat

New research shows cold weather is linked to far more cardiovascular deaths than heat, accounting for tens of thousands of extra deaths each year in the United States. The finding challenges the predominant focus on heat-related deaths in climate change discussions and highlights the ongoing threat of cold exposure, particularly for elderly and vulnerable populations.

Source: ScienceDaily
6

Immersive Dreaming Makes Sleep Feel Deeper and More Refreshing

Researchers found that immersive dreaming can actually make sleep feel deeper and more refreshing, even when brain activity is high. The counterintuitive finding suggests that vivid dreams may play a more important restorative role than previously understood, potentially opening new approaches to treating sleep disorders.

Source: ScienceDaily
7

New Prototype Supercharges Mass Spectrometry

A new prototype could supercharge mass spectrometry by analyzing thousands of molecules at once instead of one by one, boosting sensitivity by 225% — enough to reveal rare, hidden compounds. The breakthrough could accelerate drug discovery, disease diagnostics, and environmental monitoring.

Source: ScienceDaily
