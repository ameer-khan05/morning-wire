Artemis II Launches Today — NASA Returns Humans to the Moon for First Time Since 1972

NASA's crewed Artemis II mission launches today at 6:24 PM ET from Kennedy Space Center, marking humanity's return to lunar exploration after more than five decades. The four-person crew—Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover (first person of color beyond low Earth orbit), Christina Koch (first woman beyond LEO), and Jeremy Hansen (first non-U.S. citizen to the Moon)—will embark on a 10-day free-return trajectory with 80% favorable launch weather conditions.

Sources: Space.com, NASA, CBS News
MEDICINE

Single Blood Test Detects 50 Types of Cancer Before Symptoms Appear

UK clinical trials have validated a breakthrough blood test capable of detecting DNA fragments from 50 different cancer types in asymptomatic patients. The test identifies not only the presence of cancer but also the tissue of origin, potentially enabling early intervention and treatment at more treatable stages before clinical symptoms develop.

Source: CAS
POLICY

Nebraska First State to Require Work for Medicaid Under New Federal Initiative

Nebraska has become the first state to implement work requirements for Medicaid recipients under Trump's One Big Beautiful Bill. The policy affects millions of beneficiaries nationwide, while Title X funding—which supports reproductive health services for 2.8 million people—faces uncertain renewal prospects amid ongoing policy debates.

Source: KFF Health News
REGULATION

UK Clinical Trial Regulations Undergo Largest Overhaul in 20 Years

New UK clinical trial regulations take effect this month, representing the most significant regulatory change in two decades. The reformed system consolidates ethical review and regulatory approval into a single unified application process, streamlining the path to clinical research and reducing bureaucratic delays.

Source: Live Science
MEDICINE

FDA May Approve First Regulatory T Cell Therapy This Spring

The FDA is expected to approve the first regulatory T cell therapy this spring, offering a critical new treatment option for preventing graft-versus-host disease in bone marrow transplant patients. This innovation could significantly improve outcomes and quality of life for transplant recipients undergoing immune system reconstitution.

Source: Mass General Brigham
MEDICINE

Suzetrigine Brings Renewed Hope for Opioid-Free Pain Management

The FDA approval of suzetrigine (Journavax) marks an important advancement in opioid-free pain relief, offering patients and clinicians a new therapeutic option that addresses chronic pain without the addiction and side-effect risks associated with traditional opioid medications. The development signals ongoing progress in non-addictive pain management strategies.

Source: Scientific American
GLOBAL

World Health Day 2026: "No Health Without Peace" Emphasizes Science-Led Approach

The 2026 World Health Day theme—"No Health Without Peace"—underscores the critical intersection of global health and international stability in an era of ongoing conflicts. The science-led agenda emphasizes how geopolitical instability undermines public health infrastructure, disease prevention, and equitable access to medical care worldwide.

Source: Countercurrents
