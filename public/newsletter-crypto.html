Lead Story

SEC & CFTC Classify Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana as Digital Commodities

In a landmark development on March 17, 2026, regulators issued a comprehensive 68-page document officially classifying Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, XRP, and 12 other digital assets as commodities rather than securities. The ruling transfers regulatory authority to the CFTC, providing long-awaited clarity for the industry. Protocol mining has been classified as administrative activity, resolving a key ambiguity that has plagued institutional adoption. This historic reclassification removes the regulatory overhang that has constrained institutional investment and opens the door for expanded crypto derivatives markets and custody solutions.

Regulation

Stablecoin Yield Dispute Resolved — CLARITY Act Advances

Senators Rob Tillis (R-NC) and Chris Van Hollen (D-MD) have reached agreement on stablecoin yield prohibition language, removing the final regulatory hurdle for the CLARITY Act. The compromise allows stablecoin issuers to provide yield on deposits while maintaining consumer protections and reserve requirements. Industry observers view this breakthrough as a watershed moment for U.S. crypto regulation, potentially unlocking passage in the current legislative session with strong bipartisan support.

GENIUS Act Implementation on Track for July 2026 Launch

The Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) has finalized proposed procedures for implementing the GENIUS Act, which permits national banks to issue stablecoins backed by full reserve requirements. The regulatory framework is expected to be adopted in time for stablecoin issuance programs to launch by Q3 2026. Multiple large regional and community banks have expressed interest in participating, signaling potential diversification of stablecoin issuers beyond cryptocurrency-native platforms.
