Lead Story

Bitcoin Stuck Below $73K as Geopolitical Ceasefire Caps Every Rally

Bitcoin's latest attempt to push past $73,000 has stalled as market participants pivot focus back to the uncertain US-Iran truce negotiations. The $73,000 level has served as a persistent ceiling during the six-week conflict, frustrating bull-case advocates who see further upside. Technical analysts emphasize that a genuine bullish phase requires breaking through $75,000, but current momentum suggests such a breakout remains distant.

The rebound that did occur was largely driven by algorithmic leverage unwinding rather than organic buying pressure. Bearish funding rates across major exchanges underscore weak conviction among derivatives traders. Until macro conditions stabilize—either from geopolitical clarity or positive economic data—Bitcoin may remain range-bound in the low-to-mid $70,000s, vulnerable to sharp liquidations on either side.

Source: CoinSpectator, CoinDesk

Regulation & Policy

SEC "Reg Crypto" Proposal Sent to White House

The Securities and Exchange Commission has forwarded a comprehensive regulatory framework to the White House that includes a startup exemption and safe harbor system for digital asset innovation. SEC Chair Paul Atkins confirmed the submission, signaling the administration's intent to crystallize a workable regulatory perimeter.

Source: PYMNTS, SEC

SEC-CFTC MOU on Coordinated Crypto Oversight

A memorandum of understanding signed March 11 between the Securities and Exchange Commission and Commodity Futures Trading Commission establishes the framework for coordinated cryptocurrency oversight with a "minimum effective dose" regulation approach. A joint interpretive release issued March 17 clarifies enforcement boundaries.

Source: Latham & Watkins, SEC

CLARITY Act Markup Hearing April 13

The most ambitious crypto market structure bill to date heads to markup hearing April 13, with potential for full Senate consideration by end of April. The CLARITY Act represents the most comprehensive legislative push to codify digital asset regulations into existing market infrastructure law.

Source: DL News

GENIUS Act Stablecoin Framework Takes Shape

FDIC-backed stablecoin rules under the GENIUS Act framework link issuance directly to reserve integrity, with full implementation required by July 18, 2026. This approach prioritizes depositor protections through mandatory backing requirements.

Source: PYMNTS

California Digital Financial Assets Law Effective July 1

California's first-in-nation Digital Financial Assets Law takes effect July 1, requiring all digital asset custodians, exchanges, and service providers to obtain state licensing. This sets a template that other states are likely to follow.

Source: CoinPedia

SEC Roundtable for CLARITY Act April 16

The Securities and Exchange Commission has scheduled a roundtable discussion for April 16 to gather stakeholder input on the CLARITY Act before full committee markup proceedings.

Source: SEC

Corporate & Mining

Mining Firms Unload Bitcoin as Production Costs Hit $80K Per Coin

Bitcoin mining companies are beginning to liquidate holdings as production costs approach $80,000 per coin, squeezing margins in an extended bear scenario. Major operator Bitfarms has announced a strategic rebrand to "Keel Infrastructure," pivoting away from Bitcoin mining entirely toward AI data center operations where margins remain more attractive in the current market environment.

The broader trend reflects a harsh reality: companies that aggressively loaded up on Bitcoin during the 2024-2025 bull run are now forced to sell holdings to repay debt and fund strategic transitions. Bitcoin's 47% decline from its October 2025 high has trapped many miners with underwater positions, accelerating capital reallocation toward higher-margin AI infrastructure plays.

Source: 24/7 Wall St

Banks Pressure Regulators Over Stablecoin Yield "Loophole"

Traditional banking institutions are escalating calls for regulators to close what they view as a regulatory loophole around stablecoin yields, fearing erosion of their core deposit base. As yield on stablecoins approaches traditional savings account rates, banks argue consumers have an unfair incentive to move funds off-chain.

The pressure campaign reflects genuine competitive concern: if consumers can access 4-5% yields on stablecoins without counterparty risk, the value proposition of traditional bank deposits weakens. Regulators are weighing the concerns against innovation interests, with potential new stablecoin frameworks likely to address yield mechanisms.

Source: PYMNTS

Quantum Security & Protocol Development

BIP 360 Quantum Defense Proposal Merged February 2026

A major Bitcoin Improvement Proposal addressing quantum computing threats was merged in February 2026, establishing a pathway to phase out address types vulnerable to quantum decryption. The phased approach allows legacy addresses to continue operating while new quantum-resistant alternatives gain adoption.

Source: Bitcoin Dev

Starkware Publishes Quantum-Safe Transaction Scheme

StarkWare's Chief Product Officer has published a cryptographic scheme enabling quantum-safe Bitcoin transactions using existing protocol infrastructure, with transaction costs estimated at $75 per swap. The approach allows users to upgrade to quantum-resistant signing without waiting for full protocol changes.

Source: Starkware

Developers Showcase "Attack Blocks" for Community Education

Bitcoin developers have implemented educational "attack blocks" on test networks to demonstrate quantum vulnerability scenarios, helping the community understand risks and urgency of migration to quantum-resistant signing schemes.

Source: Bitcoin Dev

Arizona is exploring the possibility of holding Bitcoin in state reserves, with a potential voter approval measure scheduled for November 2026. If approved, Arizona would join El Salvador as a sovereign Bitcoin holder.

Texas & New Hampshire Bitcoin Tax Exemptions

Both Texas and New Hampshire are actively exploring state-level Bitcoin tax exemption policies to attract digital asset businesses and miners to their jurisdictions.
