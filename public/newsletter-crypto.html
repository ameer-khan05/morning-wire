Lead Story

Bitcoin Tests $68K as $14B Options Expiry Collides with Geopolitical Crisis
Bitcoin's struggle below $70,000 intensifies today as a critical $14 billion options expiration event coincides with escalating geopolitical tensions. With 189,000 BTC options contracts ($13.38B notional value) expiring today, traders face extremely bearish put-call ratios that could trigger significant price volatility.
The timing couldn't be worse for risk-on sentiment. Now in its 28th day, the Iran conflict shows no signs of cooling as Iran rejected a 15-point US ceasefire proposal. This geopolitical backdrop has added urgency to Bitcoin's year-to-date decline of ~20%, while spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded a notable $171M net outflow, signaling institutional caution.
What to watch: The options expiration could act as a pinball, bouncing price action sharply in either direction depending on which contracts expire in-the-money. Combined with weak institutional inflows and the Iran situation, Bitcoin's support levels below $68K are increasingly fragile.

Market Analysis

Ethereum Struggles at $2,070 as ETF Outflows Continue
While Bitcoin and new commodities like XRP and SOL attracted positive ETF inflows during March 16-20, Ethereum ETFs bucked the trend with notable outflows. This divergence signals weakening institutional confidence in Ethereum relative to competing smart contract platforms and commodity-classified assets.
The outflows come as Ethereum grapples with technical resistance at $2,070, a level it's failed to sustain. With macro headwinds from geopolitical tensions and profit-taking, the second-largest crypto faces uphill momentum.
Iran War Impact: Crypto Without Institutional Support is Fragile
The ongoing Iran conflict (Day 28) underscores a critical vulnerability in crypto markets: without a consistent institutional bid, prices are highly susceptible to retail-driven sell-offs during risk-off periods. The weak spot Bitcoin ETF flows despite commodity classification suggest institutional investors are taking a "show me" approach.
Each escalation in geopolitical tensions triggers fresh selling, as risk-averse traders flee to traditional safe havens. Crypto's hope for a sustained rally rests on a combination of regulatory clarity (✓ partly achieved) and geopolitical de-escalation (✗ not yet achieved).
Altcoin Forecast: XRP, SOL Rally May Continue into Year-End

SOL Price Target: $210–$290 by December 2026
Regulatory momentum around commodity classification and derivative expansion creates a compelling case for altcoins positioned to benefit from institutional adoption. XRP's benefit from the SEC clarity and Solana's ecosystem strength position both well, assuming macro conditions stabilize.
