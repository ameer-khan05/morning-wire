Lead Story

SEC Ships "Regulation Crypto" to the White House — The Most Sweeping Rulemaking in Crypto History

SEC Chair Paul Atkins confirmed that the commission's landmark "Regulation Crypto" proposal has been sent to the White House Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs, signaling imminent publication. The rulemaking focuses on the Securities Act of 1933 and addresses fundraising and startup exemptions — a framework that could finally give the industry the regulatory clarity it has sought for years.

Separately, the SEC plans to release its long-awaited "innovation exemption," which would create a regulatory sandbox for crypto startups to operate with lighter compliance requirements during their early stages. Combined with the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act nearing final passage in Congress, the U.S. crypto regulatory landscape is undergoing its most significant transformation since the inception of digital assets.

The question is no longer whether regulation is coming — it's whether the industry can absorb the compliance costs. As Bloomberg Law noted: "Crypto's rules are here. 2026 will be about making them work."

Legislation & Regulation

Digital Asset Market Clarity Act Nears the Finish Line

Lawmakers are closing in on a final agreement, with JPMorgan noting negotiations have entered their "late stage." Most disputes have been resolved, narrowing contentious issues from a dozen to just two or three. A key sticking point: whether stablecoin issuers can offer yield. The emerging compromise would prohibit passive yield while allowing activity-based rewards tied to payments and platform usage.

Source: Bitcoin Magazine | Yahoo Finance

PARITY Act Reintroduced for Crypto Tax Reform

Congressmen Steven Horsford (D-Nev.) and Max Miller (R-Ohio) reintroduced the Digital Asset Protection, Accountability, Regulation, Innovation, Taxation and Yields (PARITY) Act, aiming to bring clarity to crypto taxation. First released in draft form last December, the revised bill addresses long-standing confusion over how digital assets are taxed across different transaction types.

Source: CoinDesk

Japan Reclassifies Crypto as Securities — Prison Terms for Violators

Japan's cabinet approved legislation putting cryptocurrencies in the same legal category as stocks and bonds under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act. The move carries real teeth: up to 10 years in prison and fines up to 10 million yen for operating without registration. Japan becomes the first major economy to fully align crypto regulation with traditional securities law.

Source: Spoted Crypto

BIP-361: Freezing Quantum-Vulnerable Wallets Before It's Too Late

Bitcoin developers proposed BIP-361 to freeze quantum-vulnerable wallets — a move that could lock dormant BTC including Satoshi Nakamoto's estimated 1.1 million coins, now worth roughly $74 billion. The proposal addresses the growing concern that quantum computers could eventually crack the elliptic curve cryptography protecting early Bitcoin wallets, potentially enabling theft of billions in assets.
