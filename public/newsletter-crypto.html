Lead Story

Bitcoin's 25% rebound meets the test of regulatory reality

Bitcoin pushed above $75,000 overnight, its highest level in a month, as investors fled equities that now look fully priced and sought out a hedge against the ongoing Middle East conflict. The move extends a quiet but persistent rebound — BTC is up roughly 25% from its 2026 trough near $60,000 — and flips bullish the short-term technicals that had looked ominous only weeks ago.

The rally is not without resistance. Derivatives data show $450M in sell orders stacked just above current prices, and liquidations are ticking up as leveraged longs chase the move. Ethereum is having a tougher time, trading at $2,324 after Vitalik Buterin's headline-grabbing personal sales in early 2026. Ether has 13 bullish and 19 bearish technical signals lit on its dashboard today — a split market.

The bigger story, though, is regulatory. The CLARITY Act's stablecoin-yield deadlock broke Tuesday. Japan moved crypto under its securities act. The SEC sent its "Regulation Crypto" proposal to the White House. Almost a decade after the last bull market's speculative peak, the scaffolding of a regulated digital-asset industry is being poured in real time.

International

Japan's Cabinet approves reclassifying crypto as securities

On April 10, Japan's cabinet moved digital assets from the Payment Services Act to the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act. Operating without registration now carries up to 10 years in prison — triple the prior maximum — and fines up to 10 million yen.
