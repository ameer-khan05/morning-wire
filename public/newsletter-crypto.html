SEC and CFTC Issue Landmark Joint Crypto Classification

In a watershed moment for the crypto industry, the SEC issued a groundbreaking interpretation on March 17, 2026, clarifying how federal securities laws apply to digital assets. The CFTC joined the effort, creating what may be the clearest regulatory framework yet for cryptocurrency.

The agencies' joint guidance established a comprehensive five-part token taxonomy and made crucial distinctions: Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, and XRP are classified as "digital commodities"—not securities. This removes substantial regulatory uncertainty that has plagued the sector for years.

The interpretation also provides critical exemptions: airdrops, protocol staking, and mining are excluded from securities regulation. Investment contracts end when the issuer fulfills or fails on its promises, establishing clearer termination points for token offerings.

While the guidance may not solve all regulatory questions, it represents the first time federal regulators have provided consistent, joint definitions about what crypto assets actually are—a major step toward institutional adoption.

European Central Bank Launches Formal Altcoin Investigation Under MiCA

The European Central Bank has initiated a formal investigation into four altcoins under its Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulatory framework, signaling intensified scrutiny in the European Union. This move underscores the EU's commitment to enforcing its comprehensive crypto regulations, which took effect earlier this year.

The investigation reflects the ECB's role as a key regulator in implementing MiCA requirements, which impose strict capital reserve rules, custody standards, and operational requirements on crypto service providers. European exchanges and projects should expect increased compliance demands going forward.

CLARITY Act Stalls in Senate — Crypto Legislation at Risk

The Crypto-Asset Liability and Regulatory Accountability (CLARITY) Act, which successfully passed the House in July 2025, has stalled in the Senate Banking Committee. The committee postponed its markup session, raising concerns among crypto advocates that momentum for comprehensive digital asset legislation could evaporate.

With midterm elections looming in 2026, crypto proponents fear that without Senate action soon, the political window for passing major legislation will close. Adding to concerns is a controversial provision involving stablecoin yields, which has drawn scrutiny from regulators and some lawmakers.

Industry observers view Senate action on the CLARITY Act as critical for establishing clear rules around stablecoin issuance, custody, and trading that would accelerate mainstream adoption.

CFTC Chair Plans to Bring Perpetual Futures Back to US

The Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) chair announced plans to revive perpetual futures trading in the United States, marking a significant reversal from previous regulatory restraint. "Bringing true crypto perpetual futures back to the United States is a key task of pro-innovation policy," the chair stated.

Perpetual futures—leverage trading instruments that allow investors to bet on price movements without contract expiration dates—were effectively unavailable to U.S. traders following regulatory crackdowns on unregistered derivatives platforms. Their return would represent a major step toward legitimizing crypto derivatives and bringing trading activity back offshore platforms back onshore.

Source: The Coin Republic - U.S. Crypto Regulation: CFTC Eyes Revival of Perpetual Futures Trading

Solana in Freefall: Down 72% From All-Time High

Solana has become one of the worst performers in the crypto market, plunging 72% from its all-time high and trading near $85. The decline mirrors troubling fundamental indicators: network transactions have dropped 3.2%, and the number of active addresses has fallen 11% over the past month alone.

Binance CEO CZ Issues Urgent Warning on Crypto Listing Scams

The CEO of Binance has issued an urgent warning to investors about sophisticated scams targeting unsuspecting crypto traders seeking to list or trade tokens on major exchanges. Scammers are increasingly impersonating exchange officials and project developers to steal funds or gain unauthorized access to accounts.

With market sentiment already fragile and retail investors on edge, fraudulent schemes are proliferating. Users are advised to exercise extreme caution when approached with offers for expedited listings, exclusive trading access, or guaranteed returns. Always verify communications directly with official channels rather than responding to unsolicited messages.
