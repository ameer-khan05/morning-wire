Lead Story

Bitcoin Holds Above $72K as Ceasefire Optimism Battles Inflation Fears

Bitcoin reached $72,885 before settling around $72,204 on April 11. The global crypto market gained to $2.55T, but sentiment remains in extreme fear territory. BTC surged past $72K when the US-Iran ceasefire was announced on April 8, as oil crashed. TD Cowen predicts BTC could hit $140K by late 2026, calling it "digital gold." The market's volatile reaction underscores crypto's sensitivity to macroeconomic factors and geopolitical developments.
Source: Coin Gabbar - Latest Crypto Market Update

SEC Sends 'Reg Crypto' Proposal to White House

SEC Chair Paul Atkins confirmed the "regulation crypto" proposal has been sent to the White House Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs. It addresses how to regulate crypto and distinguish between securities and non-securities transactions, including startup exemptions. This landmark development represents the SEC's effort to create a cohesive regulatory framework after years of piecemeal guidance.
Source: CoinDesk - SEC Regulation Crypto Proposal

Japan Reclassifies Crypto Under Financial Instruments Act

Japan's cabinet approved legislation on April 10 putting crypto in the same legal category as stocks and bonds, upgrading from the Payment Services Act. Penalties increase to 10 years in prison and 10 million yen fines for operating without registration. This significant reclassification positions Japan as a leader in bringing crypto into mainstream financial regulation.
Source: Cryptonomist - Japan Crypto Regulation

CLARITY Act Could Reach Senate Floor This Month

Senator Bill Hagerty says the cryptocurrency CLARITY Act could advance through Senate Banking Committee and reach full Senate vote before end of April. The act aims to provide clarity on digital asset taxation and regulatory classification, addressing long-standing questions from the crypto industry.
Source: Coinpedia - US Crypto Regulation 2026

SEC and CFTC Issue Joint Crypto Interpretation Framework

On March 17, the SEC and CFTC issued a comprehensive interpretation clarifying how federal securities laws apply to cryptoassets, following a March 11 Memorandum of Understanding establishing coordination between both agencies. This joint framework represents a major step toward unified regulatory clarity in the digital asset space.
Source: Latham & Watkins - US Crypto Policy Tracker

Bitcoin's Next Move Hinges on Oil Prices

Analysts say BTC's trajectory is closely linked to oil markets. The US-Iran ceasefire sent oil crashing and BTC surging, but the Strait of Hormuz remaining closed keeps both markets on edge. This correlation highlights how traditional commodity markets and geopolitical factors can significantly impact crypto price movements.
Source: CoinDesk - Bitcoin and Oil Market Correlation

Global Regulatory Wave Hits Crypto in Single Week

Japan, Hong Kong, South Korea, and the US all took major crypto regulatory action in a single week in April 2026, signaling coordinated global movement toward comprehensive frameworks. This synchronized regulatory push suggests a shift toward institutionalization of digital assets across major financial centers worldwide.
Source: Spotted Crypto - Global Regulation Wave

Crypto Industry Embraces 'Regulator-in-the-Loop' Strategy

As federal rules roll out, crypto firms are proactively engaging regulators rather than fighting them, marking a cultural shift in the industry. This collaborative approach reflects maturation of the sector and recognition that regulatory clarity benefits long-term market development and consumer protection.
Source: PYMNTS - Regulator-in-the-Loop Strategy
