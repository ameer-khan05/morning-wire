Bitcoin Drops Below $71K as Oil Surge and Rate Fears Trigger Broad Risk-Off

Bitcoin dropped to $71,300 and Ethereum fell toward $2,000 as surging oil prices, falling equities, and weak liquidity sparked broad risk-off flows. The token briefly hit its lowest level in two weeks before rebounding nearly 5% on Monday after a geopolitical de-escalation signal, but gave back gains as the Iran situation remained unresolved. Bitcoin has traded in a tight range for nearly 50 days — structural consolidation, not a bear flag — but downside risks are rising.

Regulation & Policy

Clarity Act Stablecoin Language Drops — Industry Reacts

The revised CLARITY Act text from Senators Alsobrooks and Tillis would ban yield payments for simply holding a stablecoin. However, activity-based rewards tied to loyalty programs, promotions, subscriptions, transactions, and payments remain allowed — provided they don't meet an "economic equivalence" standard. Both the crypto industry and banks are unhappy: crypto sees it as overly narrow, banks see it as full of loopholes.

SEC Names Bitcoin, Ether, Solana and 13 More as Digital Commodities

In a landmark regulatory shift, the SEC has classified Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, and 13 other crypto assets as digital commodities — not securities. This moves oversight of these assets toward the CFTC and away from the SEC's enforcement-heavy approach. The SEC and CFTC also signed a Memorandum of Understanding establishing a Joint Harmonization Initiative to coordinate oversight.

Bhutan Continues Bitcoin Sell-Off — Down to 4,453 BTC

The Kingdom of Bhutan has continued liquidating its sovereign Bitcoin reserves, dropping from a peak of roughly 13,000 BTC to just 4,453 BTC. The sales have been gradual and are occurring during a period of consolidation, suggesting the government is taking advantage of relatively stable prices rather than panic selling.
