Lead Story
SEC & CFTC Classify 16 Crypto Assets as Digital Commodities
In what's being called the most significant regulatory development in crypto history, a March 17 joint interpretive rule from the Securities and Exchange Commission and the Commodity Futures Trading Commission officially classified 16 major tokens—including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, Cardano, and Avalanche—as "digital commodities." This landmark decision fundamentally reshapes the regulatory landscape by shifting spot market jurisdiction to the CFTC, unblocking the ETF pipeline for all 16 newly classified assets, and most critically, confirming that staking is not a securities transaction. The framework builds on a March 11 memorandum of understanding between the agencies that established a formal coordination framework for ongoing oversight. The clarity has energized institutional investors and sparked a fresh wave of product development across the industry.
Source: SEC Official Statement, Bloomberg Law

GENIUS Act Implementation Deadlines Approach
Signed by President Trump in July 2025, the GENIUS Act establishes aggressive implementation timelines for comprehensive stablecoin regulation. Regulators must finalize stablecoin rules by July 18, 2026, with full implementation targeted for January 2027. Industry participants are preparing for what promises to be an extensive rulemaking process that will define stablecoin issuance, reserves, and redemption mechanics for the next decade.
Source: DL News
