Lead Story

Iran War Day 29 — Ceasefire Talks Deadlocked as Strikes Continue

The US-Israel conflict with Iran has entered its second month with no resolution in sight. The war began February 28 when the US and Israel launched surprise airstrikes killing Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and several officials. Iran has since responded with missile and drone strikes against Israel, US bases, and US allies. The Strait of Hormuz remains closed, disrupting 20% of global crude supply.
Iran dismissed the US 15-point peace plan as "maximalist, unreasonable." Iran's foreign minister stated "No negotiations have happened with the enemy until now, and we do not plan on any negotiations." Iran issued its own counterproposal demanding: halt to killings of officials, guarantees against future wars, reparations, end of hostilities, and sovereignty over the Strait of Hormuz.
Trump extended the deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait to April 6, holding off on striking Iran's power plants. US envoy Steve Witkoff said he expects meetings "this week."
Sources: Al Jazeera, PBS NewsHour
