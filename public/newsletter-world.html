Iran War Enters Day 30: Houthis Join Conflict as USS Tripoli Deploys

The conflict expands with Houthi involvement and continued military escalation

Regional Tensions Escalate

As the Iran conflict enters its fourth week, the conflict zone expands with Houthi rebels launching their first strikes on Israel since the war began. Iran continues warning against any US ground invasion, while the Trump administration claims Iran has agreed to "most of" a 15-point demand list. Meanwhile, the USS Tripoli has arrived with 3,500 US service members, bolstering the American military presence across the region.

Strait of Hormuz Remains Closed

The Strait of Hormuz has remained closed since March 2, with the Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) confirming the blockade. The US military campaign to reopen the critical waterway began March 19. As of March 12, there have been 21 confirmed attacks on merchant vessels, with 150+ ships anchored outside the strait unable to pass through this vital chokepoint for global oil trade.
