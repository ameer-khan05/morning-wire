Iran War Reaches One Month — Houthis Enter the Fray

The US-Israel war on Iran has reached its one-month mark with the conflict widening dangerously. Yemen's Houthi rebels confirmed their first attack on Israel since the war began February 28. At least 10 US service members were injured in an Iranian attack on Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia, with at least two suffering shrapnel wounds. Israel's military struck a heavy water reactor in Iran, which it said was part of Iran's nuclear enrichment program. President Trump warned the war is "not finished" with thousands of targets remaining. The Strait of Hormuz — through which about 20% of the world's oil flows — remains largely blocked, causing global economic disruption.

Sources: NPR • CNN • Al Jazeera

US Troops Wounded in Attack on Saudi Base

At least 10 US service members were injured in an Iranian attack on Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia — one of the most direct attacks on US forces during the conflict. No service members were killed, and at least two had shrapnel wounds considered not life-threatening. The attack underscores the expanding geographic scope of the war.

Source: NPR

Israel Strikes Iranian Heavy Water Reactor

Israel's military reported striking a heavy water reactor in Iran, claiming it was part of Iran's nuclear enrichment program. The strike represents a significant escalation targeting Iran's nuclear infrastructure. Iran has fired waves of missiles and drones at Israel in retaliation, while Tehran strongly rejects US claims that Iran's Navy has been destroyed.

Source: Jerusalem Post

Ukraine Peace Deal 90-95% Agreed, Trump and Zelenskyy Say

Presidents Trump and Zelenskyy have agreed on 90 to 95 percent of a peace proposal, with the United States appearing to endorse European plans for security guarantees and a reassurance force for Ukraine. Russian forces have captured ~29,154 square miles (13% of Ukraine) since 2022. As of March 26, 158 combat engagements were recorded in 24 hours with 231 guided aerial bombs dropped. Russia has suffered approximately 1,293,170 casualties.

Source: Russia Matters

Sudan Accuses Ethiopia of 'Hostile Acts'

Sudan's military-led government accused neighboring Ethiopia of "hostile acts," noting that drone strikes had been launched from Ethiopian territory against targets in Sudan. Mounting evidence suggests Addis Ababa has been supporting the paramilitary Rapid Support Forces, threatening to draw another major African power into the conflict.

Source: Foreign Affairs

China Condemns Iran Strikes as 'Grave Violation of Sovereignty'

China characterized the US-Israel attack and killing of Iran's supreme leader as "a grave violation of Iran's sovereignty and security" that "tramples on the purposes and principles of the UN Charter." The strong condemnation highlights deepening geopolitical fault lines as the world's great powers take sides.

Source: Chinese Foreign Ministry

Trump Slams NATO's Lack of Support for Iran War

President Trump again criticized NATO allies for their lack of support for the US-led war on Iran. Europe is described as rattled by Trump's approach to geopolitics but unable to produce an alternative vision, with Trump's trade policies testing relationships with closest allies including Australia, Canada, Europe, Japan, and New Zealand.

Source: Al Jazeera

India and Russia Resume Direct LNG Supplies

India and Russia agreed to resume direct supplies of liquefied natural gas, signaling the two countries' continued energy partnership despite Western pressure. The deal comes as global energy markets face severe disruption from the Iran war and Strait of Hormuz blockage.

Source: Pravda News
