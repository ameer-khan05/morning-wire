Lead Story

Trump Brokers 10-Day Israel-Lebanon Ceasefire, but Cracks Already Show

President Trump announced a 10-day ceasefire between Israel and Iran-backed Hezbollah in Lebanon, set to begin at 5 p.m. ET Thursday. The agreement came after Trump confirmed he spoke with both Lebanese President Joseph Aoun and Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, calling it a critical step toward a broader Middle East peace framework.

However, the deal showed immediate cracks. Lebanese President Aoun declined to speak directly with Netanyahu, contradicting earlier claims by Israeli officials and Trump himself that the two leaders would hold historic talks. The disconnect underscores the deep mistrust between the parties and the fragility of any diplomatic progress in the region.

The ceasefire attempts to build on the two-week truce between Iran and the U.S. announced on April 7, though that agreement has been strained by Israel's continued bombing campaign against Iranian proxy groups in Lebanon — a move Tehran argues violates the spirit of the truce. The question now is whether 10 days is enough to create momentum toward a durable peace, or whether it merely delays the next escalation.

Source: CNN | Al Jazeera
Middle East & Iran

Senate Rejects War Powers Resolution on Iran, 47-52

The U.S. Senate rejected a resolution seeking to rein in President Trump's ability to wage war against Iran. All Senate Republicans except Kentucky's Rand Paul voted against the measure, effectively granting the administration continued latitude in the Iran theater. The vote comes amid growing debate over congressional war powers and executive authority in the conflict.

Source: Democracy Now\!

Oil Companies Bank $30M Per Hour in Iran War Windfall Profits

A Guardian analysis reveals the world's top 100 oil and gas companies banked more than $30 million in windfall profits per hour during the first month of the US-Israeli war in Iran. Saudi Aramco, Gazprom, and ExxonMobil were among the biggest beneficiaries, raising urgent questions about war profiteering and the politics of energy markets during conflict.

Source: Democracy Now\! / The Guardian

Iran-US Negotiations Continue in Islamabad

Negotiators arrived in Islamabad for high-stakes ceasefire talks between the U.S. and Iran amid allegations of bad faith and ceasefire violations from both sides. The central dispute: Israel and the US maintain the Lebanon campaign falls outside the Iran-US ceasefire framework, while Tehran insists it is integral to any deal. Pakistan is emerging as a neutral broker in the delicate negotiations.

Source: International Affairs Review
Europe & Eastern Europe

Hungary's Orbán Suffers Historic Electoral Defeat

Longtime Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orbán suffered a massive defeat in national elections, with opposition leader Péter Magyar's party claiming a supermajority. After 16 years of increasingly illiberal governance, Hungary now has the chance to realign with European democratic norms and play a constructive role in NATO and European integration. The result is being hailed as the most significant democratic reversal in Central Europe since the fall of communism.

Source: Foreign Affairs

Russia Launches Massive Drone and Missile Attack on Ukraine

Russia hammered civilian areas of Ukraine with hundreds of drones and dozens of missiles in an attack that stretched for hours, killing at least 16 people. The barrage was one of the heaviest bombardments of the year, targeting residential neighborhoods and critical infrastructure across multiple Ukrainian cities. The attack came as international attention remained focused on the Middle East.

Pope Leo XIV Condemns "Tyrants" Fueling War During Africa Visit

Pope Leo XIV delivered a forceful condemnation of "tyrants" fueling war with billions during his ongoing trip to Africa. His calls for global peace come amid rising tensions with President Trump, with the Vatican increasingly positioning itself as a moral counterweight to great power politics. The Pope's remarks were widely interpreted as addressing both the Iran conflict and the global arms trade.

IMF Warns: "Global Economy in the Shadow of War"

The International Monetary Fund released its April 2026 World Economic Outlook from Washington, D.C., with the stark title "Global Economy in the Shadow of War." The report highlights the Iran conflict and energy price spikes as the dominant risks to global growth, downgrading forecasts for oil-importing nations. The G-24 ministers met to discuss how developing nations can weather the storm.
