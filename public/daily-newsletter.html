Lead Story

Iranian Missiles Strike Oil Tanker Off Qatar as War Escalates Across the Gulf

Three cruise missiles launched from Iran targeted Qatar early Wednesday, with two intercepted and a third striking the Aqua 1 fuel oil tanker 17 nautical miles north of Qatar's Ras Laffan industrial hub. No casualties were reported, but the attack knocked out 17% of Qatar's LNG export capacity and has stalled 150 freight ships — including dozens of oil tankers — in the Strait of Hormuz.

Iran simultaneously struck targets in Bahrain, Israel, Jordan, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE. A drone attack sparked a large fire at Kuwait International Airport's fuel tanks. In Tel Aviv and Bnei Brak, missile fragments wounded at least 16 people including a child. Trump says the U.S. could be done with the war in two to three weeks, and will address the nation at 9 p.m. tonight.
