The Anchor

BTC retreats as Brent breaks $111 — $155B in crypto ETF AUM shows institutions buying the dip even as oil caps the rally.

Bitcoin opened Tuesday at $77,368 — its lowest level since Saturday — as Brent crude broke $111 in early morning trading on Iran's Day 60 stalemate. The pattern is now well-established: Iran peace-talk optimism drives a BTC and equity risk-on rally; oil's surge as talks fail reasserts inflation expectations and caps the move. Monday's trajectory was textbook: BTC touched $79,000 at the Bitcoin 2026 Conference open, pulled back to $78,670 by close as oil broke $108, then opened Tuesday at $77,368 as Brent crossed $111 and Nasdaq futures fell 1.2%. The correlation between BTC price action and Hormuz-driven oil moves is not coincidental — it reflects the same macro regime: high oil prices → inflation risk → Fed on hold longer → higher real rates → risk assets capped.

The institutional picture is more constructive than the price action suggests. Crypto ETFs attracted $933 million in single-day inflows Monday — a figure that pushed total AUM across all crypto funds to $155 billion, the highest level since February. Spot Bitcoin ETF inflows have remained consistently positive throughout the April conference period even as price has oscillated between $77,000 and $79,000. The inflow-price divergence reflects two parallel investor communities: retail and momentum traders responding to daily macro headlines, and institutional allocators building structural positions on a multi-week or multi-month horizon. Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy) continues to exemplify the institutional posture: its 818,334 BTC treasury — valued at approximately $63.4 billion at current prices — is unchanged, with Polymarket pricing the probability of any 2026 sale at 10%.

Crypto ETFs · $155B AUM $933M Single-day inflows · highest AUM since Feb · institutional accumulation

Crypto ETFs attract $933M in a single day — total AUM hits $155B, highest since February, even as BTC price falls.

Crypto exchange-traded funds attracted $933 million in net inflows on Monday — one of the largest single-day inflow figures since the spot Bitcoin ETF launches in January 2024 — pushing total AUM across all crypto funds to $155 billion, the highest level since February's highs. The inflow-price divergence is significant: BTC fell 1.65% on Tuesday morning even as Monday's ETF inflows were the largest in weeks. The pattern reflects two distinct investor communities operating on different time horizons. Retail and macro-sensitive traders respond to daily Iran/oil headlines and reduce exposure when Brent crosses $110; institutional allocators — including pension funds, family offices, and sovereign wealth entities using ETFs as their primary BTC access vehicle — are using price dips as buying opportunities within a multi-month allocation framework. BlackRock's IBIT and Fidelity's FBTC remain the dominant vehicles; combined, the two ETFs account for more than 60% of the total crypto ETF AUM. The $155 billion AUM figure, if sustained, represents approximately 3.2% of total S&P 500 market cap — a non-trivial institutional allocation that would have been inconceivable three years ago.

BIP-360 · Quantum · Day 3 Tomorrow Nakamoto Stage · Hunter Beast · 6.9M BTC at risk · testnet v0.3.0

BIP-360 quantum-resistance panel tomorrow at Bitcoin 2026 — the upgrade that would protect 6.9M BTC from future quantum attack.

The most technically consequential session at Bitcoin 2026 arrives tomorrow (Day 3, April 29): a panel titled "How Real Is The Quantum Threat?" on the Nakamoto Stage featuring Hunter Beast, co-author of BIP-360 — the quantum-resistant Bitcoin wallet proposal that has already been merged into the Bitcoin Core BIP repository. BIP-360 introduces a new P2MR (Pay to Merkle Root) address type that replaces the ECDSA signature scheme currently used by Bitcoin with a quantum-resistant alternative based on Merkle trees and SHA-256 hashing. The urgency: CoinDesk analysis estimates approximately 6.9 million BTC — including coins in Satoshi Nakamoto's original wallets — are held in address formats that would be theoretically vulnerable to a sufficiently powerful quantum computer. BIP-360 was deployed on Bitcoin Quantum Testnet v0.3.0 in March 2026; activation on mainnet requires broad community consensus. Google's Willow quantum chip, announced in late 2025, demonstrated capabilities that shortened the industry's timeline for quantum risk from "decades away" to "within this decade."

CLARITY Act · Senate Pending Markup delayed · Lummis warning · Warsh vote crowds calendar · July 18

CLARITY Act Senate markup still pending — Warsh vote and Big Tech earnings may crowd it out of the April window.

The Senate Banking Committee's planned markup of the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act remains unscheduled as of Tuesday, with the Warsh confirmation vote and Big Tech earnings absorption consuming the committee's available bandwidth this week. Senator Lummis has publicly warned that missing the April markup window could push comprehensive US digital-asset market-structure legislation to 2030. The CLARITY Act would resolve stablecoin yield ambiguity from the GENIUS Act's Section 4(c), establish a DeFi regulatory framework, and clarify exchange licensing requirements. The GENIUS Act stablecoin implementing regulations are due by July 18 — 81 days away — giving the CLARITY Act limited runway to integrate with the stablecoin framework before the regulatory clock expires. Tether's USDT supply has grown to nearly $150 billion, providing the liquidity base for the broader crypto market; without CLARITY Act passage, the regulatory status of USDT yield — and thus its institutional deployability — remains legally ambiguous under US law.

Tomorrow's Kevin Warsh confirmation vote at Senate Banking carries crypto implications beyond monetary policy. Warsh has expressed more openness to digital-asset market-structure clarity than the Powell-era Fed, which repeatedly deferred to Congress on crypto and imposed restrictive guidance on banks holding digital-asset assets (SAB 121 and its successors). A Warsh-led Fed may be more willing to clarify reserve eligibility for stablecoin issuers and issue clearer custody guidance for banks seeking to hold client BTC. The more immediate implication: Warsh's first FOMC meeting is May 7, six days after the April PCE print (May 1), with Brent at $111. A new Fed chair holding rates steady with oil above $100 would be the most crypto-bullish monetary policy outcome possible at the current juncture — and markets are pricing exactly that for May.

Quick hits

  • Bitcoin's intraday range between $77,000 and $79,000 over the past three sessions is tighter than any comparable three-day window since March — a compression that typically precedes a significant directional move. The catalyst is binary: a US acceptance of Iran's Hormuz proposal resolves oil, drives a BTC surge above $80,000; a continued stalemate keeps the $77–79K range intact until the FOMC meeting on May 7.
  • ETH's smaller decline (▼ 0.52%) versus BTC (▼ 1.65%) on Tuesday is notable: ETH has been the less macro-sensitive asset in the current cycle, with its price action more influenced by DeFi TVL flows and staking yield than by oil and Iran headlines. BTC dominance at 60.45% suggests the cycle remains BTC-led; when dominance peaks and turns, the historical pattern is an ETH-led altcoin rotation phase within 4–8 weeks.
  • Strategy's 818,334 BTC treasury — 108 purchases at an average of $75,528 — is now approximately $1,300 per coin in unrealized profit at current prices ($77,370). The company's stock trades at a premium to BTC NAV that implies continued BTC appreciation; if BTC stays in the $77–79K range through May, that premium will compress, creating potential MSTR selling pressure from arbitrageurs regardless of BTC direction.
  • The "institutional grift" criticism of Bitcoin 2026 reflects a genuine tension in Bitcoin's cultural evolution: the original conference ethos centered on sovereignty, privacy, and permissionless finance; the 2026 edition features corporate treasury panels, ETF discussions, and law-enforcement keynotes. Critics argue this represents Bitcoin being captured by the institutions it was designed to circumvent. Defenders argue institutional adoption is the mechanism by which Bitcoin achieves monetary sovereignty at scale.
  • BIP-360's quantum panel tomorrow arrives as the most technically credentialed audience in Bitcoin's 17-year history — Google's Willow chip, announced in late 2025, has shifted the quantum threat from theoretical to engineering-timeline relevant. The key question the panel will address: is the threat imminent enough to justify a Bitcoin-wide soft fork within 3–5 years, or does the community have more time to deliberate the tradeoffs of a new address type?
  • Tether's $150 billion USDT supply makes it one of the 20 largest buyers of short-dated US Treasury bills globally — a position that has direct implications for T-bill market liquidity and, ironically, for the very dollar-denominated financial system that crypto was designed to circumvent. CLARITY Act passage would bring this purchasing activity under a formal regulatory framework for the first time, which both stabilizes and constrains it.
