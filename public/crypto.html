The Anchor

Lazarus Group forges a cross-chain message and walks out with $292 million.

At 17:35 UTC Saturday, attackers drained 116,500 rsETH from Kelp DAO's LayerZero-powered bridge — worth roughly $292 million and representing 18% of rsETH's entire circulating supply. The method was elegant and surgical: the Lazarus Group compromised two RPC nodes, used a DDoS attack to force failover, and tricked LayerZero's verifier into approving a fraudulent cross-chain transaction. LayerZero has since attributed the exploit to North Korea and placed partial blame on Kelp's decision to use a single-verifier configuration, which Kelp had previously been warned against.

The downstream damage was swift and severe. Aave saw $8 billion in total value locked exit the platform over the weekend as the stolen rsETH was deposited as collateral to generate approximately $195 million in bad debt, triggering a wave of panic withdrawals. rsETH lost its peg. Justin Sun publicly pleaded with the attacker to "just talk." Meanwhile, BTC slipped below $75,000 — hammered by both the hack's market impact and the renewed Hormuz closure that sent the broader risk-off signal through every asset class on Monday.

Exploit · DeFi $292M Kelp DAO · rsETH

Kelp DAO loses $292 million — the largest DeFi hack of 2026.

The attacker compromised two RPC nodes serving Kelp's LayerZero bridge, used a DDoS to force failover onto the compromised nodes, then submitted a fraudulent cross-chain transaction that the verifier approved. 116,500 rsETH exited to an attacker-controlled address in a single block.

Source: CoinDesk
Contagion · Aave $8B TVL · bank run

Aave loses $8 billion in TVL in a Kelp DAO-triggered bank run.

Stolen rsETH was deposited as collateral on Aave to generate ~$195 million in bad debt, spooking depositors. Total value locked on the lending protocol dropped nearly $8 billion over the weekend. Aave's governance is weighing emergency measures to address the bad debt overhang.

Source: CoinSpot · CoinMarketCap
Attribution Lazarus North Korea · LayerZero

LayerZero attributes exploit to North Korea's Lazarus Group.

The preliminary attribution matches prior Lazarus signatures: RPC compromise, DDoS-forced failover, single-block drain. LayerZero also noted Kelp had been warned to adopt a multi-verifier setup and declined. Justin Sun, whose Tron ecosystem held rsETH exposure, publicly asked the attacker to "let's just talk."

Source: CoinDesk · DL News
Macro · BTC Below $75K Iran + hack double pressure

Bitcoin drops below $75,000 as Iran and the hack collide.

BTC traded at $74,335 Monday morning — down 1.45% on the day — under the combined weight of renewed Hormuz closure risk and the $292M hack's market impact. Ethereum underperformed at $2,282 (−2.51%). Bitcoin dominance rose to 59.2% as altcoins absorbed the heavier selling.

April crypto hack losses top Q1 by nearly 4×.

Kelp DAO ($292M, April 19) and Drift protocol ($285M, April 1 — attributed to Lazarus Group) account for the bulk of April's $606 million in thefts. The pace has reversed the relative quiet of Q1 and renewed calls in Washington for exchange-level security mandates ahead of the CLARITY Act markup.

CLARITY Act markup under pressure to add bridge-security provisions.

Senate Banking staff are now fielding requests from both parties to attach bridge-security minimum standards to the CLARITY Act stablecoin legislation — the Kelp DAO exploit arriving as the bill enters markup.
