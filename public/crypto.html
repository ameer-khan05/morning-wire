Bitcoin fell to $62,499 and Ethereum to $1,688 Friday morning — down 2.40% and 2.20% from Thursday's opening — as crypto continues absorbing the post-FOMC hawkish rate signal on Juneteenth, with US equity and bond markets closed and crypto the only major financial market actively trading.

Friday's declines extend the two-session slide that began with Warsh's hawkish FOMC dot plot on Wednesday, bringing BTC approximately $1,729 below Wednesday's close of $64,228. The structural question for the weekend is whether the post-FOMC repricing has found a floor or continues pressing lower. The nuclear talks postponement adds a new variable: the geopolitical floor the MOU provided — the removal of deal-execution tail risk — is intact, but the delay in nuclear talks introduces uncertainty about whether the second stage of the MOU achieves its goals within the 60-day window. If the talks delay becomes a talks failure, the geopolitical floor weakens. If the delay is temporary and talks begin next week, the floor holds. With US equity and bond markets closed for Juneteenth, crypto bears the full weight of price discovery on Friday — no equity recovery rally to provide a floor signal, no Treasury yield movement to update the rate-cost calculation. Whatever BTC and ETH do Friday is crypto doing it alone, without the correlated market signals that have been part of this week's repricing.

The Anchor

BTC is at $62,499. ETH is at $1,688. US markets are closed. Crypto is trading alone on Juneteenth — processing a hawkish FOMC and a nuclear talks postponement with no correlated market signals. The Iran deal floor is still there. The question is whether the nuclear talks delay erodes it before Monday.

Friday's crypto price action is unusual in one specific way: US equity and bond markets are closed for the Juneteenth federal holiday, meaning crypto is trading without the correlated signals — S&P futures, Treasury yields, dollar index — that normally inform near-term Bitcoin and Ethereum positioning. When equity markets fall, BTC tends to follow. When equity markets recover, BTC often stabilizes or rallies. With equities closed Friday, crypto's direction is being set by two factors the market is processing without external confirmation: the ongoing weight of the hawkish FOMC rate-hike projection, and the new uncertainty introduced by Vance's Switzerland postponement.

BTC's week-over-week performance remains positive in aggregate: from approximately $61,170 last Friday before the MOU announcement to $62,499 Friday morning is still a gain of about $1,329, or roughly 2.2%. The Iran deal recovery trade is not over — it is being compressed. The recovery's peak was approximately $66,433 in the days following the deal announcement. From peak to Friday's $62,499 is a 5.9% retracement. Whether that retracement stabilizes or continues toward $60,000 depends on whether the FOMC's rate projection gets incrementally hawkish commentary from speakers this week, or whether the nuclear talks resume quickly and restore the geopolitical floor.

VP Vance's postponement of the Switzerland nuclear talks Thursday evening introduced a new uncertainty into crypto's geopolitical floor. The floor was built on two components: the MOU's signed execution (removing deal-cancellation tail risk) and market confidence that the second stage — nuclear talks within the 60-day window — would proceed. The postponement challenges the second component. Iran's demand for Lebanon ceasefire guarantees before resuming nuclear talks creates a direct link between Israeli military operations in Lebanon and the timeline of US-Iran nuclear negotiations. If the Lebanon situation is unresolved for weeks, nuclear talks could be delayed beyond the point where 60 days provides adequate negotiating time.

For crypto, the geopolitical floor's durability depends on whether the nuclear talks delay is temporary or structural. A temporary delay — resolved by a Lebanon ceasefire arrangement this week or next — keeps the MOU's second stage on track and preserves the floor. A structural delay — in which Israel refuses to modify its Lebanon position and Iran refuses to negotiate without the guarantee — risks the collapse of the 60-day framework and the potential unwinding of the geopolitical premium that brought BTC from $59,000 to $66,433. The market is pricing something between these scenarios: Friday's 2.40% decline is not a collapse, but it is a floor test.

Juneteenth makes Friday an unusual price discovery session for crypto. NYSE, NASDAQ, and CME are closed for the federal holiday, removing the correlated market signals — S&P 500 moves, Treasury yield changes, dollar index shifts — that normally inform institutional BTC and ETH positioning throughout a trading day. Crypto's 24/7 structure means BTC and ETH trade through the holiday without interruption, but they do so with lower institutional participation (many desks take the federal holiday off) and without the real-time equity market feedback that normally anchors directional conviction.

Historically, crypto trading on US holidays tends to be lower volume with higher volatility per unit of price movement — the result of thinner order books. Friday's 2.40% BTC decline and 2.20% ETH decline are meaningful, but they carry less conviction than the same percentage moves on a full trading day with equity markets open. The more important data points will come Monday when equity markets return: if BTC holds above $62,000 through the holiday weekend and Monday's equity open brings a recovery (on the thesis that Thursday's Nasdaq +1.91% is the start of a broader recovery), the post-FOMC repricing may be near its floor. If Monday brings continued selling with equity markets confirming the hawkish rate thesis and nuclear talks still postponed, the $60,000 range becomes the next test level.

Ethereum's staking yield advantage over Bitcoin persists at $1,688, though the 10-year Treasury yield reaching 4.53% has widened the income gap between Treasuries and ETH staking. At last week's 4.42% Treasury yield, the gap between Treasuries and ETH's approximately 3.5% staking yield was 0.92 percentage points. At 4.53%, that gap has widened to 1.03 percentage points — incrementally reducing ETH staking's relative attractiveness versus fixed income at the margin. The ETH-versus-BTC relative advantage, however, persists: BTC offers zero yield, making ETH staking's 3.5% still a meaningful income component that BTC cannot match, regardless of what Treasuries yield.

The staking yield dynamic explains why ETH has held up better than BTC over the full recovery cycle — ETH's week-over-week gain from pre-deal levels is approximately 3.1% versus BTC's 2.2%, with ETH's income component providing a durable reason for institutional allocators to prefer ETH when constructing a rate-sensitive portfolio. Friday's roughly equal percentage declines in BTC and ETH (-2.40% vs. -2.20%) suggest the staking yield advantage is not the dominant factor in holiday session trading — position liquidation and thin markets are driving both lower together. At longer holding periods, the staking yield remains the most structurally distinct reason to hold ETH versus BTC in the current rate environment.

Quick hits

  • The oil rebound to $76.60 on Thursday — up 2.12% from Wednesday's $75.01 — is a counter-signal worth watching. The conventional expectation was that the MOU signing would drive oil continuously lower as Hormuz reopening added supply. Instead, oil rebounded on Thursday alongside equity markets. The partial explanation is that the Hormuz main channel with 80 mines still requiring clearance is constraining the full supply release that markets expected: alternative routes through Iranian and Omani waters are open, but full commercial tanker traffic through the most efficient route is still weeks away. Oil at $76.60 versus $75.01 means the immediate supply-release trade is being partially priced back out, while the nuclear talks postponement adds uncertainty about the Iranian export sanctions waiver timeline. A slower oil decline is modestly more hawkish for the FOMC's September decision — the data that reaches Warsh by September will show less oil disinflation than the $75.01 print implied if oil retraces to $76-77.
  • DeFi markets remain stable at BTC $62,499 and ETH $1,688, with no cascade liquidations reported Friday morning. Collateral positions built during the recovery from $59,000 retain substantial buffer at current prices, and the Juneteenth holiday session's thin order books have not triggered the kind of violent price spike that could flash-crash collateral ratios below liquidation thresholds. The DeFi stability reading is one data point supporting the view that crypto's current decline is orderly — a macro repricing rather than a structurally driven liquidity event. The floor test at $60,000-$62,000 is real, but DeFi's absence of stress suggests it is not a cascading liquidation floor, which would make a recovery more likely once the macro signals stabilize.
