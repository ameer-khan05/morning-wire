If Micron confirms strong HBM orders through 2027, the AI infrastructure thesis survives Tuesday's correction; BTC and ETH should recover toward pre-selloff levels ($65,000 and $1,750+) over the following sessions. WTI crude fell a further 2.25% to $72.06 Wednesday — the lowest level of 2026 — and Brent dropped to $75.86, extending Tuesday's sanctions-waiver-driven oil decline as Iranian crude continued entering global supply chains. The mechanism: the Treasury's 60-day waiver announced Tuesday covered the full Iranian oil supply chain including banking and shipping, meaning buyers can transact through conventional payment rails rather than evasion channels. Iranian barrels that were previously moving through opaque third-country intermediaries are now being processed through normal trade finance, increasing transparency and volume simultaneously. WTI's 5-day decline from $76+ before the sanctions waiver announcement to $72.06 represents a cumulative ~6% oil price reduction that will feed directly into June CPI energy calculations.

For crypto, WTI at $72.06 matters through the rate mechanism. The oil-to-CPI transmission runs: WTI $72.06 → gasoline prices → June CPI energy component (published mid-July) → FOMC's August and September assessment → rate hold vs hike decision. In June's dot plot, nine FOMC members projected a September rate hike; that projection was made with oil at approximately $76–77. With WTI now at $72.06 and the 60-day sanctions waiver providing supply certainty through August 21 — covering both the June and July CPI prints the FOMC will assess at September — the case for the nine-member hike projection holding has weakened substantially. UST 10Y at 4.50% (up 3 basis points on mild risk-on, not on rate-hike repricing) still has meaningful room to fall if the oil-to-CPI chain plays out as the forward price curve implies.

IAEA Director-General Rafael Grossi confirmed Wednesday that nuclear inspectors will visit Iran's bombed enrichment sites — "the accord says explicitly that the nuclear activities will be supervised by the IAEA... this is going to happen" — ending the two-day "war of words" between Trump's claim that Iran "fully and completely" agreed to inspections and Tehran's denial. Trump formally declared a ceasefire on Day 7, while Iran simultaneously tested the ceasefire by closing the Strait of Hormuz over renewed Israel-Hezbollah fighting in Lebanon, demonstrating that the broader Lebanon architecture is the deal's most immediate vulnerability with 53 days remaining in the 60-day MOU window.

For crypto, the ceasefire confirmation and IAEA clarity are medium-term positives through two channels. The direct channel: a ceasefire that holds means geopolitical risk premium — which had been partially unwound in Tuesday's sell — is partially restored as a support factor for BTC, which has historically carried a geopolitical hedge premium during active conflict resolution processes. The indirect channel: a deal that holds keeps WTI at $72 (rather than spiking on conflict escalation risk), which keeps the CPI disinflation chain intact, which keeps the FOMC's September hold case building. The Hormuz closure over Lebanon fighting is the risk: if Lebanon conflict draws Iran back into active hostility, oil could spike sharply and the rate disinflation narrative would reverse. Watch Lebanon ceasefire developments for this specific risk.

The Hormuz demining coordination mechanism agreed on Day 6 was specifically designed to prevent accidental escalation in the Strait; a deliberate closure tests whether Iran sees Hormuz as a bargaining chip in the broader Lebanon negotiation or is willing to keep it open while Lebanon remains unresolved. If Iran keeps Hormuz closures brief and tactical, the deal's architecture is intact. If closures become sustained or tied explicitly to Lebanon demands, the deal is structurally more fragile than Tuesday's ceasefire optimism reflected.
