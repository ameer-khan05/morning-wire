Bitcoin surged to $64,631 at 6:30 AM ET Wednesday — up $2,237 from Tuesday's $62,393, a 3.59% gain — and Ethereum rose to $1,874 (▲5.52%), outperforming Bitcoin for the session, CryptoNews reporting "Bitcoin and Ethereum Price Jump on Softer CPI and Japan Bitcoin ETF" and Fortune publishing the current Bitcoin price. The dual catalyst structure is notable: June CPI at 3.5% YoY and -0.4% month-on-month — below the 3.8% consensus and the largest monthly decline since April 2020 — removed the hawkish rate premium that Monday's Hormuz blockade had injected into macro risk pricing, cutting Fed July hike odds from 42% to 17% and pushing the 10-year Treasury yield down 6 basis points to 4.56%. Simultaneously, Japan's parliament cleared a committee vote in the Upper House to reclassify cryptocurrency as financial instruments under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act — the Crypto Times reporting Japan is "one vote from Bitcoin ETFs and a 20% crypto tax cap," with the Upper House committee passage of the FIEA amendment the last procedural hurdle before a full chamber vote that the ruling LDP's control of both houses makes a formality. US spot Ethereum ETFs added $58 million in net inflows, reversing the mixed trend from earlier in the month, and Morgan Stanley updated its ETH and Solana ETF filings naming Coinbase as custodian and staking provider, signaling continued institutional ETF development across the Ethereum ecosystem.

The CLARITY Act continues on its floor vote timeline for the week of July 20, with Thursday July 17 now identified by crypto.news as the "hearing that decides crypto's 2026" — a Senate Agriculture Committee hearing that crypto industry analysts described as the last substantive opportunity to shape the merged bill's text before floor action. CoinDesk reported that lawmakers may drop a new version of the bill as early as next week, reconciling the Senate Banking and Agriculture Committee texts. Prediction market odds of 2026 passage sit at approximately 43% — near the coin-flip threshold that reflects genuine uncertainty about whether the three unresolved disputes (ethics disclosures around Trump's cryptocurrency income, law enforcement access under Section 604, and stablecoin yield authorization) can be reconciled in a merged bill that attracts seven Senate Democrats. ETH's outperformance of BTC on Wednesday — 5.52% vs. 3.59% — partially reflects the stablecoin yield question: if the merged CLARITY Act includes yield authorization, ETH infrastructure demand benefits directly from DeFi growth, and the ETH ETF inflows suggest institutional positioning for that outcome ahead of the July 17 hearing.

Japan's parliament cleared a committee vote in the Upper House to reclassify cryptocurrency as financial instruments under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act — Crypto Times reporting Japan is "one vote from Bitcoin ETFs and a 20% crypto tax cap" and Crypto Briefing confirming Japan "advances bill to legalize Bitcoin ETFs, cut crypto taxes" — with the FIEA amendment targeting spot Bitcoin ETF availability on the Tokyo Stock Exchange by fiscal 2027 and a separate 20% flat tax on crypto gains replacing the current 55% maximum rate in 2028, the full Upper House floor vote widely regarded as a formality given Japan's ruling Liberal Democratic Party's control of both chambers, making the committee passage the last substantive procedural obstacle to Japan's most consequential crypto regulatory reform.

The Japan development matters at a different scale than a price catalyst. Japan's household savings market holds approximately $17 trillion. The current 55% maximum tax rate on crypto gains — treated as miscellaneous income under Japanese tax law — has been the primary structural barrier to retail crypto investment in Japan's massive savings market. A 20% flat tax replaces that barrier with a rate competitive with equity investment tax treatment. Spot Bitcoin ETFs on the Tokyo Stock Exchange give Japanese institutional asset managers and retail investors a compliant access vehicle for BTC exposure that meets domestic fiduciary and regulatory standards. Together, the two changes — compliant ETF access and competitive tax treatment — open Japan's household savings market to Bitcoin in a way that has not previously been available. The global significance: Japan's regulatory advance, concurrent with the US CLARITY Act's floor vote timeline, represents the simultaneous move of two of the world's largest and most developed financial markets toward crypto regulatory frameworks that support institutional adoption.

Quick hits

  • Japan's 55%-to-20% crypto tax reduction deserves to be examined in the context of what it does to the return math for Japanese retail investors. At a 55% maximum tax rate, a Japanese investor who bought Bitcoin at $20,000 and sold at $64,631 would owe roughly 55% of $44,631 in gains — approximately $24,547 per coin in taxes, leaving a net gain of about $20,084. At a 20% flat rate, the same investor owes $8,926 in taxes and nets $35,705. That is a 77% increase in net return on the same investment. No change in the underlying asset is required — just a tax rate change. For a country with $17 trillion in household savings currently earning near-zero returns in bank deposits, the combination of compliant ETF access and competitive tax treatment creates a structurally new investment alternative for millions of Japanese savers. The policy change is straightforwardly the most significant demand unlock for Bitcoin in any single developed-economy market since the US spot ETF approvals in January 2024.
  • The CLARITY Act's prediction market odds at 43% should be read carefully. A 43% probability is not "unlikely to pass" — it is "genuinely uncertain," which is a fundamentally different reading. In legislative prediction markets, a 43% probability reflects near-even odds among sophisticated participants with access to Senate vote-count information. The market is not saying the bill is likely to fail; it is saying the bill's outcome depends on the resolution of disputes whose resolution is genuinely unknowable before the merged bill drops next week. If the stablecoin yield provision is stripped, Democratic vote-count improves materially but DeFi's most significant growth catalyst is removed; if it is included, DeFi gets its catalyst but Democratic support becomes more fragile. The market is pricing the uncertainty of that binary, not the probability of failure. At 43%, the CLARITY Act is closer to passage than its eight-year legislative history might suggest — and much of what happens in the next seven days determines which side of 50% it ends up on.
