CLARITY Act Polymarket odds dropped 5 points to 38% Thursday as Senate Democrats publicly criticized the specific ethics language in the latest draft — creating a gap between the White House's acceptance of "the ethics provision" in principle on Tuesday and the text as drafted, which Murphy, Van Hollen, and Merkley apparently find narrower than the commitment they were given. 15 days remain to the August 7 Senate recess. The July 28 week is the primary remaining floor window after the current week.

The CLARITY language dispute is a second-order political problem compared to the original first-order ethics dispute. First-order: does any ethics provision exist in the bill? Resolved Tuesday — yes. Second-order: does the specific text of the provision match the Democratic Senators' definition of "the ethics provision" as they described it publicly? That is Thursday's question. The resolution space is narrow: the draft text either needs amending to match the Senators' demand, or the Senators need to accept that the text they received matches the commitment. Neither is impossible within 15 days. The 5-point odds move reflects uncertainty about which happens first — language amendment or Senate floor scheduling — rather than a judgment that passage has become unlikely on the merits.

The behavioral read on six consecutive ETF inflow days into a declining spot price is straightforward: institutions are separating the passage thesis from the current political noise. The CLARITY language dispute is a text-drafting problem that requires one of two outcomes to resolve — language amendment matching Democratic demand, or Democratic acceptance of the draft text. Institutions pricing through both variables simultaneously, accumulating rather than reducing, is the behavioral definition of conviction in the underlying thesis at a better entry price. The January 2024 ETF approval period showed this dynamic: institutional inflows preceded the appreciation cycle by 2–3 weeks of price-dip accumulation.
