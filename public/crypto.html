The psychological difference between "BTC broke when Hyperliquid unlocked" and "BTC held flat through the Hyperliquid unlock" is the difference between a market that is visibly fragile and one that has demonstrated demand depth. Rain unlock recipients making sell-or-hold decisions over the next five days are looking at a market that absorbed $630 million in supply without flinching. The Hyperliquid outcome provides favorable context: a market that absorbed the first unlock without a price break is psychologically different for Rain unlock recipients making hold-or-sell decisions than a market that broke on the first supply event. Bitcoin ETF June outflows of $4.5 billion — the worst month since the products launched in January 2024 — remain the structural bearish counterweight to whale accumulation at the current $60,000–$63,000 range, representing institutional loss-cutting at a level $27,000–$37,000 below their cost basis.

The July 31 FOMC meeting is the next formal catalyst capable of changing the ETF flow picture. The June NFP miss (57,000 vs 115,000 consensus) firmed September rate-hold expectations; a July 31 statement that explicitly confirms a September hold would give the institutional community its first formal basis to reclassify Bitcoin ETF positions from a loss-cutting scenario to a rate-hedge scenario. The practical effect of that reclassification — if it happens — is a reversal of the June outflow pattern: institutions that were selling at $60,000–$63,000 to cut losses would shift to holding (or adding) at the same prices as a rate-sensitive asset whose forward thesis has been confirmed.
