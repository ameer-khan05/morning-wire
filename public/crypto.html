The Anchor

Strategy holds more Bitcoin than BlackRock — and Capital Group just spent $747 million on MSTR shares.

Strategy officially surpassed BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust to become the largest corporate holder of Bitcoin in the world, with 815,061 BTC — ahead of BlackRock by more than 12,000 tokens. The milestone lands alongside one of the largest single institutional purchases of MSTR shares ever recorded: Capital Group's American Funds Fundamental Investors acquired 4.32 million MSTR shares for approximately $747 million, a transaction that drove Strategy shares up 9.4% in Thursday's session. Combined, the two developments represent the clearest institutional validation of Strategy's corporate Bitcoin treasury model since Michael Saylor first pivoted the company in 2020. Strategy holds more than 3.8% of all Bitcoin that will ever exist.

Bitcoin itself is recovering Friday morning after a difficult Thursday session. BTC fell 0.7% after failing to break the $80,000 resistance level — a technical threshold where a cluster of short-term holder cost bases and open short positions sits — as oil's surge past $100 a barrel pulled risk assets broadly lower. Friday morning, BTC has rebounded to $78,553, up 3.75%. ETH is at $2,395, up 3.47%. Analysts at Bernstein maintain their $150,000 year-end Bitcoin target, describing the recent volatility as a "confidence shock rather than structural damage." Cantor Fitzgerald separately raised its BTC price target to $212,000, citing institutional accumulation momentum and ETF inflow trends. The "most hated rally" framing refers to the persistent bearish positioning in derivatives markets: a sustained break above $80,000 would force a painful short squeeze.

Regulation · CLARITY Markup Senate Banking · late April · digital assets

CLARITY Act Senate Banking Committee markup still on track for late April — window closing fast.

Senate Banking Committee Chair Tim Scott is expected to hold a markup session for the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act before the end of April. Senator Lummis has warned that missing this window could effectively delay comprehensive digital-asset market structure legislation until 2030. The bill resolves stablecoin yield ambiguity created by the GENIUS Act's Section 4(c) and would create the most comprehensive US digital-asset regulatory framework for DeFi protocols and trading platforms. If the bill does not clear committee this month, the probability of 2026 passage drops sharply as the congressional calendar fills.

GENIUS Act · July 18 85 days Stablecoin regs · compliance clock

85 days to GENIUS Act implementing regulations deadline — stablecoin issuers in active compliance mode.

Federal regulators must issue stablecoin implementing regulations under the GENIUS Act no later than July 18, 2026 — 85 days from today — mandating 1:1 reserve backing in high-quality liquid assets, monthly public attestations, and annual audits for payment stablecoin issuers. Tether's most recent quarterly attestation showed $128 billion in US T-bill holdings. Circle and other issuers have already begun restructuring product offerings and reserve management practices. The incoming regulations will determine whether yield-bearing stablecoin products — a $40B+ market — survive the new framework or are classified as securities.
