Strategy bets $2.54 billion on Bitcoin. Kelp hackers go dark.

Michael Saylor's Strategy made the largest single corporate Bitcoin purchase since 2024 — 34,164 BTC at $74,395 average, pushing total holdings to 815,061 BTC. BTC recovered above $75,000 as peace-talk optimism eased risk-off pressure. Kelp DAO's $290M haul began moving through privacy protocols. And Kraken's parent closed a $550M derivatives deal.

At a glance · 08:00 ET $2.54B Strategy · largest Bitcoin buy since 2024
The Anchor

Strategy drops $2.54 billion on Bitcoin in the market's most volatile week.

On Monday, as markets processed the Kelp DAO exploit fallout and oil's 6.5% surge, Strategy executed its largest Bitcoin purchase in more than a year: 34,164 BTC acquired at an average price of $74,395 per coin, for a total outlay of approximately $2.54 billion. The firm's total Bitcoin holdings now stand at 815,061 BTC — acquired over time for $61.56 billion at an all-in average of $75,527 per coin. At current prices, Strategy holds the largest single institutional Bitcoin position in the world by a substantial margin, representing more than 3.8% of all Bitcoin that will ever exist.

The purchase is not surprising in isolation — Strategy has been accumulating steadily through every major correction — but its timing and magnitude carry a signal. Saylor bought aggressively into Monday's fear peak, with BTC sitting at $74,335 as the Kelp hack aftermath and Hormuz closure dragged the market red. By Tuesday morning, BTC had recovered to $75,450, up 1.5%. Bitcoin dominance eased slightly from 59.2% to 58.8% as ETH and select altcoins outperformed on the rebound. Meanwhile, Kelp DAO's hackers began moving the $290 million in stolen rsETH across chains using privacy protocols — a predictable but concerning development given the scale of the laundering task ahead of them.

Strategy · BTC $2.54B 34,164 BTC · Monday buy

Strategy buys 34,164 BTC for $2.54B — the largest corporate purchase in over a year.

Executed at an average of $74,395 per coin on Monday, April 20, the purchase pushed Strategy's total holdings to 815,061 BTC and its cumulative acquisition cost to $61.56 billion. The company continues to fund purchases through its STRC preferred stock program and has no public ceiling on accumulation. Saylor has described 1 million BTC as a long-term reference point.

Source: CoinAlertNews · CoinDesk
BTC Recovery $75,450 Tuesday morning · peace talk lift

Bitcoin recovers above $75,000 as peace-talk optimism eases risk-off pressure.

BTC climbed from Monday's close of $74,335 to $75,450 on Tuesday morning — up 1.5% — as reports that Vice President Vance was flying to Pakistan for Iran talks lifted the geopolitical risk premium across assets. ETH outperformed at $2,431, up 3.0% on the day. Bitcoin dominance ticked down from 59.2% to 58.8% as altcoins participated in the recovery more broadly.

Source: Yahoo Finance
Kelp DAO · Laundering $290M Cross-chain · privacy protocols

Kelp DAO hackers begin moving $290M across chains using privacy tools.

The Lazarus Group's stolen rsETH began flowing through privacy protocols and cross-chain bridges on Tuesday, following the established North Korean playbook of rapid fragmentation to complicate chain analysis. Kelp's security council has frozen 30,766 ETH in an intermediary wallet accessible only via further governance action. rsETH remains depegged. On-chain investigators estimated the laundering operation will take weeks to complete given the volume.

Source: Investing News Network · CoinDesk
Acquisition · Derivatives $550M Payward · Bitnomial · CFTC stack

Kraken parent Payward acquires Bitnomial for $550M, securing full CFTC derivatives stack.

Announced April 17, Payward's cash-and-stock acquisition of Bitnomial — the first crypto-native exchange to hold all three CFTC-issued licenses (exchange, clearinghouse, brokerage) — values Payward's total equity at $20 billion. Initial offerings will include spot margin, perpetual futures, and options for US clients under CFTC oversight. The deal creates the most comprehensive regulated US crypto derivatives platform available.

Source: CoinDesk · Kraken Blog
April · Hack Tally $606M Month total · legislative pressure

April's $606M hack toll fuels CLARITY Act bridge-security push.

The Kelp DAO exploit ($292M, April 19) and Drift protocol drain ($285M, April 1, attributed to Lazarus Group) have pushed April's crypto theft total to $606 million — nearly 4× Q1's entire figure. Senate Banking staff are now receiving bipartisan requests to attach bridge-security minimum standards to the CLARITY Act stablecoin legislation during markup, with the Kelp hack providing fresh urgency.

Source: BeInCrypto
Regulation · EU MiCA Phase-2 · Germany · France

MiCA Phase-2 reporting requirements active in Germany and France.

The second phase of the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation went live in Germany and France on Monday, adding new reporting obligations for large exchanges and custodians. Major platforms including Coinbase EU, Binance, and Bitstamp have already updated their compliance frameworks in anticipation. The requirements represent the most comprehensive crypto regulation yet in force in any major economy.
