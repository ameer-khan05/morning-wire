Bitcoin climbed to $66,433 Tuesday — its highest level in two weeks — as the confirmed Iran-US MOU terms included direct US sanctions relief on Iranian oil exports, adding a macro tailwind beyond the Hormuz reopening itself. Ethereum reached $1,792, up 3.94% from Monday, extending its outperformance in the risk-on recovery. The June 19 Geneva signing is the next confirmation catalyst; Standard Chartered's $59,000 cycle-low call is confirmed in progress.

The MOU's sanctions relief language is new information for crypto markets. What was initially described as a ceasefire-and-Hormuz-reopening framework has been confirmed to include a direct US sanctions waiver on Iranian oil exports and the unfreezing of billions in Iranian assets — terms that expand the oil supply picture beyond Hormuz reopening alone. For crypto, this matters through the rate-cut repricing channel: more Iranian oil flowing at lower prices means a deeper CPI passthrough, which means the Fed's cut path clears faster, which means real rates decline further than the initial Hormuz-only scenario implied. BTC and ETH are pricing Tuesday's MOU confirmation as a secondary tailwind on top of Monday's primary Iran deal resolution move. The recovery from the $59,000 cycle low — called by Standard Chartered on Saturday before the deal announcement — is tracking exactly as the institutional macro playbook predicted: BTC first, then ETH outperforming in the confirmation stages, then broader risk assets joining as the resolution trade extends. The June 19 Geneva signing is the next event-risk moment: formal treaty signature produces the secondary risk-on move that analysts have consistently flagged as the second act of the two-stage resolution trade. BTC at $66,433 is 10.7% below its May 31 peak of $74,054. The path to that peak is clear; the only question is pace.

The Anchor

BTC at $66,433. ETH at $1,792. Both at two-week highs. The MOU added sanctions relief on Iranian oil to the macro tailwind. The recovery toward $74,054 has a second engine now — and the Geneva signing on Thursday is the next event that moves prices.

Tuesday's MOU confirmation is the second act of Monday's risk-on move in crypto. When Trump announced the deal Sunday night, BTC moved from $64,385 to $66,157 (+2.75%) as the geopolitical risk premium began unwinding. On Tuesday, the confirmed MOU terms — particularly the direct sanctions relief on Iranian oil — added a second layer of macro tailwind: if Iranian oil exports resume under a sanctions waiver, that's additional downward pressure on WTI beyond the Hormuz reopening supply surge. More oil at lower prices means a faster CPI passthrough, a clearer Fed cut path, and declining real rates. BTC and ETH benefit from all three through the standard institutional macro channel: as real rates fall, non-yielding crypto assets become relatively more attractive than fixed income, and institutional allocators who reduced positions during the 13-session ETF outflow streak find the cost of re-entry lower relative to missing the recovery.

ETH's continued outperformance — +3.94% from Monday's close of $1,724 to Tuesday's $1,792 — is consistent with the historical pattern in macro risk-on recoveries. In a two-stage resolution trade (announcement + confirmation), ETH outperforms BTC in both stages as higher-beta assets capture more of the recovery upside relative to their compressed starting levels. ETH entered the recovery at a deeper discount to its pre-conflict level than BTC, creating a larger compressed spring. At $1,792, ETH is still roughly 14% below its pre-conflict level; BTC at $66,433 is 10.7% below its May 31 peak. ETH has more recovery runway on a percentage basis, and the institutional pattern in prior recoveries has the gap narrowing over the 2-4 weeks following a macro resolution event of this magnitude.

Quick hits

  • The MOU's frozen asset release — confirmed as part of the deal terms — has a secondary crypto effect that markets have not yet fully priced. Iran's frozen assets, once released, improve Iran's fiscal position and reduce the domestic pressure on the Iranian government to maintain aggressive policies that increase geopolitical risk premiums globally. A more fiscally stable Iranian government is less likely to take actions that re-inject geopolitical uncertainty into oil markets, making the Iran deal's macro de-risking more durable than a ceasefire alone would provide. BTC and ETH are pricing the deal, but the durable risk-premium reduction that follows a genuine fiscal normalization in Iran is a slow-moving tailwind that extends the recovery thesis beyond the initial signing reaction.
  • The European Hormuz mission — Germany, France, Britain, and Italy pledging a "purely defensive" mine-clearance and escort operation — has a direct crypto implication: it means the physical reopening of the strait is backed by Western military logistics, not just US-Iranian agreement. A strait that is reopening with Western escort is more likely to stay open than one that depends solely on the bilateral agreement's durability. For crypto markets, which have been pricing Hormuz reopening as the key supply-normalization event, European military backing of the clearance operation de-risks the reopening process and makes the oil-decline-to-rate-cut chain more likely to materialize on schedule.
  • The Bitcoin ETF re-entry thesis is the single most actionable near-term market development for institutional crypto. The 13-session, $4.4 billion outflow streak that characterized the two weeks before the Iran deal ended as the macro headwinds resolved. Institutional allocators who reduced BTC ETF positions — many citing Iran uncertainty, SPCX rotation, or de-risking ahead of geopolitical events — are now positioned in front of a confirmed MOU with sanctions relief, a June 19 formal signing, a European Hormuz mission, and a Fed cut path clearing on lower oil. The aggregate BTC ETF inflow number in the 5 sessions following the Geneva signing will be the most direct confirmation of whether the institutional re-entry hypothesis is playing out at the scale the macro backdrop implies.
