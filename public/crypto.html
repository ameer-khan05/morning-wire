BTC's recovery from its Wednesday low to $63,005 on a single Trump statement illustrates the nature of this week's crypto price action: it is not driven by crypto-specific fundamentals but by geopolitical risk-on/risk-off oscillation that crypto tracks alongside equities. The rate-cut support from weak US jobs data — which provided the floor that prevented a deeper sell-off on Wednesday — remains intact; the oil-inflation complication that the US-Iran exchange introduced also remains intact. What changed on Thursday morning is that Trump introduced a deal signal that markets interpreted as reducing tail-risk probability. BTC at $63,005 is the market pricing a slightly lower probability of sustained conflict than Wednesday's $62,029 priced. The actual Iran situation — US and Iranian forces both conducting strikes, Gulf Arab states now targeted — has not materially improved. Rain's $812 million token unlock arrives July 11 into a market where the fundamental backdrop changes every 12 hours.

Bitcoin recovered to $63,005 (▲1.75%) on Thursday as President Trump's statement that Iran had called him and "wants to make a deal so badly" produced a rapid risk-on response across assets — with US equity futures turning green, the Nasdaq-100 futures climbing 0.5%, and Bitcoin recovering the $976 decline it had suffered on Wednesday when the US-Iran military exchange had pushed it below the analyst July floor of $62,939 for the first time this month. Crypto News reported that Bitcoin "bounced sharply from the lows" and that by Thursday morning "the fear had mostly disappeared" as Trump's deal signal overrode the Iran escalation narrative. The global crypto market capitalization, which had declined 2.1% to $2.21 trillion at its session low, stabilized at that level. The recovery occurred even as the US and Iran were both actively conducting military strikes and Iran had targeted Gulf Arab states for the first time.

BTC's recovery mechanism on Thursday is worth isolating: a single presidential statement produced a hundreds-of-dollar move in minutes, recovering most of a full session's decline. This is not how crypto traded two years ago, when price action was more driven by on-chain flows and crypto-specific catalysts. BTC in mid-2026 is tracking geopolitical risk probability with the speed and sensitivity of a macro risk asset — which means it recovers on deal signals and declines on strike reports with the same immediacy that oil does. The implication for the FOMC July 31 resolution event: if Iran and the US de-escalate before July 31, BTC's floor will have been confirmed and the rate-cut support thesis will be the dominant driver. If they don't, BTC is pricing deal probability that may not materialize.

Ethereum declined to $1,706 (▼1.60%) on Thursday despite Bitcoin's recovery on Trump's Iran deal signal — an unusual BTC-ETH divergence in a macro-driven session where both assets typically track together — with the critical $1,716 daily S1 pivot support tested for a second consecutive session without breaking, while the 200-hour EMA at $1,723 has shifted from intraday support to resistance. Rain's $812 million token unlock arrives on July 11 — two days away — into a market context that has produced more macro volatility in 48 hours than the previous two weeks combined: the same window in which Rain recipients must decide whether to hold or sell has seen a US-Iran military exchange, Iranian strikes on Gulf Arab states, a Trump "ceasefire over" declaration, a Trump "deal" signal, and Bitcoin oscillating through a range of nearly $1,000.

ETH's failure to recover alongside BTC on the Trump deal signal is the most technically significant development in Thursday's crypto session. When BTC and ETH diverge during a macro event that should affect both equally, it typically means ETH faces an additional protocol-level or market-structure headwind that is not driven by the geopolitical narrative. Possible explanations include DeFi position unwinding that is creating forced selling, a specific Ethereum protocol event not yet publicly disclosed, or simply that institutional geopolitical-proxy buyers are defaulting to BTC rather than ETH when adding risk-on exposure rapidly. The $1,716 support surviving two tests this week is the technical positive; ETH's refusal to recover alongside BTC is the structural concern. Rain's supply event landing into this specific context — where even the recovery is ETH-specific bearish — is the supply calendar story of the week.

The FOMC on July 31 remains the structural resolution event: labor market weakness supporting rate cuts (BTC-positive) is competing against oil's inflation channel (BTC-headwind) with the 10-year Treasury yield now at 4.52% after a 7-basis-point rise on Thursday.

The stablecoin legislation story is moving on its own timeline independent of the Iran crisis and the crypto price action. The structural regulatory maturation thesis — that crypto is entering a period of legal definition rather than legal ambiguity — is advancing through the geopolitical volatility rather than being delayed by it. The geopolitical headlines are noise; the regulatory architecture is the signal that will matter in six months.
