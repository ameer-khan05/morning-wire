BTC spot ETFs log $2.1 billion in 8 straight days of inflows — cumulative total reaches $58 billion since launch.

US spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded eight consecutive days of net inflows totaling $2.1 billion through April 23 — the strongest sustained run since January 2026's post-halving rally. Cumulative net inflows since all spot BTC ETFs launched have now reached $58 billion. The streak reflects a shift in institutional behavior: ETF inflows are no longer correlated with price rallies in a simple way — money has continued flowing in even as BTC consolidated below $80,000, indicating strategic accumulation rather than momentum chasing. Short-term holders, per CoinDesk, have been quietly selling into ETF buying — a distribution pattern that has historically preceded significant upward price moves as supply shifts to longer-duration holders.

Morgan Stanley · MSBT $100M+ First week · 0.14% fee · first major-bank spot ETF

Morgan Stanley's MSBT crosses $100M in first week — the cheapest spot BTC ETF on the market at 0.14%.

Morgan Stanley's Bitcoin Trust (MSBT), launched April 8, surpassed $100 million in net inflows in its first week of trading — the top 1% of ETF launch performance by any measure, per analyst benchmarks. MSBT charges 0.14% per year, the lowest annual fee of any spot BTC ETF, undercutting the field on cost while matching it on structure. The launch made Morgan Stanley the first major US commercial bank to issue a spot Bitcoin ETF under its own name — a watershed in institutional crypto normalization that signals even the most conservative Wall Street institutions now treat Bitcoin as a core allocatable asset. MSBT pulled in $34 million on day one and an average of $14 million per day for the remainder of its first week.

BlackRock and Morgan Stanley add $34M in BTC ETF exposure Sunday — weekend accumulation continues.

BlackRock and Morgan Stanley together added a combined $34 million in fresh Bitcoin ETF exposure on Sunday April 26, according to The Market Periodical — underscoring that institutional BTC accumulation is no longer pausing for weekends or market closures. BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT) and Morgan Stanley's MSBT remain the two largest and fastest-growing US spot BTC ETF vehicles. Combined, they account for more than 60% of total spot BTC ETF assets under management. Sunday activity from major institutions in crypto ETFs is noteworthy — the market structure that Bitcoin's 24/7 trading enables is increasingly being exploited by institutional desks that previously sat out weekend sessions.

Regulation · CLARITY Days away Senate markup · Lummis warning · 2030 risk

CLARITY Act Senate markup window now days away — Lummis: missing it delays crypto legislation to 2030.

Senate Banking Committee Chair Tim Scott is expected to hold a markup session for the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act before the end of April — a window that is now days away. Senator Lummis has publicly warned that missing this deadline could push comprehensive US digital-asset market-structure legislation to 2030 as the congressional calendar fills. The CLARITY Act would resolve stablecoin yield ambiguity from the GENIUS Act's Section 4(c), establish a DeFi regulatory framework, and clarify exchange licensing. If it clears committee this month, Senate floor consideration before the summer recess becomes possible. The markup, if it occurs, would be the first Senate committee action on crypto market structure in 2026.

GENIUS Act · Day 82 82 days Stablecoin regs · July 18 · Tether $150B

GENIUS Act stablecoin regulations due in 82 days as Tether's USDT supply nears $150 billion.

Federal regulators must issue stablecoin implementing regulations under the GENIUS Act no later than July 18, 2026 — 82 days from Sunday. Tether's USDT supply has grown to nearly $150 billion — up $5 billion in recent weeks — providing the liquidity fuel behind the broader crypto market rebound. The GENIUS Act framework mandates 1:1 reserve backing, monthly public attestations, and annual audits for payment stablecoin issuers. The central unresolved question: whether yield-bearing stablecoin products — a $40+ billion market — survive the framework or are classified as securities. If the CLARITY Act markup passes this week, the two legislative tracks could converge into a single coherent US digital-asset regulatory framework before the July deadline.
