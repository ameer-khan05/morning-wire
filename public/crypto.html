The Anchor

Bitcoin tops $78,000 — an 11-week high — as Trump's open-ended ceasefire fuels risk rally.

Wednesday morning's session opened with Bitcoin at $77,800 and climbing. By 7:30 AM ET it had cleared $78,000 — the highest level since early February. The catalyst: Trump's late Tuesday announcement extending the US-Iran ceasefire indefinitely, removing the Wednesday deadline markets had been hedging against for days. The move was unambiguously risk-on. Equity futures added 0.5–0.7%. Oil held elevated near $90 — the blockade premium isn't going anywhere — but for crypto, the directional read was clear. Bitcoin is up 4.3% on the week, wiping out all of the weekend's Kelp-hack-driven losses and adding to the recovery that began when Strategy executed its $2.54 billion buy on Monday.

The rally is not purely a geopolitical trade. Weekly inflows to global crypto investment funds hit $1.4 billion, according to data compiled by Bloomberg — led by Bitcoin and Ethereum, with the latter up nearly 1% on the day. Strategy's Monday purchase of 34,164 BTC at a $74,395 average continues to act as a psychological floor; the position is now modestly in profit at current prices. The next catalysts: the CLARITY Act markup in the Senate Banking Committee, expected in late April, which could create the most comprehensive US digital-asset regulatory framework outside stablecoins; and Bitcoin's technical chart, where $80,000 is the key resistance level analysts are watching.

BTC · 11-week high $78K Ceasefire extension · risk rally

Bitcoin tops $78,000 on Trump's open-ended ceasefire extension — highest since early February.

BTC cleared $78,000 Wednesday morning in a move analysts described as a relief rally, not a fundamental repricing. The ceasefire extension removed the most acute near-term geopolitical risk. Bitcoin is up 4.3% on the week, and the $80,000 resistance level — marked by a cluster of short-term holder cost bases — is now the next technical target. Analysts note that a sustained rally above $80K would trigger significant short liquidations.

Inflows · Week $1.4B Global crypto funds · BTC + ETH led

$1.4 billion flows into global crypto funds this week — Bitcoin and Ethereum lead.

Weekly inflows to global crypto investment funds hit $1.4 billion, according to Bloomberg data, reflecting strong institutional demand that preceded the ceasefire extension rally. The inflows were led by spot Bitcoin ETF products and Ethereum funds. Japanese institutional investors have been among the most active new buyers of Bitcoin ETF exposure in recent weeks. BTC.D eased to 58.2% as altcoins outperformed in the risk-on move.

Source: Bloomberg · CoinDesk
Regulation · US CLARITY Senate Banking · markup · late April

CLARITY Act digital-asset market structure bill advances to Senate Banking Committee markup in late April.

Senate Banking Committee Chair Tim Scott is expected to hold a markup session for the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act in the latter half of April. Senator Lummis has warned that missing this window could push the legislation to 2030+. The bill resolves stablecoin yield ambiguity created by the GENIUS Act's Section 4(c) and would create the most comprehensive US digital-asset regulatory framework for DeFi and trading platforms. If it does not clear committee this month, the probability of 2026 passage drops sharply.

Source: Gate Blog · CoinDesk Policy
Kelp DAO · Recovery Vote live Governance · rsETH · frozen ETH

Kelp DAO recovery governance vote goes live — rsETH stabilizing as frozen ETH path clarifies.

Kelp DAO launched its recovery governance vote Wednesday, giving token holders a say in how to handle the 30,766 ETH frozen in an intermediary wallet following the $292M Lazarus Group exploit last weekend. rsETH has been trading closer to its intended peg as the governance process provides a structural path to recovery. The Kelp DAO security council suspended all new deposits pending the vote outcome. On-chain investigators say the $290M in stolen funds continues moving through privacy protocols.

Source: CoinDesk · Investing News Network
Strategy · BTC Stack 815,061 BTC held · $2.54B buy now in profit

Strategy's $2.54B Monday buy is now in profit as BTC climbs past its $74,395 average cost.

Strategy's 34,164 BTC acquisition at a $74,395 average — the largest corporate Bitcoin buy in 18 months — is now showing an unrealized gain as BTC trades above $77,000. Total holdings stand at 815,061 BTC, acquired for $61.56B at an all-in average of $75,527 per coin. The position represents more than 3.8% of all Bitcoin that will ever exist. Saylor has described 1 million BTC as a long-term reference point; the firm continues funding purchases through its STRC preferred-stock program.

Source: CoinAlertNews · CoinDesk
GENIUS Act · Phase 1 July 18 Implementing regs · stablecoin framework

GENIUS Act Phase 1 implementing regulations due July 18 — stablecoin compliance clock running.

The GENIUS Act, signed in July 2025, requires federal regulators to issue implementing stablecoin regulations no later than July 18, 2026 — the one-year anniversary of its passage. The law mandates 1:1 reserve backing in high-quality liquid assets, monthly public attestations, and annual audits for payment stablecoin issuers. Tether's Q1 attestation showed $128B in T-bill holdings. Circle and other issuers have already begun restructuring products ahead of the deadline.
