The Anchor

Bitcoin de-levers after the $80,000 failure — open interest drops 6% as the market resets for the next attempt.

Bitcoin entered the weekend at $77,510, down 0.57% in 24 hours, in what derivatives data describes as an orderly leverage unwind rather than a capitulation. Futures open interest fell more than 6% from approximately 792,000 BTC to 744,300 BTC in 24 hours, as the market flushed out positions accumulated during last week's run toward $80,000. Funding rates, which had been mildly positive throughout the rally, have moved toward neutral — a reset that analysts say improves the technical setup for a subsequent test of the $80,000 resistance. The "most hated rally" framing from earlier in the week remains in effect: bearish positioning in options markets is still elevated, meaning a sustained break above $80,000 would trigger short liquidations and amplify any move higher. For now, Bitcoin is on track for its best monthly performance in a year, and $5 billion in USDT stablecoin growth has fueled the broader crypto rebound.

BTC dominance rose to 60.14% as altcoins underperformed across the board Saturday morning: SOL is at $151.20 (▼1.43%), XRP at $2.18 (▼1.36%), and DOGE at $0.180 (▼1.64%). Ethereum, the second-largest asset, is nearly flat at $2,316.31 (▲0.06%). On the equity side, Strategy (MSTR) added 1.15% to $475.20 and Coinbase (COIN) gained 0.95% to $306.40 on Friday's broader tech rally — despite BTC itself softening. Strategy holds 815,061 BTC; the company remains the world's largest corporate Bitcoin holder, ahead of BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust by more than 12,000 coins. Cantor Fitzgerald's $212,000 BTC price target and Bernstein's $150,000 year-end call remain unchanged.

Regulation · CLARITY Late Apr Senate markup · window closing · Lummis · Scott

CLARITY Act Senate markup window closing — Lummis warns delay pushes comprehensive crypto law to 2030.

Senate Banking Committee Chair Tim Scott is expected to hold a markup session for the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act before the end of April — a window that is now days away. Senator Lummis has publicly warned that missing this markup deadline could effectively delay comprehensive US digital-asset market-structure legislation until 2030, as the congressional calendar fills with other priorities. The bill would resolve stablecoin yield ambiguity from the GENIUS Act's Section 4(c), establish a regulatory framework for DeFi protocols, and clarify exchange licensing requirements. If it clears committee this month, Senate floor consideration becomes possible before the summer recess.

GENIUS Act · Day 83 83 days Stablecoin regs · July 18 deadline

GENIUS Act stablecoin regulations due July 18 — 83 days to compliance deadline as issuers restructure.

Federal regulators must issue stablecoin implementing regulations under the GENIUS Act no later than July 18, 2026 — 83 days from Saturday. The rules mandate 1:1 reserve backing in high-quality liquid assets, monthly public attestations, and annual audits for payment stablecoin issuers. Tether's most recent quarterly attestation showed $128 billion in US T-bill holdings. Circle, Paxos, and other issuers have begun restructuring reserve management and product offerings. The central question: whether yield-bearing stablecoin products — a $40+ billion market — survive the framework or are classified as securities. If CLARITY Act markup passes late April, the two regulatory tracks could converge into a single coherent US digital-asset framework.
