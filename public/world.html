World · Deep Dive · Iran Day 23: US Bases Struck in Jordan + Bahrain — First Attacks Outside Iran · Russia Role Confirmed · Brent $98.44 Approaching $100 · Pakistan Asked to Mediate Again · Mojtaba Khamenei Still Not Appeared · 25 Days to Nuclear Deadline

Iranian forces struck US military installations at Al-Azraq base in Jordan and Shaikh Isa Air Base in Bahrain on Day 23 — the first Iranian military operations targeting US bases outside Iranian territory since the conflict began — as Russia's role helping Iran plan and carry out strikes on CIA facilities across the Middle East was publicly confirmed, sending Brent crude to $98.44 (▲3.11%) approaching $100; the Iranian military command simultaneously warned that if the United States attacks Iranian infrastructure — bridges, power plants — Iran will block all oil exports from West Asia and strike energy infrastructure across the region; Pakistan, which mediated the original US-Iran ceasefire before its July 7 collapse, is again being asked to play the same role; and new Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei has still not appeared publicly since succeeding his father, leaving the IRGC in operational control and the political decision point for ceasefire authorization unclear.

The strikes on Jordan and Bahrain are a qualitative escalation that changes the conflict's strategic calculus on both sides. Prior Iranian operations had targeted US-aligned forces and assets within the Iran-Gulf corridor; Jordan and Bahrain are US partner states whose security relationships have been stable for decades. The IRGC authorizing operations in both countries — one of which is the home of the US Fifth Fleet — signals either that the new Supreme Leader has approved a dramatically expanded target set, or that the IRGC is operating with autonomous authority that the succession vacuum has created. Russia's confirmed role makes the conflict bilateral (US-Iran) + trilateral (Russia implicated) simultaneously. Pakistan's second mediation attempt is the one proven diplomatic channel — it worked once, producing the July 4 ceasefire that held for three days before Iran attacked the commercial vessels that triggered the resumption of strikes on July 7. The question is whether any ceasefire framework can hold when both the IRGC and Russian support for Iran's military operations are active.
