The Anchor

The USS Spruance fires on an Iranian ship. The ceasefire collapses by Wednesday.

The Touska, a nearly 900-foot Iranian-flagged cargo vessel, was intercepted Sunday by the guided-missile destroyer USS Spruance in the Gulf of Oman after six hours of ignored warnings. US Central Command said the ship refused to stop; the Navy fired on its engine room, boarded it, and US Marines now hold the vessel. Trump announced the seizure on Truth Social, accusing Iran of "total violation" of the ceasefire by attempting to run the blockade.

Tehran's response was immediate and unambiguous. Iran's foreign ministry called the seizure "armed piracy" and said the Iranian military "will soon respond." The Strait of Hormuz, which had been commercially open for three days following the April 17 ceasefire, was shut again. Iranian military spokespeople said the closure would remain in effect as long as the US maintained its blockade of Iranian ports. Trump told Bloomberg the ceasefire expires Wednesday evening Eastern time and that an extension is "highly unlikely." Vice President Vance is traveling to Pakistan for a second round of talks Iran has not confirmed it will attend.

Ship Seizure Touska USS Spruance · Gulf of Oman

US Navy seizes Iranian cargo vessel Touska after six hours of warnings.

The guided-missile destroyer USS Spruance fired on the Touska's engine room on Sunday after the vessel ignored repeated commands to stop in the Gulf of Oman. US Marines boarded and secured the ship. Trump said the vessel was "defying the blockade" and represented a "total violation" of the ceasefire terms.

Source: NPR · Al Jazeera
Iran Response Piracy Tehran · vows retaliation

Iran calls seizure "armed piracy" and vows military retaliation.

Iran's military headquarters said the armed forces "will soon respond to this act of armed piracy and theft by the American military." The foreign ministry accused Washington of lacking "seriousness in pursuing a diplomatic process" and restricted Hormuz shipping, saying closure would remain while the US blockades Iranian ports.

Source: CNBC
Deadline Wednesday Ceasefire · Trump · expiry

Trump: ceasefire ends Wednesday, extension "highly unlikely."

The president told Bloomberg News on Monday that the 10-day ceasefire brokered April 17 expires Wednesday evening Eastern time and that extending it is "highly unlikely" given Iran's response to the Touska seizure. The window for a deal has narrowed to 48 hours.

Source: CNN · Time
Talks · Pakistan Vance Pakistan round · Iran absent?

JD Vance flies to Pakistan. Iran has not confirmed it will join.

Vice President Vance and top US officials departed for Islamabad for a second round of talks on the war, but Iran's foreign ministry confirmed "no decision" yet on participation. Iranian spokespersons said the seizure of the Touska makes talks "meaningless" without a prior acknowledgment from Washington that the boarding was illegal.

Source: NBC
IMF · Economy 3.1% 2026 global growth · revised down

IMF trims 2026 global growth to 3.1% as conflict reshapes supply chains.

The International Monetary Fund's April World Economic Outlook, published this week, revised down global growth to 3.1% for 2026 from a pre-conflict baseline of 3.4%. The fund projects global headline inflation will rise modestly before resuming decline in 2027, with energy prices and maritime insurance the primary transmission channels.

Source: IMF
Lebanon · Holds Day 4 Israel–Lebanon truce · still in effect

Israel–Lebanon ceasefire holds at Day 4 despite escalation elsewhere.

The 10-day truce along the Israel–Lebanon border, which took effect April 17, remained in effect Monday despite the US-Iran flareup. The UK and France, who co-chaired the April 17 summit, issued a joint statement calling for all parties to preserve the truce framework and allow the Pakistan talks to proceed.
