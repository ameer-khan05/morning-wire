The Anchor

The ceasefire extension lasted less than a day. Israel struck Lebanon Friday as Hezbollah called the deal worthless.

The three-week Israel-Lebanon ceasefire extension announced Thursday evening did not hold through Friday morning. Israel's Defense Forces carried out airstrikes on Hezbollah rocket-launcher sites in the southern Lebanese towns of Yater and Kafra, saying the sites "posed a threat to its troops and Israeli civilians." The strikes killed at least 6 people and injured 2 others. Hezbollah returned fire, targeting Israeli positions in the border area. Hezbollah lawmakers responded to the extension by calling it "meaningless in light of Israel's insistence on hostile acts, including assassinations, shelling, and gunfire." Netanyahu issued a parallel statement saying Israel was "maintaining full freedom of action against any threat," an assertion that directly contradicts the premise of a ceasefire agreement.

The broader conflict clock is at Day 57. On the Iran front, the US naval blockade of the Strait of Hormuz is tightening by design. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth said Friday that Tehran now faces a "stark choice" — accept a nuclear deal under US terms or face mounting economic collapse under sustained military and economic pressure. Iran's foreign ministry spokesperson confirmed there are no plans for a meeting between Iranian and US negotiators in Pakistan, shutting down the back-channel track that had been the only potential diplomatic bridge. Saudi Arabia's envoy remains in Tehran as the sole face-to-face diplomatic contact. The US-Iran ceasefire, technically in force since Day 1, is now described in Washington as a "pause, not a peace" — a formulation that creates little constraint on either side's behavior in the Strait.

Israel–Lebanon · Airstrikes 6 dead Yater · Kafra · ceasefire violated · Hezbollah fires

Israel airstrikes kill 6 in southern Lebanon Friday — ceasefire extension violated in under 24 hours.

Israeli forces carried out strikes on Hezbollah rocket-launcher sites in Yater and Kafra Friday, the Israel Defence Forces said, citing threats to troops and civilians. The strikes killed at least 6 people and injured 2. Hezbollah returned fire. The strikes began hours after President Trump announced the three-week ceasefire extension Thursday. Netanyahu's office issued a parallel statement: "Israel is maintaining full freedom of action against any threat" — directly contradicting any reading of the ceasefire as a prohibition on military action. Hezbollah lawmakers called the extension "meaningless" given Israel's continued military operations. The Washington Post and CFR both note the exchange marks the most serious breakdown in the ceasefire framework since it was signed.

Source: Al Jazeera · ANI News
US–Iran · Blockade Tightening Hegseth · "stark choice" · no talks planned

US naval blockade of Iran tightening "by the hour" — Hegseth says Tehran faces stark choice as no talks planned.

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth said Friday the US naval blockade of Iran is "tightening by the hour" and that Tehran faces a "stark choice" — accept a nuclear deal or face mounting economic collapse under sustained military and economic pressure. Iran's foreign ministry confirmed the same day that no meeting between Iranian and US negotiators is planned in Pakistan, closing the back-channel diplomatic track. Iran's parliamentary speaker maintained that the US blockade constitutes a ceasefire violation, conditioning any talks on its removal — a position the US categorically rejects. Al Jazeera's analysis asks how long Iran's economy can sustain the blockade; estimates range from months to over a year depending on sanctions workarounds.

Source: CNN · Al Jazeera
Hezbollah · Response "Meaningless" Ceasefire extension · Defiant · Washington Post

Hezbollah calls ceasefire extension "meaningless" — defiant posture tests Trump's three-week window.

Hezbollah's response to the three-week ceasefire extension was unequivocal: a Hezbollah lawmaker stated that the ceasefire "is meaningless in light of Israel's insistence on hostile acts, including assassinations, shelling, and gunfire." The Washington Post reports that Hezbollah is defiant in the face of the extension, with the group's military command framing Israel's Friday airstrikes as justification for continued retaliatory action. The three-week window Trump described as a "great chance" for a lasting peace deal now begins with both sides still exchanging fire, a structural problem the Trump administration has not publicly addressed. CFR notes the extension remains formally in place even as both parties violate it.

Source: Washington Post · CFR
Ukraine · Dnipropetrovsk Nearly free Oblast · spring offensive blunted · counter-push

Ukraine operations nearly fully liberate Dnipropetrovsk Oblast — Russia's anticipated spring offensive blunted.

Ukrainian forces have almost fully liberated Dnipropetrovsk Oblast through counter-attacks that have had a "meaningful strategic effect" — dislodging Russian forces across the southern front and disrupting preparations for Russia's anticipated spring offensive. Military analysts say the Ukrainian counter-push has blunted Russian momentum that had been building for weeks. The EU's $106 billion loan package approved Thursday is expected to reach Kyiv in coming weeks, providing the budgetary support Ukraine needs to sustain operations while the ceasefire in eastern Ukraine remains a stated objective of US and European diplomacy. The Dnipropetrovsk operation represents the most significant Ukrainian advance since the EU loan package was proposed.

Source: Saachi Baat · WFAE / NPR
Iran · Economic Pressure How long? Blockade survival · sanctions · IRGC oil workarounds

Al Jazeera: how long can Iran survive the US Hormuz blockade? Estimates range from months to over a year.

Al Jazeera published an analysis Friday examining Iran's economic resilience under the US naval blockade of the Strait of Hormuz. The assessment found that Iran has multiple partial workarounds: oil exports through non-Hormuz routes via Iraq and Turkey pipelines, sales to China under pre-existing contracts that the US has not sanctioned at the buyer level, and currency reserves built during the 2021–2024 period of elevated oil prices. Analysts estimate the Iranian economy can sustain the current pressure for anywhere from a few months to over a year before social and fiscal strain forces a policy shift. The Trump administration's calculation appears to be that Hegseth's "stark choice" framing will accelerate that timeline.

Source: Al Jazeera · CNN
Food Crisis · GRFC 2026 2× decade Acute hunger · conflict-driven · 59 countries

Global food crisis deepens: acute hunger has doubled in a decade, GRFC 2026 finds — conflict is the top driver.

The Global Report on Food Crises 2026 found that acute food insecurity and malnutrition levels remain "alarmingly high and deeply entrenched," with acute hunger having doubled over the past decade across 59 countries in crisis. The report identifies conflict — including the US-Iran and Russia-Ukraine wars — as the primary driver of acute food insecurity in 2026, ahead of economic shocks and climate extremes. The Strait of Hormuz crisis is directly affecting food supply chains to Yemen, Oman, and East Africa, regions that depend on Hormuz-routed grain and commodity shipments. The IMF's April WEO, which projects 3.1% global growth "in the shadow of war," identifies food insecurity as the conflict's most diffuse humanitarian consequence.
