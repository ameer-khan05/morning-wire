Day 53 of the US-Iran conflict. Vice President Vance and top US officials departed for Islamabad Tuesday morning — but Iranian state TV confirmed no Iranian delegation had left Tehran. Day 53 · Ceasefire Day 5 Wed Ceasefire expiry · window narrowing
The Anchor

The US team lands in Pakistan. Iran's seat is empty.

Vice President JD Vance departed Washington Tuesday morning for Islamabad alongside Special Envoy Steve Witkoff and senior administration officials — the US side of a second round of negotiations intended to produce a framework for extending or replacing the ceasefire before it expires Wednesday evening Eastern time. But Iranian state television, citing no diplomatic source, confirmed that no delegation had left Tehran as of Tuesday morning. Iran's foreign ministry said "no decision has been made" about attending, and spokespersons reiterated Tehran's precondition: Washington must acknowledge that the seizure of the cargo vessel Touska was illegal and release the ship before any substantive talks can begin.

The US categorically rejects that framing. CENTCOM maintains the Touska was violating the naval blockade under terms that Iran accepted when the ceasefire was signed April 17. With the two sides still deadlocked on the precondition and the clock at less than 48 hours, the UK and France have put forward a joint proposal for a 72-hour "technical extension" — a procedural pause that would require neither party to make a public commitment — to allow talks time to begin. Saudi Arabia dispatched its first direct diplomatic envoy to Tehran since the ceasefire, arriving overnight Monday. The Hormuz Strait has partially reopened to neutral-flag commercial shipping, though Iranian warships and US carrier groups remain in close proximity.

Vice President Vance flew to Pakistan with Witkoff and Kushner Tuesday morning for a second round of US-Iran negotiations. Touska · Precondition Release it Iran demand · US refuses

Iran demands Touska release before talks. Washington refuses. A standoff within a standoff.

Tehran's condition for sending a delegation to Pakistan: the US must publicly acknowledge that the seizure of the cargo vessel Touska was illegal and immediately release the ship. CENTCOM maintains the Touska was in violation of the naval blockade under terms Iran accepted April 17. Until this legal disagreement is resolved — or set aside — the diplomatic track has no on-ramp.

Source: NBC · Euronews
UK · France 72 hrs Technical extension · joint proposal

UK and France propose 72-hour technical ceasefire extension — no commitment required.

London and Paris, the co-chairs of the April 17 summit that produced the original ceasefire, have jointly proposed a procedural pause that would extend the truce by 72 hours without requiring a public acknowledgment from either the US or Iran. The proposal is designed to give diplomats time to get into the same room. Neither Washington nor Tehran has accepted the proposal as of Tuesday morning.

Source: Euronews
Hormuz · Status Partial Neutral-flag vessels · warships remain

Hormuz partially reopens to neutral-flag shipping as Iran differentiates commercial passage.

Iran has allowed neutral-flag commercial tankers through the Strait of Hormuz while continuing to block US-flagged and US-escorted vessels. Oil tanker operators are rerouting around the restriction; shipping insurance premiums remain elevated at Lloyd's. US and Iranian warships remain in proximity in the Gulf of Oman, maintaining the conditions that triggered Sunday's Touska incident.

Source: Euronews · Al Jazeera
Saudi Arabia Envoy Riyadh → Tehran · overnight contact

Saudi Arabia sends envoy to Tehran overnight — first direct contact since April 17.

Riyadh dispatched a senior diplomatic envoy to Tehran overnight Monday, marking the first direct Saudi-Iran contact since the ceasefire was signed. Saudi Arabia, whose oil infrastructure sits within range of Iranian ballistic missiles, has been quietly pressing both sides to find a path to talks. The envoy's mandate and the substance of discussions have not been disclosed.

Source: CNN
Israel–Lebanon Day 5 Truce holds · UN monitors

Israel–Lebanon truce holds at Day 5; UN monitors report no major violations.

The parallel Israel-Lebanon ceasefire, signed alongside the US-Iran truce on April 17, remained intact through Tuesday with no major violations reported by UN monitors. Lebanon's government issued a formal statement urging Iran to send a delegation to Pakistan, warning that an escalation of the US-Iran conflict would collapse the fragile quiet along the northern border. France reaffirmed its commitment to providing peacekeeping support regardless of the US-Iran outcome.
