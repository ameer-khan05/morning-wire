US Central Command completed its seventh consecutive strike night, targeting a desalination plant in southern Iran — the first civilian-adjacent infrastructure target of the campaign. CENTCOM: dual-use military logistics. Iran: civilian water supply. The strike opens an international law dimension the prior six nights largely avoided.

A desalination plant requires a more complex legal justification than the prior target set. Coastal missile batteries and logistics bridges are recognized military targets; their civilian use is incidental. A desalination plant's primary function is civilian water supply; in southern Iran, where few alternative sources exist, its destruction has direct humanitarian consequences. CENTCOM's dual-use characterization must satisfy international humanitarian law's proportionality requirement — military advantage must outweigh civilian harm. Iran's counter-characterization will be argued in every multilateral forum, including at the WAIC summit where 29 nations are meeting with the UN Secretary-General present.
