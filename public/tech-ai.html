OpenAI released the full GPT-5.6 family — Sol, Terra, and Luna — to full public availability on July 9, ending the government-security-review preview period that began June 26 after the export-control framework applied to advanced AI models required scrutiny before broad release. GPT-5.6 Sol, the flagship, is priced at $5 per million input tokens and $30 per million output tokens for extended API use and is now available to all ChatGPT Pro subscribers, enterprise API customers, and Codex users; Terra occupies the mid-tier performance-cost balance and Luna is the lightweight fast-inference model. The launch means that for the first time since the Fable 5 suspension began June 12, every major frontier AI lab — OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, and the China-based labs — has a publicly available flagship model simultaneously. The 27-day competitive asymmetry created by overlapping governance actions is over.

The significance of today's launch is less about GPT-5.6 Sol's specific capability benchmarks — which will take weeks of independent evaluation to fully establish — and more about the competitive signal that full simultaneous availability creates. Enterprise AI procurement teams that have been deferring Q3 decisions while the Fable 5 suspension and GPT-5.6 preview created uncertainty about which frontier model to build on can now compare all options simultaneously. The three-tier architecture (Sol for maximum capability, Terra for balanced performance-cost, Luna for speed-sensitive applications) mirrors the tiered access model that Anthropic established for Fable 5 under the governance framework and that Google uses for the Gemini family. Convergent architecture across labs suggests the governance framework's requirements are shaping model product design as much as raw capability competition is.

SK Hynix's $29 billion Nasdaq IPO — the largest US listing by a South Korean company — opened Thursday 7-fold oversubscribed, with shares surging more than 8% in early trading as the company's listing on the Nasdaq drew investor demand that markets analysts called extraordinary given the backdrop of Samsung's paradoxical earnings last week and the DeepSeek AI chip reports that drove the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index down more than 5%. Kioxia, the Japanese semiconductor company, surged in sympathy on Korean markets. Fortune magazine described SK Hynix's US debut as the test of whether the AI trade can still boom — or is headed for a bust — given that the company's core product (high-bandwidth memory for AI accelerators) is the same product category that Samsung's record results failed to sustain in market cap terms.

The 7-fold oversubscription resolves the interpretive question that Samsung's results left open: was last week's sell-on-news dynamic a verdict on the AI memory trade broadly (duration risk across the sector) or on Samsung specifically (a company-specific peak-pricing assessment)? Seven-fold IPO demand for another AI memory company, on the day after Samsung's results and the DeepSeek chip report, suggests the market is distinguishing between Samsung's maturity premium and SK Hynix's growth-stage positioning. SK Hynix is a newer entrant to US public markets with more exposure to the HBM3 product cycle that AI accelerator demand requires; Samsung carries more of the cycle's history. Whether that distinction holds through Thursday's full trading session will be the first clean data point on where the AI memory trade actually stands.

Moonshot AI's Kimi K2.7 Code became the first open-weight model available in GitHub Copilot's model picker on July 9, running at provider-list rates under Copilot's usage-based billing — a milestone for open-weight model distribution that represents a meaningful change in Microsoft's historically proprietary-model stance within its flagship developer AI tool. Google simultaneously expanded the Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform with two new models: Omni Flash for conversational video editing at $0.10 per second of video output and Nano Banana 2 Lite, capable of generating images in approximately 4 seconds. Hewlett Packard Enterprise expanded its AI Factory portfolio with NVIDIA to add the NVIDIA Vera CPU for agent orchestration and the NVIDIA Agent Toolkit for managing autonomous agents safely in production environments.

Kimi K2.7 Code's entry into GitHub Copilot is the open-weight model distribution breakthrough that the China AI lab ecosystem has been working toward: GitHub Copilot has more than 1 million paid enterprise seats and is the primary development workflow for a large share of professional developers. An open-weight model from a Chinese lab entering that distribution channel is strategically significant for Moonshot AI regardless of K2.7 Code's specific benchmark performance relative to GPT-5.6 and Fable 5. Google's enterprise platform expansion and HPE-NVIDIA's agentic AI push represent the infrastructure layer hardening that follows model availability: once frontier models are simultaneously publicly available, enterprise deployment at scale becomes the next competitive arena, and agentic orchestration is where that competition is now playing out.
