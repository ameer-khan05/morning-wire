Gemini 3.5 Pro launched Friday as confirmed — Gate.com reporting "Google Gemini 3.5 Pro Launches July 17 with Enhanced Visual Generation Capabilities" — with a 2-million-token context window double GPT-5.6 Sol's ceiling, a Deep Think reasoning layer for complex problem-solving and autonomous coding and tool-management workflows, rebuilt SVG scene generation addressing the original model's structural failure, and audio capability through Fable integration, arriving on the same day China's World AI Conference opened with Xi Jinping's keynote, ensuring maximum AI news density for the rebuilt model's market entry.

The architectural rebuild is the story behind the launch. Google DeepMind scrapped the original Gemini 3.5 Pro after finding structural failures in recursive tool-calling — the capability that enables agentic AI workflows where a model calls a tool, receives an output, and chains further tool calls autonomously. Shipping a model that failed in recursive tool-calling would have damaged the Gemini brand for the enterprise-agentic market that Google Cloud Next '26 explicitly targeted with Gemini Enterprise. The rebuilt architecture held the July 17 date while resolving the tool-calling and SVG failures — a decision that reveals which strategic costs Google management weighted most heavily. The 2-million-token context window is the commercial claim: enterprise developers loading entire codebases for AI-assisted debugging, or legal teams loading full document sets for AI-assisted analysis, have a Google option that doubles the context ceiling of any competing commercial model. Pricing was not confirmed at launch, but the competitive pressure from GPT-5.6 Sol at $5/$30 per million input/output tokens constrains the upper range.

Xi choosing this specific moment for his first AI summit keynote is a deliberate strategic signal. The practical commitments — 5,000 training slots, meteorological tool for 30 nations, five multilateral cooperation frameworks — are designed to be countable and deliverable, giving the framing operational substance rather than just rhetorical positioning. TSMC reported a better-than-expected second quarter but raised its 2026 capital expenditure forecast to $60–64 billion from prior guidance of $52–56 billion — CNBC reporting the capex hike "overshadowed" the earnings beat — triggering a chip-sector sell-off that dragged the Nasdaq down 1.47% to 25,881.95 in its worst session of July, the S&P 500 falling 0.51% to 7,533.77 as the market interpreted the $8 billion capex increase as near-term margin pressure before the long-run AI infrastructure payoff arrives, even as the spending signal itself confirms that AI chip demand is accelerating beyond prior forecasts.

The market's negative reaction to a capex increase that signals demand acceleration is a classic growth-company tension: higher investment today compresses margins and earnings per share in the near term, even when the investment is justified by confirmed customer demand. TSMC's Q2 earnings beat shows the current demand is real; the $60–64 billion capex range shows management expects demand to grow further. Equity markets price the near-term earnings dilution before the capacity payoff arrives. Chip stocks led the sell-off because they are priced most directly on foundry margins and utilization. The broader Nasdaq decline reflects the AI-adjacent premium compression that occurs when the investment cycle's near-term costs are made visible — the same dynamic that punished software infrastructure companies when hyperscaler capex announcements came in above consensus earlier in the year.

Quick hits

  • The 2-million-token context window in Gemini 3.5 Pro deserves a concrete explanation of what it enables that GPT-5.6 Sol's 1-million-token ceiling cannot. One million tokens is approximately 750,000 words — roughly 3,000 pages of text. This is already larger than most real-world document sets that enterprise users need to analyze. But software developers working with large codebases frequently encounter repositories that exceed 1 million tokens when all files, documentation, and test code are included. Legal and regulatory compliance teams analyzing cross-referenced bodies of law, contracts, and precedents run into similar ceilings. At 2 million tokens, Gemini 3.5 Pro can load entire enterprise codebases or full regulatory corpora in a single session without chunking or retrieval systems. The enterprise value is not marginal — it eliminates an entire class of workaround that enterprise AI developers currently build to compensate for context limits. Whether the 2M context quality is consistent across the window (models often degrade in the "lost in the middle" problem for very long contexts) will be the first empirical test that enterprise adopters run.
  • Xi's choice of the AI meteorological tool as a concrete deliverable in the WAIC keynote is revealing about China's AI diplomacy strategy. An AI-powered early-warning meteorological system is maximally non-threatening — it has no dual-use military application, it addresses a real need in developing countries vulnerable to climate-related disasters, and it creates a visible Chinese AI footprint in 30 countries that competes with no other major player's offering in that space. This is the AI equivalent of vaccine diplomacy: a concrete, beneficial, visible contribution that builds relationships and establishes China as a reliable AI technology partner in regions where the US is primarily present as a restrictor of chip access. The 5,000 training opportunities are the long-term play — engineers trained in Chinese AI frameworks and tooling are more likely to deploy Chinese AI systems in future national AI infrastructure projects. The strategy is patient, operational, and backed by concrete commitments, not just rhetoric.
