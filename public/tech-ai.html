The cyber AI arms race goes corporate.

Anthropic's Project Glasswing puts Claude Mythos in the hands of six tech giants to hunt zero-days. OpenAI counters with GPT-5.4-Cyber the same week. Big Tech earnings begin Tuesday. And $606 million in April crypto hacks underscore why this race suddenly matters.

At a glance · 20 Apr 83.1% Claude Mythos · CyberGym benchmark
The Anchor

Project Glasswing: the six companies deputized to hunt zero-days with AI.

Anthropic's Claude Mythos — the cybersecurity-specialized model revealed April 8 — scored 83.1% on the CyberGym benchmark, a test that measures a model's ability to autonomously discover real software vulnerabilities. That number matters not as a leaderboard entry but as a threshold: 83% is the point at which automated vulnerability discovery becomes economically competitive with red-team labor.

Project Glasswing operationalizes that capability. AWS, Apple, Google, JPMorganChase, Microsoft, and Nvidia are receiving early-access API credentials to run Mythos against their own codebases and infrastructure to identify and patch vulnerabilities before adversaries do. A Foreign Policy analysis published today called it "a step change in the offense-defense calculus" and noted that the same capability available to defenders is available to nation-state attackers — making Glasswing's head start a geopolitical signal as much as a security one. OpenAI, not to be outflanked, widened access to GPT-5.4-Cyber within days of Mythos's unveiling.
