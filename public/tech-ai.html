The Anchor

Google bets $40 billion on Anthropic at a $350 billion valuation — the largest AI infrastructure commitment in venture history.

Alphabet announced Friday it will invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic — $10 billion immediately, with an additional $30 billion to follow if Anthropic hits agreed performance milestones — plus guaranteed access to 5 gigawatts of compute from Google Cloud infrastructure. The $350 billion valuation makes Anthropic the most valuable AI-native company outside of OpenAI. The $40 billion commitment from Google alone exceeds the combined venture capital raised by all AI startups globally in 2024. Anthropic separately agreed to spend $100 billion purchasing compute from Amazon as part of a parallel deal, giving the company approximately 10 gigawatts of committed infrastructure — more than most sovereign nations hold in AI compute capacity. For Google, the investment is both defensive (keeping Anthropic from going exclusive to AWS) and offensive (making Claude the AI backbone of Workspace and Google Cloud enterprise products). For Anthropic, it provides a decades-long compute guarantee without the dilution risk of a public market cap reckoning.

The Google-Anthropic deal is the defining tech story of the week, but it shares the stage with an unusually dense cluster of frontier model releases. DeepSeek V4 Flash and Pro launched Friday at $0.14 and $1.74 per million tokens respectively — a 97% discount to GPT-5.5 — establishing a permanent new floor for AI pricing. OpenAI's GPT-5.5 was presented by co-founder Greg Brockman in a Thursday briefing. Moonshot AI's Kimi K2.6 matched GPT-5.5 exactly at 58.6% on SWE-Bench Pro — the leading coding evaluation benchmark — three days before GPT-5.5 even posted its score. xAI's Grok 4.3 joined the release wave. The pattern is clear: the global AI frontier is compressing to near-parity across geographies, with Chinese labs matching US labs on coding benchmarks within days and at a fraction of the inference cost. Alphabet, Meta, and Microsoft begin reporting Q1 on Wednesday April 29; Bridgewater estimates combined Big Tech AI capex at $650 billion in 2026, up from $410 billion in 2025.

Google · Anthropic $40B $350B valuation · 5 GW compute · $100B AWS parallel

Google commits $40 billion to Anthropic — $10B now, $30B contingent, plus 5 GW cloud compute access.

Alphabet announced Friday it will invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic — $10 billion immediately at a $350 billion valuation, with an additional $30 billion contingent on performance milestones. As part of the deal, Anthropic secured guaranteed access to 5 gigawatts of compute from Google Cloud, in addition to a parallel $100 billion Amazon compute commitment. Together the two deals give Anthropic approximately 10 gigawatts of infrastructure — a level that rivals or exceeds the independent compute capacity of most national AI programs. The $350 billion Anthropic valuation makes it the most valuable AI-native company outside OpenAI and significantly exceeds Anthropic's $200 billion valuation from its last round in late 2025. For Google, the bet is both defensive and strategic: it deepens Claude's integration into Google Workspace and Google Cloud enterprise products while preventing an AWS exclusive.

Alphabet, Meta, Microsoft report April 29 — Bridgewater puts 2026 Big Tech AI capex at $650 billion.

The most consequential earnings week of the first half of 2026 begins Wednesday April 29: Alphabet, Meta, and Microsoft all report Q1 results after the close; Amazon follows Thursday April 30. Bridgewater estimates combined Big Tech AI capex at $650 billion in 2026, up from $410 billion in 2025. Analyst consensus: Alphabet at $92.2 billion in Q1 revenue (+20.6% year-on-year), with management expected to reaffirm $175–$185 billion in full-year capex; Meta at $55.5 billion (+31% YoY); Microsoft at $81.4 billion (+16% YoY). The market is no longer rewarding AI spending on faith — every transcript will be searched for evidence that the capex is producing revenue, stronger margins, and durable monetization. Motley Fool calls Microsoft's April 29 earnings "the most important tech event of 2026."

DeepSeek · V4 −97% vs GPT-5.5 · permanent cost floor · China AI

DeepSeek V4 reaction: China's 97% pricing discount to GPT-5.5 has set a permanent new cost floor for AI.

One day after DeepSeek V4's launch, the industry's response is crystallizing: the pricing gap between US frontier models and Chinese alternatives is no longer a disruption — it is a structural market feature. DeepSeek V4 Flash at $0.14 per million tokens versus OpenAI's GPT-5.5 at $5 per million (a 97% discount) forces every US AI lab to either match pricing, defend premium positioning with measurable superiority, or watch enterprise customers route workloads to Chinese alternatives. OpenAI has already advised developers not to carry over old GPT-5.5 prompts when migrating workflows — a signal that the model's positioning is shifting. The pattern, appearing one year after DeepSeek V3's first disruption, confirms the dynamic is systematic, not accidental.

Source: Bloomberg · CNN · TechCrunch
Kimi K2.6 · Moonshot AI 58.6% SWE-Bench Pro · matches GPT-5.5 · frontier flattens

Moonshot AI's Kimi K2.6 matches GPT-5.5 at 58.6% on SWE-Bench Pro — the coding frontier is effectively tied.

Kimi K2.6, released by Chinese lab Moonshot AI last week, achieved a 58.6% resolve rate on the SWE-Bench Pro coding evaluation — the identical score posted by OpenAI's GPT-5.5 three days later. For software engineering automation — the highest-value near-term AI application — the race between US and Chinese labs is now effectively a tie on public benchmarks. The "3-to-6 month gap" between US and Chinese AI cited in DeepSeek V4 coverage applies to broad reasoning; on coding tasks, it has closed to near-zero. Kimi K2.6 joins DeepSeek V4, Alibaba Qwen 3.6-Plus, and Zhipu GLM-5.1 in a cluster of Chinese model releases this month that collectively match or approach the US frontier.

Source: LLM Stats · devFlokers
Anthropic · Mythos Glasswing Not public · cybersecurity risk · select partners only

Anthropic withholds Mythos from public release on safety grounds — available only to select partners under Project Glasswing.

Anthropic confirmed this week that its most powerful model, Mythos, will not be publicly released due to cybersecurity risks — a striking decision for an AI safety company now valued at $350 billion. Available only to vetted partners under "Project Glasswing," Mythos reportedly demonstrates capabilities in offensive security research that Anthropic concluded require restricted access and cannot be safely open-released. The decision is the most visible example to date of a frontier AI lab declining to release a model specifically on safety grounds rather than commercial strategy. The backdrop: AI security experts from NIST, OWASP, SANS, and COSAI convened in Washington this week to set standards for AI systems operating autonomously in enterprise environments — and a White House memo from National Cyber Director Kratsios cited evidence of foreign industrial-scale AI distillation campaigns.

AI · Military War Room Commanders · AI-generated targeting · intelligence

Generative AI now has a seat in military command — commanders are acting on AI-generated targeting advice.

MIT Technology Review's April 2026 "10 Things That Matter in AI" issue leads with the integration of generative AI into active military command structures: commanders in conflict zones are using AI-synthesized intelligence briefings and — in documented but not publicly confirmed cases — receiving and acting on AI-generated targeting recommendations. The US-Iran conflict has accelerated the deployment timeline. OpenAI, Google, and Palantir have all disclosed or been reported to have active US military contracts in 2026, a shift from 2023's employee-led protests over Project Maven. The development reshapes the institutional relationship between AI labs and national security customers — and raises unanswered questions about accountability for AI-assisted lethal decisions.
