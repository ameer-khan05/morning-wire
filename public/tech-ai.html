The Anchor

Tesla Q1 hits the tape tonight. The EPS doesn't matter. Terafab does.

Wall Street has been pricing the delivery miss for weeks. Tesla delivered 358,023 vehicles in Q1 — down 14% sequentially, approximately 7,000 short of consensus. The miss is known. Street consensus is $0.37 EPS on $22.71B revenue, and analysts broadly expect Tesla to meet or narrowly beat that bar on adjusted numbers even as automotive gross margins remain under pressure from pricing actions in China and Europe. The real question is not the backward-looking financial result. It is whether Elon Musk uses tonight's 5:30 PM ET call to disclose anything substantive about Terafab: the planned one-terawatt AI compute facility that has appeared in media reports but remains excluded from Tesla's $20B+ official capital expenditure guidance.

If Musk confirms Terafab with real numbers — scale, timeline, financing structure — it would represent the largest corporate infrastructure commitment in US history by a significant margin. Goldman Sachs estimates the combined 2026 AI capex of the Magnificent Seven at roughly $350 billion; Terafab alone, if the rumored figures are accurate, could approach half that. The market has been pricing Tesla as a car company that moonlights in energy and robotics. A serious Terafab disclosure reframes the entire thesis. Meanwhile, the broader earnings calendar has shifted: Alphabet and Meta, previously expected this week, now report April 29 alongside Microsoft. Amazon follows April 30.
