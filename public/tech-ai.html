China launched the World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organisation — WAICO — at WAIC Day 2 in Shanghai, with UN Secretary-General Guterres present alongside representatives from 29 founding nations including Indonesia, Brazil, Malaysia, South Africa, Russia, and Pakistan. WAICO converts Xi's Thursday bilateral AI frameworks (ASEAN, African Union, Arab League, BRICS, SCO) into a multilateral body with a formal charter and secretariat. Guterres's presence is the most significant external validation possible — signaling potential recognition as a legitimate global AI governance actor rather than a Chinese diplomatic initiative alone.

Apple's trade-secret lawsuit (filed July 10, Bloomberg: "blockbuster case") names Tang Tan — former VP product design, led iPhone/Apple Watch — and Chang Liu, who worked at Apple eight years before joining OpenAI in January 2026. Central allegation: Liu downloaded "dozens of Apple's confidential hardware-related files, including unreleased products, engineering presentations, and proprietary project data." A trade-secret injunction could force OpenAI to redesign hardware from a clean-room start — "threatens to disrupt OpenAI's bid to rival the iPhone." Separately, Google delayed Gemini 3.5 Pro's broader rollout after internal testing revealed shortfalls in coding and complex long-horizon reasoning — gaps in exactly the high-value developer use cases enterprise AI adoption depends on.

China formally launched the World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organisation — WAICO — at WAIC Day 2 in Shanghai, with UN Secretary-General Guterres present alongside representatives from 29 founding nations. Al Jazeera: "China's Xi Jinping launches new AI alliance" — institutionalizing the bilateral cooperation frameworks Xi announced Thursday.

WAICO's founding membership and UN Secretary-General presence are the two facts that determine whether it becomes a durable governance institution or a summit declaration with a name. The 29-nation membership spans Southeast Asia, South America, sub-Saharan Africa, and Russia — a majority of the world's population. Guterres's participation gives WAICO a UN-adjacent legitimacy no bilateral Chinese AI cooperation framework could claim. The governance charter will determine whether authority is binding or advisory — questions the founding ceremony established the organization exists to answer.

Apple filed a trade-secret lawsuit against OpenAI naming former VP Tang Tan and former engineer Chang Liu — who joined OpenAI in January 2026 — alleging Liu downloaded "dozens of Apple's confidential hardware-related files, including unreleased products, engineering presentations, and proprietary project data." Bloomberg: "threatens to disrupt OpenAI's bid to rival the iPhone."

The lawsuit's strategic context: the 2024 Apple-OpenAI ChatGPT-iOS partnership brought both companies into organizational proximity. The theory — that proximity enabled confidential hardware IP transfer — stakes Apple's case on whether Liu's downloaded materials informed OpenAI's device project. Tang Tan led iPhone and Apple Watch product design; a VP of that role would have had access to the most sensitive competitive intelligence about Apple's device roadmap. A trade-secret injunction could force design changes or delays in OpenAI's AI hardware plans. OpenAI's response has not yet been published.

Quick hits

  • WAICO gives China a multilateral AI governance institution with a membership roster, formal governance documents, and UN Secretary-General validation — a different kind of influence than bilateral MoUs or summit declarations. The practical question is whether it develops real normative authority or remains a diplomatic vehicle for framing the US-China AI competition. The US and EU have not yet decided whether to engage it as a legitimate governance body.
