SpaceX priced its IPO at $135 per share Thursday, selling 555.5 million shares of Class A common stock and raising $75 billion — the largest initial public offering in history, more than 2.5 times the previous record set by Saudi Aramco in 2019, and roughly three times the size of any prior technology company IPO. The company begins trading today on the Nasdaq under the ticker SPCX. At its $1.78 trillion valuation, SpaceX is one of the most valuable companies on earth by market capitalization at listing, surpassing the market cap of most S&P 500 companies at the moment of its debut. Elon Musk retains majority voting control through a multi-class share structure.

The institutional order book was reported oversubscribed many times over ahead of Thursday's pricing, reflecting demand from investors who had been unable to access SpaceX in its private rounds — where the company had remained private for 24 years. The $135 price represents a discount to some secondary market valuations that had circulated among SpaceX private shareholders, meaning the deal was priced conservatively to ensure a strong opening. The combined SPCX + Anthropic + OpenAI simultaneous IPO pipeline creates a unique dynamic: institutional allocators are managing three concurrent frontier technology IPO exposures for the first time, each with different risk and return profiles.

SPCX shares represent ownership in a merged SpaceX-xAI entity that combines three distinct and each individually consequential businesses: Starship, the most powerful rocket ever built and the vehicle behind NASA's Artemis moon program and commercial payload missions; Starlink, the satellite internet constellation serving more than 3 million subscribers across 100+ countries with a growing enterprise and government data segment; and xAI's Grok large language model, which is deployed as a consumer AI assistant, an enterprise API, and — in Musk's stated thesis — the intelligence layer that coordinates Starlink satellite data for AI-powered applications. The merger argument is that all three are fundamentally infrastructure companies, and shared infrastructure (compute, communications, launch capacity) creates compounding returns at scale.

The xAI merger is the element of SpaceX's IPO that most distinguishes it from prior technology listings: Anthropic and OpenAI are pure AI plays; SpaceX-xAI is an AI play with a launch vehicle and a satellite constellation attached. The Starlink subscriber revenue provides a cash flow base that AI-only companies lack, while Grok's enterprise API growth gives SPCX exposure to the LLM market. Whether the combined entity deserves a premium over the sum of its parts — or a conglomerate discount — will be one of the analyst debates of the SPCX listing's first weeks.

Nvidia Blackwell Laptop · ~1 Petaflop · Arm CPU + Blackwell GPU · 128GB Unified Memory · Autumn 1 PFLOP Nvidia: Arm CPU + Blackwell GPU + 128GB unified memory → ~1 petaflop local AI · consumer laptop shipping autumn 2026 · OEM partners: Microsoft, Dell, HP, Asus, Lenovo, MSI · Windows "agentic" OS update targets local agent runtime · 1 petaflop: previously required data center GPU cluster · edge AI inference at data-center scale · turns Windows into autonomous-agent platform · Nvidia Agent Toolkit: open-source enterprise agent framework + OpenShell runtime

Nvidia confirmed that its Blackwell laptop chips — pairing an Arm CPU with a Blackwell GPU and 128GB of unified memory to deliver approximately 1 petaflop of local AI compute — will ship this autumn in laptops from Microsoft, Dell, HP, Asus, Lenovo, and MSI. The configuration brings data-center-class AI inference capability to the consumer laptop market, enabling on-device AI reasoning at scales that previously required cloud GPU clusters. Microsoft's Windows operating system is being updated to support an "agentic" runtime optimized for the Blackwell architecture, turning the laptop into a platform for autonomous AI agents running without internet connectivity. Nvidia also released the Agent Toolkit, an open-source enterprise AI agent framework including OpenShell, a runtime that enforces policy-based security and privacy guardrails on autonomous agents.

The 1-petaflop laptop milestone is significant as a hardware inflection: it is the point at which AI inference capability becomes portable, private, and continuous — running on a device that a person carries rather than in a cloud accessed via API. For enterprise use cases where data privacy prevents cloud AI (healthcare, legal, financial services), the Blackwell laptop provides a compliant alternative. For consumer use, it enables AI assistants that work offline, remember context across sessions, and run complex reasoning without latency. The Nvidia Agent Toolkit's OpenShell guardrail runtime addresses the security concern that autonomous agents running locally could exfiltrate data or execute unauthorized actions — a precondition for enterprise deployment of laptop-based autonomous AI.

Quick hits

  • SpaceX's $135 IPO price represents the first publicly tradeable price for a company that has been valued in private markets for 24 years. Secondary market transactions in SpaceX shares had suggested private valuations in the $1.5-2.0 trillion range, making $135 and $1.78 trillion a pricing outcome near the middle of secondary market expectations. The stock's opening trade today — and its first-week price action — will be the market's verdict on whether $1.78 trillion is cheap, fair, or expensive for the combined SpaceX-xAI business at current scale. Institutional analysts who had private research on SpaceX for years are publishing their first public price targets simultaneously with the listing.
  • The Nvidia Blackwell laptop's 128GB unified memory pool is the architectural feature that enables 1-petaflop local AI. Current high-end laptops typically have 16-32GB of RAM and 8-12GB of discrete GPU memory — insufficient for running frontier language models with hundreds of billions of parameters. 128GB unified memory means the same memory pool is shared between the CPU and GPU, enabling the full parameter footprint of a large model to reside in device memory and run inference at petaflop speeds. This is the hardware capability that makes on-device frontier AI (not just smaller edge AI) achievable for the first time in a portable form factor.
  • The SpaceX IPO's timing coincides with the Iran conflict's possible resolution — a double catalyst for markets Friday. SpaceX's launch business is not directly affected by the Iran conflict, but xAI's enterprise AI contracts and Starlink's government communications contracts have indirect exposure to the geopolitical risk environment. A confirmed Iran ceasefire would remove a macro drag on risk assets including SPCX, potentially amplifying the stock's first-day move beyond what the company's fundamentals alone would produce. The overlap of the IPO debut and the peace deal timeline is accidental but consequential for price discovery.
