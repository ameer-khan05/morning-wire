Anthropic reported $47 billion in annualized revenue as of May 2026 — up from $9 billion at end-2025, a 47-fold increase in 17 months that AI Business Weekly described as "an unprecedented growth trajectory for enterprise software" — driven primarily by Claude Code, which has become the most commercially successful agentic AI product in the market, and by direct API adoption across enterprise and startup customers paying per token through Anthropic's API, Amazon Bedrock, and Google Cloud Vertex AI. More than 1,000 customers now spend over $1 million annually on Anthropic services, a count that doubled in under two months as of April 2026. Over 100,000 organizations are running Claude on AWS Bedrock alone. Anthropic filed confidentially for an IPO on June 1, 2026, targeting a $965 billion valuation following a $65 billion Series H round, and is projecting Q2 2026 as its first profitable quarter — Futurum Group estimating approximately $559 million in operating profit if the company hits its $10.9 billion quarterly revenue target, more than doubling the prior quarter. Claude Sonnet 5, launched June 30, offers near-Opus 4.8 performance as Anthropic's most agentic Sonnet yet — capable of using browsers and terminals autonomously — at introductory pricing of $2 per million input tokens and $10 per million output tokens through August 31.

Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro is scheduled for Thursday July 17 with a 2-million-token context window — the largest commercial context window available from any AI model — which enables enterprise developers to load entire codebases, legal document sets, or research corpora into a single AI session without retrieval-augmented generation systems, directly changing the economics of complex enterprise AI deployments. At Google Cloud Next '26, Google has been presenting enterprise buyers with AI "agent fleets" rather than chatbots: autonomous agents connected to enterprise data running cross-organizational workflows, a framing that matches how the highest-value AI deployments actually work in production. Federal Reserve Chair Warsh's five task forces — one of which specifically examines how AI could impact productivity and jobs — represent the first time the US central bank has formally embedded AI analysis into its economic modeling framework, a signal that the productivity debate is now institutional rather than speculative. Warsh's House Financial Services Committee testimony today is expected to address AI's economic role as part of his commentary on the rate path.

Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro with a 2-million-token context window is targeting a July 17 launch — the largest commercial context window available from any AI model, enabling enterprise developers to process full codebases, legal document repositories, and research corpora in a single AI session without retrieval-augmented generation systems — while Google Cloud Next '26 has been presenting enterprise buyers with AI "agent fleets" rather than chatbots: autonomous agents connected to enterprise data, executing workflows across an organization in a framing that directly competes with Anthropic's Claude Code enterprise positioning and reflects how the highest-value enterprise AI deployments actually work in production.

The 2-million-token context is not just a benchmark achievement — it changes enterprise AI deployment economics. Retrieval-augmented generation systems, which retrieve relevant document chunks to fit within shorter context windows, add latency, infrastructure cost, and retrieval error to enterprise AI workflows. A 2M-token context makes it viable to load a company's entire codebase or document collection into the model context for each session, eliminating the RAG layer for many enterprise applications. This is the architectural shift that makes AI agents practical: instead of retrieving context, the agent has the context. Google's Cloud Next '26 "agent fleet" framing is the commercial packaging of that technical shift, and its timing — one week before anticipated Anthropic IPO roadshow activity and concurrent with OpenAI's $730B IPO planning — maximizes competitive pressure on both companies' enterprise sales motions.

Quick hits

  • The Anthropic IPO valuation math at $965B deserves to be examined in the context of its revenue trajectory. At $47B ARR in May 2026, Anthropic is trading at roughly a 20x revenue multiple in the confidential filing. OpenAI's $730B IPO targets a revenue run rate of approximately $40B ARR — similar trajectory — at a 18x revenue multiple on the same basis. The Anthropic multiple premium reflects the market's view that Claude Code's enterprise agentic positioning is structurally more defensible than OpenAI's consumer and API-general positioning, and that Anthropic's first-profitable-quarter achievement reduces the execution risk discount that has historically weighed on high-growth pre-profitability AI companies. The two IPOs together — the first major frontier AI company listings — will establish the pricing framework for every AI model company valuation for the next decade. How the market receives them will determine whether AI model companies are valued as software businesses (high multiple, predictable subscription revenue) or as R&D-intensive technology bets (lower multiple, capital-intensive).
  • The 2M-token context window that Gemini 3.5 Pro brings to market Thursday is the product feature that enterprise AI buyers have been waiting for most. The single biggest friction in deploying AI at enterprise scale — across codebases, legal libraries, financial document sets, and research collections — is that context limits force either RAG complexity or information loss. A 2M-token context that can hold an entire enterprise knowledge base in a single session session eliminates the retrieval accuracy problem that has caused the most AI deployment failures. Google's Cloud Next '26 "agent fleet" framing is explicitly targeting the enterprise segment that has been most frustrated by RAG limitations — legal, finance, and software development — which are exactly the segments where Anthropic's Claude Code has built its highest-retention customer base. The head-to-head between Gemini 3.5 Pro's context advantage and Claude Code's agentic depth will define enterprise AI market share through year-end.
