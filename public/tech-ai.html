The Anchor

OpenAI's $25B turn — and what it means for everyone chasing it.

The number that moved the Valley overnight wasn't a benchmark score. It was revenue. OpenAI's annualized run-rate has crossed $25 billion — a figure large enough that bankers are now openly pricing a late-2026 public listing. If it lands, it will be the first trillion-dollar IPO the market has ever seen. And it will force every other frontier lab to answer the same question: at this scale, who else can actually compete?

Today's Cerebras debut is the first downstream tremor. The wafer-scale chipmaker lists this morning targeting $35 billion — a 60% premium to its February mark. On the other end of the spectrum, Mozilla shipped Thunderbolt, an open-source self-hosted AI client. The barbell has never been more pronounced: hyperscaled centralized inference on one side, run-it-yourself infrastructure on the other.
