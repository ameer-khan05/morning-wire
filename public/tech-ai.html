OpenAI ships GPT-5.5. SpaceX bets on in-house GPUs.

OpenAI released GPT-5.5 Thursday — the first fully retrained base model since GPT-4.5 — with a 1-million-token context window, improved reasoning, and a doubled API price of $5/$30 per million tokens, rolling out immediately to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise subscribers. The Anchor

GPT-5.5 ships with a 1-million-token context window — and SpaceX wants to build the chips to run the next generation.

OpenAI's GPT-5.5, released Thursday, is the company's first fully retrained base model since GPT-4.5 — a meaningful architectural distinction. Where GPT-5.4 was an incremental update, GPT-5.5 represents a new training run with improved reasoning, stronger coding performance, and a 1-million-token context window, the first in any OpenAI API model. Pricing doubled from GPT-5.4's $2.50/$15 to $5/$30 per million input/output tokens, reflecting both the model's capability jump and OpenAI's confidence in enterprise demand. The release came just six weeks after GPT-5.4, a pace that underscores how fiercely the frontier labs are competing for enterprise customers. GPT-5.5 is available immediately in ChatGPT Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise; the model also powers the latest Codex update. OpenAI's own benchmarks show GPT-5.5 outperforming Gemini 3.1 Pro and Claude Opus 4.5 across its evaluation suite — though both Anthropic and Google contest the framing of those comparisons.

The infrastructure story arriving alongside the model release is just as significant. SpaceX's S-1 IPO registration, reviewed by Reuters, lists "manufacturing our own GPUs" among the "substantial capital expenditures" the company is undertaking ahead of its $1.75 trillion IPO expected this summer. The disclosure connects directly to Terafab: a vertically integrated mega-fab in Austin, Texas, designed as a joint venture between SpaceX, xAI, Tesla, and Intel, combining chip design, fabrication, memory production, advanced packaging, and testing under one roof. SpaceX simultaneously warned investors it "does not have long-term contracts with many of our direct chip suppliers" — a risk that in-house manufacturing is intended to mitigate over time. The convergence of SpaceX's AI ambitions with Musk's other companies creates the most complex multi-entity AI infrastructure play in the industry, with Terafab at its center.

OpenAI · GPT-5.5 1M ctx $5/$30 · fully retrained · ChatGPT

OpenAI releases GPT-5.5: first full retraining since GPT-4.5, 1-million-token context, $5/$30 pricing.

GPT-5.5 is the first OpenAI API model to ship with a 1-million-token context window, enabling it to process and reason over roughly 750,000 words in a single call. The model shows meaningful gains in agentic coding, mathematical reasoning, and scientific research tasks. OpenAI describes it as "a new class of intelligence for real work." The 2× API price increase — from $2.50/$15 to $5/$30 per million tokens — reflects both capability and enterprise demand confidence. The release came just six weeks after GPT-5.4, accelerating the frontier release cadence to a pace the industry has not previously sustained.

SpaceX S-1 discloses GPU manufacturing plans via Terafab — and warns investors of chip supply risk.

SpaceX listed manufacturing its own GPUs among the "substantial capital expenditures" it plans to undertake, per S-1 excerpts reviewed by Reuters. Terafab — the joint venture involving SpaceX, xAI, Tesla, and Intel — is designed as a vertically integrated mega-fab in Austin, combining design, fabrication, memory, and packaging under one system. SpaceX simultaneously warned IPO investors that it "does not have long-term contracts with many of our direct chip suppliers," making the GPU production plan both a strategic ambition and an explicit risk hedge. Trump administration vows crackdown on China's exploitation of US AI models as frontier gap narrows.

The Trump administration announced it will intensify enforcement against foreign entities — specifically naming China — exploiting US-developed AI models for military, surveillance, and strategic applications. The move comes as research shows China narrowing the frontier AI performance gap, with multiple Chinese models now competitive on major reasoning benchmarks. The administration is expected to issue updated export-control guidance covering model weights and fine-tuned derivatives, not just chip exports — a significant regulatory expansion that would directly complicate the Alibaba-Tencent-DeepSeek investment landscape.
