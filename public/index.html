OpenAI and Broadcom unveiled Jalapeño on June 24 — OpenAI's first custom AI accelerator, a reticle-sized ASIC designed specifically for LLM inference, developed in nine months by co-locating OpenAI's engineering teams with Broadcom's silicon implementation group and using OpenAI's own models to compress and accelerate the chip design and optimization process. Early testing shows Jalapeño delivering substantially better performance per watt than current Nvidia alternatives for inference workloads, at approximately half the cost; initial deployment targets end of 2026, with full production scale in early 2028 as part of a multi-generation compute platform for gigawatt-scale data centers with Microsoft and other partners.

Separately, Anthropic sent a letter to the Senate Banking Committee accusing Alibaba's Qwen AI lab of running approximately 25,000 fraudulent accounts — concealed behind commercial proxy services to circumvent geographic restrictions on Chinese entity access — generating 28.8 million unauthorized exchanges with Claude between April 22 and June 5, 2026, systematically targeting the model's software-engineering and agentic-reasoning capabilities in the largest known AI model distillation campaign ever filed. Senators Bill Hagerty (R-TN) and Andy Kim (D-NJ) are moving to add sanctions and blacklisting language to defense legislation. DeepSeek simultaneously made its V4-Pro 75% price cut permanent, settling the model at $0.435 input / $0.87 output per million tokens — undercutting GPT-5.5 by more than 5× on input — structurally escalating the AI pricing war.

The Iran ceasefire is in crisis. An IRGC drone struck a Singapore-flagged cargo ship in the Strait of Hormuz on Friday, June 26, in an attack Trump characterized as a "foolish violation" of the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding signed June 17. The US military struck Iranian missile and drone storage facilities and coastal radar installations in retaliation, with US Central Command calling it a "powerful response" to Iran's "dangerous behavior." The IRGC then fired missiles and drones at US-linked targets in the early hours of Sunday, June 28, between 2 and 3 a.m., in what it characterized as retaliation for the US strikes; Kuwait and Bahrain reported incoming projectiles and condemned the aerial attacks as "heinous." The MOU is now Day 11 with 49 days remaining before its August 17 expiry.

The Hormuz escalation directly strikes the MOU's economic foundation: the 60-day sanctions waiver allowing Iranian crude to flow through the strait was the deal's most durable stabilizing mechanism, keeping WTI at disinflationary levels and giving both sides an economic incentive to hold the framework together. Active military exchange at the strait's choke point puts that supply assurance at risk in a way that diplomatic protests — the posture Iran had maintained through Day 10 — did not. A separate dispute continues over IAEA nuclear inspector access: the IAEA's director general confirmed the US-Iran peace deal grants inspectors access to Iranian nuclear sites, contradicting Iran's position that no new inspection arrangements were agreed. The World Cup group stage closes June 30 and the Round of 16 begins July 2; Iran's Team Melli continues to compete in the US-hosted tournament even as the two countries exchange strikes overnight.

Oropouche Cuba: 2,257× More Infections Than Detected · Nature Health · 16.7% Seroprevalence · H5N1 Udder Mystery Solved · B12 + Mitochondria 2,257× Nature Health (2026): 670 serum samples La Habana · pre-outbreak (2020–21) vs post-outbreak (2025) · OROV IgG: 0.9% → 13.4% · age-adjusted: 16.7% seroprevalence → 2,257× more infections than detected · ongoing transmission: 1.8% IgM positive · Oropouche: biting midges + some mosquitoes · vertical transmission, fetal deaths, birth defects documented · H5N1 (U Pittsburgh): glycan receptors concentrated in mammary tissue → explains udder vs lung preference → necrotizing mastitis → milk = high viral loads · B12 deficiency: may impair mitochondrial function · AMD3100: freed T cells to attack rare liver cancer

A Nature Health study published this weekend found that the 2024–2025 Oropouche virus outbreak in Cuba infected an estimated 2,257 times more people than official case counts detected — analyzing 670 serum samples from La Habana, the study found IgG seroprevalence rose from 0.9% before the outbreak to 13.4% after, with age-adjusted estimates suggesting 16.7% of the population had been infected, while official case counts captured only a fraction of that burden. Oropouche, spread by biting midges and some mosquitoes, has documented associations with vertical transmission, fetal deaths, and birth defects — making a surveillance gap of this magnitude in a naive population a public health concern that extends well beyond Cuba's borders. A separate University of Pittsburgh study this weekend explained H5N1 bird flu's puzzling preference for dairy cattle's udders over lungs: glycan receptors — the molecular attachment points influenza viruses use to enter cells — are concentrated in mammary tissue, producing severe necrotizing mastitis and high viral loads in milk rather than the pneumonia that would be expected from a respiratory virus.

Researchers also found that Vitamin B12 deficiency — a condition common in older adults, vegans, and people taking metformin — may impair mitochondrial function, the energy-production machinery inside cells, adding a cellular mechanism to the known neurological and hematological consequences of B12 deficiency. A separate study found that AMD3100, an FDA-approved drug used for stem cell mobilization in cancer patients, can free T cells to infiltrate and attack rare liver cancers by releasing them from stroma that typically prevents immune cells from accessing the tumor microenvironment — a repurposing finding that opens a new immunotherapy strategy for a cancer type with very limited options.
