The Lede · 06:00 ET

Trump extends the ceasefire. Iran won't negotiate. BTC just hit an 11-week high.

President Trump late Tuesday replaced the looming Wednesday ceasefire deadline with an open-ended extension — conditioning the truce on Iran submitting a "unified proposal" from its leadership. The naval blockade of Iranian ports stays. Iran's foreign minister responded within hours, calling the blockade "an act of war and a violation of the ceasefire," and Tehran reiterated it will not negotiate while the blockade stands. Vice President Vance's trip to Pakistan was called off. Day 54 begins with the guns quiet and the diplomatic track effectively empty.

Markets exhaled: S&P 500 and Nasdaq futures added 0.5–0.7% Wednesday morning. Bitcoin surged past $78,000 — an 11-week high — as the ceasefire extension combined with $1.4 billion in weekly crypto-fund inflows drove the risk-on move. Oil stayed elevated near $90; the Hormuz blockade premium is not going anywhere until the ships move. Tesla reports Q1 earnings after the bell tonight — Street expects $0.37 EPS on $22.71B revenue, but any Terafab disclosure could reprice the AI-infrastructure story overnight. And it's Earth Day 2026 — the 56th — with new research warning that industrial chemical leaks are delaying ozone recovery by up to seven years.
