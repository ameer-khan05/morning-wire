The European Commission selected the EUROPA consortium — led by Italian AI company Domyn — to build Europe's first open-source frontier AI model covering all 24 official EU languages at more than 400 billion parameters, a scale matching the world's most advanced AI systems. The project receives up to 2.5% of total EuroHPC computing capacity for one year and is Europe's most concrete challenge yet to the US-China dominance of frontier AI development. Anthropic's Fable 5 entered its ninth consecutive day offline under US export-control suspension. The Colorado AI Act takes effect June 30, placing new compliance duties on AI developers and deployers operating in the state — the broadest US state AI regulation to date.

The EUROPA selection is the EU's answer to a question that has hovered over the continent's AI strategy since ChatGPT's launch: can Europe build its own frontier models, or will it be permanently dependent on American and Chinese systems? At 400+ billion parameters trained across all 24 EU languages, EUROPA is not a research experiment — it is an industrial-scale model built to compete with GPT-class systems on multilingual European tasks. The timing alongside Fable 5's absence and OpenAI's DeployCo enterprise push signals that the AI industry is bifurcating: US providers are consolidating enterprise position through deployment services, while Europe is trying to establish sovereign model capability before dependency becomes irreversible.

Shadow Blaster Galaxy: Cosmic Neutrino Source · 11B Light-Years · Nature Astronomy · Flavanols + Heart Health · Interspecies Communication 11 Bly Shadow Blaster (JCMT0402): galaxy 11 billion light-years away · high-energy neutrino IC-210922A traced to it · source: extreme star formation, not a black hole · IceCube Observatory detection · published Nature Astronomy June 19 · 20% of all high-energy neutrinos may originate in similar galaxies · flavanols: 5 fruit/vegetable servings insufficient without flavanol-rich foods · heart health implication · interspecies communication: cross-species "conversations" more flexible than assumed · June 20 research

Astronomers traced a high-energy cosmic neutrino — particle IC-210922A detected by the IceCube Neutrino Observatory in Antarctica — to a galaxy nicknamed Shadow Blaster (JCMT0402−0424) located 11 billion light-years away, with the source being extreme star formation rather than a supermassive black hole, according to a paper published June 19 in Nature Astronomy. Researchers estimate that 20% of all high-energy neutrinos in the universe may originate from similar star-forming galaxies. Separate research found that eating five servings of fruits and vegetables may be insufficient for heart health without including flavanol-rich foods. And a new study published June 20 found that interspecies "conversations" — communication between animals of different species — are more flexible than previously understood.

The Shadow Blaster finding overturns the field's dominant assumption: that supermassive black holes are the primary factories for the universe's highest-energy particles. If 20% of high-energy neutrinos originate from star-forming galaxies like Shadow Blaster, the IceCube observatory's next phase of detections will need to account for an entirely different class of source — compact, dust-obscured, infrared-bright galaxies that are effectively invisible at optical wavelengths and would not appear in conventional surveys of active galactic nuclei.
