The US Commerce Department lifted export restrictions on Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5 Thursday — reversing the government's commercial AI restriction posture one day after the White House asked OpenAI to hold GPT-5.6's public launch. Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick said Anthropic would no longer require an export license after the company agreed to "proactively detect and address security risks associated with the models," work with government on standards for upcoming models, and inform the government of malicious activity. Simultaneously, Anthropic launched Claude Sonnet 5 — approaching Opus 4.8 performance at $2/$10 per million tokens and positioned as the "most agentic Sonnet yet" — filling the mid-tier with a model designed for autonomous multi-step tasks.

The Fable 5/Mythos 5 release alongside the GPT-5.6 gate defines the emerging US frontier AI governance structure: companies that formalize monitoring agreements and government standards collaboration receive commercial freedom; others face informal review gates. Tempus AI separately launched the next generation of Lens, its agentic AI platform for drug development, advancing AI deployment in one of the highest-value scientific applications — and Google's Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite entered public preview as the most cost-efficient model in the Gemini family.

Glamsterdam introduces EVM improvements and gas efficiency enhancements aimed at meaningfully increasing the network's capacity, addressing Ethereum's performance gap versus Solana and competing chains at a moment when institutional interest, channeled through Ethereum Institutional, is building. Ethereum diverged from BTC's rally Thursday, falling 0.51% to $1,565, as Ethereum-specific macro uncertainty persisted despite the protocol's improving long-term narrative.

Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh said at the ECB Forum in Sintra Thursday that inflation risks have come down — a dovish surprise that moved markets decisively: gold surged 1.61% to $4,103, the 10-year Treasury yield fell 6 basis points to 4.32%, and Bitcoin broke toward $60,000, while equities traded mixed as investors positioned ahead of Friday's June non-farm payrolls report in a holiday-shortened session before the July 4 weekend. Warsh's remark departs from the hawkish tone of the June FOMC meeting — where nine dot-plot members projected a rate hike — and represents the first dovish signal from the new Fed chair since taking the role, with immediate effect on risk assets and fixed income.

Friday's June NFP arrives in a low-liquidity session ahead of the four-day July 4 holiday, an environment where initial price moves typically overshoot before correcting when full liquidity returns July 7. A soft print combined with today's Warsh dovish signal would sharply shift September FOMC expectations toward a hold and could produce an extended risk-on move. Oil slipped 1.70% to $68.32 (WTI) as Iran's diplomatic posture — parliament speaker Qalibaf declaring Tehran is "not negotiating at all" — kept Hormuz resolution murky, though the stand-down remains in effect and the energy inflation channel for the Fed remains quiet.

Iran Parliament: "Not Negotiating At All" · Vance: Force on Table · Doha Technical Talks Continue · MOU Day 15 · 45 Days to Aug 17 Doha Iran parliament speaker Qalibaf: "currently not negotiating with the United States at all" · hardliner pushback on MOU · Tehran: won't negotiate until US implements all MOU clauses · indirect technical talks: continue in Doha · Qatar + Pakistan: mediators · VP Vance: US prepared to use force if diplomacy fails · 60-day MOU: halted hostilities but core disputes unresolved · issues: Hormuz governance, nuclear enrichment, sanctions · MOU Day 15 · 45 days remain to Aug 17

Iran's parliamentary speaker Mohammad Baqer Qalibaf declared Thursday that Tehran "is currently not negotiating with the United States at all" — the most direct public repudiation of the Islamabad Memorandum framework by a senior Iranian official since it was signed June 17 — even as indirect technical talks between US and Iranian delegations continued Thursday in Doha under Qatari and Pakistani mediation on MOU Day 15. Qalibaf said Iran would not enter further negotiations until Washington implements all clauses of the MOU, a condition the administration has not publicly addressed. VP Vance separately signaled that the White House is prepared to use force against Iran if diplomacy fails — the clearest US force threat since the ceasefire — raising the stakes around the 60-day window that expires August 17.

Qalibaf's statement reflects domestic hardliner pressure — particularly from IRGC-aligned factions — that has made the MOU politically costly for Iran's executive to openly defend. The dual-track structure this creates is Iran's consistent pattern: public rejection for domestic consumption, continued technical engagement through intermediaries. The core unresolved issues — Hormuz governance, nuclear enrichment levels, sanctions relief, and highly enriched uranium stockpiles — remain contested. Vance's force signal and Qalibaf's negotiation denial, arriving on the same day, illustrate the maximum pressure on both sides of the MOU's 45 remaining days.

Scientists have identified the missing genetic ingredient that enables melanoma cells to become effectively immortal — resolving a long-standing mystery about why the most dangerous form of skin cancer resists treatment so persistently, and defining a new drug target for attacking the mechanism that sustains cancer cell survival beyond normal biological death signals. A Colorado research team separately developed experimental osteoarthritis treatments that appear to regenerate damaged joint tissue rather than simply controlling pain — a potential structural breakthrough for 32 million Americans whose current treatment options address symptoms without reversing the underlying joint deterioration that drives long-term disability.

A study of more than 2,000 older adults in Japan found that people with lower blood vitamin C levels tended to have less gray matter — a correlation suggesting the vitamin's role in neurological health extends to brain volume maintenance and may be relevant to cognitive aging beyond its known antioxidant function. A national US study found that nearly half of Americans with kidney failure who are referred for a transplant never begin the evaluation process, and only 19% ultimately make it onto the transplant waiting list — a systemic access gap with documented racial and socioeconomic disparities in the referral-to-listing pipeline that affects who receives life-extending care.
