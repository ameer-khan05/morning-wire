The Lede · 07:00 ET

April PCE inflation came in at 3.8% annual (3.3% core) Thursday morning, confirming the Iran war's oil premium has settled into consumer prices — the CME FedWatch tool now shows a 39% probability of a Federal Reserve rate hike by year-end, the starkest tightening risk since the conflict began. Nvidia delivered $81.6 billion in Q1 FY2027 revenue, beating estimates by $3.6 billion and guiding Q2 to an unprecedented $91 billion, with Jensen Huang calling Blackwell GPU demand "off the charts." The Iran deal that President Trump called "largely negotiated" on Saturday is now complicated by three rounds of US military strikes.

The April PCE data is the hardest evidence yet that the Iran war's oil premium is not merely a futures-market phenomenon — it is inside the consumer price index, inside the Federal Reserve's preferred inflation gauge, and now inside the Fed's policy calculus in a fundamentally new way. Headline PCE rose 0.4% monthly and 3.8% year-over-year in April, up from March's 3.5%, with core PCE rising to 3.3% from 3.2%. Before this week, the market assigned near-zero probability to a Fed rate hike. Thursday's CME FedWatch reading of 39% probability of at least one rate hike by year-end is not a tail risk — it is a coin flip that is now priced differently than any day since the conflict began on February 28. The 10-year Treasury yield ticked up 4 basis points to 4.50% on the release. The Dow, which set a record close of 50,644.28 on Wednesday as Iran deal optimism built, faces its first genuine Fed-tightening headwind of 2026. S&P 500 futures fell before Thursday's open, though the index remains near all-time highs on the strength of Nvidia's results and the prospect of an Iran resolution that would send WTI materially lower than its current $90.35.

Nvidia's Q1 FY2027 results, reported Tuesday after the bell, resolved the central debate that has run alongside the yield headwind for the past two months: is AI demand strong enough to hold equity valuations at current yield levels? The answer is definitively yes. Revenue of $81.6 billion beat the $78 billion consensus by 4.6% and represents 85% year-over-year growth. Data Center alone generated $75.2 billion, up 92% from a year ago and comprising 92% of total revenue. EPS came in at $2.14 versus the $1.77 estimate. And Q2 guidance of $91 billion — 11.5% sequential growth from an already-record quarter — implies an annualized run-rate above $360 billion before year-end, a number unprecedented in semiconductor history. Jensen Huang said on the earnings call that cloud customers "cannot get enough Blackwell" and that GPU allocations are fully booked. Microsoft is in separate talks to supply Anthropic with its MAIA 200 custom AI chips, a development that would mark the first significant diversification of AI chip supply away from Nvidia for a frontier AI lab — even as Nvidia's dominance at scale remains structurally intact. OpenAI launched DeployCo, a $4 billion enterprise deployment subsidiary backed by TPG, Goldman Sachs, and McKinsey, on May 11.

The Iran situation entered its most dynamic week since the April 8 ceasefire. Trump told reporters Saturday that a deal was "largely negotiated" and would be announced soon, describing a framework including a 60-day ceasefire extension, a Strait of Hormuz reopening, and permission for Iran to sell oil freely while nuclear talks proceed. WTI fell from $108.59 (May 20) to below $90 on the deal prospect, sending the Dow to a record and the Russell 2000 to 2,919.94. But on May 25-26, US Central Command executed self-defense strikes on Iranian missile launch sites and IRGC boats near the Strait, then hit a drone-launch ground control station near Bandar Abbas on May 27. Iran's IRGC accused the US of "egregious ceasefire violations" and warned that retaliation is "legitimate and certain." Trump said Wednesday he will not "rush" the deal and characterized Iran as "negotiating on fumes." The conflict is now in Day 90. WTI has rebounded to $90.35 and Brent climbed to $96.57 (+2.41% this morning) as markets reprice the probability that strikes will delay the deal's conclusion. Bitcoin fell 4% to $72,714 as the combined Iran-strike and PCE-data risk-off move triggered $528 million in BlackRock Bitcoin ETF outflows — the second-largest daily outflow on record for the fund. Ethereum broke below $2,000 to $1,989.

Day 90: President Trump said Saturday that a deal with Iran is "largely negotiated" and would be announced soon — a framework including a 60-day ceasefire extension, Strait of Hormuz reopening, and Iran's right to sell oil freely while nuclear talks proceed. But US Central Command launched three rounds of strikes on Iranian targets on May 25-27, hitting missile launch sites, IRGC boats, and a drone-launch ground control station near Bandar Abbas. Iran's IRGC accused the US of "egregious ceasefire violations" and warned that retaliation is "legitimate and certain." Trump said Wednesday he will not rush the deal because Iran is "negotiating on fumes."

The deal structure reported by Axios on May 24 involves a 60-day ceasefire in which the Strait reopens and Iran resells oil freely, followed by negotiations on a longer nuclear framework — a sequencing that defers the hardest issues (enrichment moratorium duration, verification) but provides immediate oil-market and humanitarian relief. The tension between Trump's "largely negotiated" language and the continuation of US military strikes reflects the deal's dual-track design: diplomatic progress and military pressure are being run simultaneously as leverage mechanisms, each intended to accelerate the other's outcome. Iran's internet blackout — 88 days with near-total connectivity cut since February 28 — is now being partially restored after President Pezeshkian issued a directive to return to pre-December 2025 conditions, a signal of tentative domestic normalization that may indicate regime confidence a deal is imminent.

Brain Aging Reversal · Texas A&M Nasal Spray · GSK Hepatitis B Cure Nasal Texas A&M nasal spray · stem cell extracellular vesicles · quiets neuroinflammation · restores memory · aging mice · Journal of Extracellular Vesicles · GSK hepatitis B drug · "functional cure" · two trials succeeded May 2026 · Japan vitamin K neuron regeneration · USC APOE4 Alzheimer's compounds

Researchers at Texas A&M have demonstrated that a nasal spray delivering stem cell-derived extracellular vesicles reversed brain aging in mice — quieting neuroinflammation and restoring memory after just two doses, in findings published in the Journal of Extracellular Vesicles. The approach works by delivering nano-scale signaling particles that calm microglial overactivation (the brain's inflammatory immune response) and restore mitochondrial function in aging neurons, addressing two root causes of age-related cognitive decline simultaneously.

GSK announced Thursday that two trials of its hepatitis B drug succeeded in producing a "functional cure" — a result scientists described as a significant development for the approximately 296 million people living with chronic hepatitis B infection worldwide. Separately, Japanese researchers identified vitamin K-based compounds that may stimulate the brain to regenerate lost neurons — a potential therapeutic pathway for Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease. USC researchers found drug compounds that reduce brain inflammation specifically linked to the APOE4 gene variant, which carries the highest genetic risk for late-onset Alzheimer's. The week's science is unified by a theme of reversibility: conditions long treated as progressive and irreversible — brain aging, hepatitis B, neurodegeneration — are yielding to targeted interventions at the molecular level.
