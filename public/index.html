The Lede · 07:00 ET

Trump rejects Iran's latest peace proposal — "can't agree to terms," threatens to "blast them away" — as Lebanon's ceasefire disintegrates with IDF strikes killing 30-plus in a day and 12 days left on the May 14 expiry clock.

Iran submitted its latest peace proposal to US mediators in Pakistan on Thursday evening, and President Trump rejected it publicly Friday morning. "They want to make a deal, but I'm not satisfied with it," he told reporters, adding that he would "blast them away" if negotiations collapse. "They're asking for things I can't agree to," he said. The ceasefire that has technically held since early April remains in place but is visibly deteriorating. Israeli strikes killed more than 30 people in southern Lebanon in a single day on Friday — the deadliest day of the extended ceasefire period — as the IDF chief declared "there is no ceasefire" in the south. Hezbollah counts more than 500 alleged IDF violations. The Lebanon ceasefire, extended three weeks in late April, expires May 14 — 12 days from Saturday — with no diplomatic renewal framework in sight and no resolution to the underlying Iran-Hormuz conflict driving the regional pressure.

In technology, a historic industry reversal: Anthropic officially crossed $30 billion in annualized revenue in April, surpassing OpenAI's $25 billion and claiming the top position in the global LLM revenue table for the first time. Counterpoint Research data for Q1 2026 gives Anthropic 31.4% of LLM market revenue share against OpenAI's 29%, driven by an average monthly revenue per user of $16.20 versus OpenAI's $2.20 — from 134 million monthly active users against OpenAI's 900 million. More revenue, dramatically fewer users, dramatically higher per-user rate: Anthropic's premium-enterprise model has outpaced the mass-market model for the first time in the industry's history. Simultaneously, the Department of Defense announced classified AI deployment agreements with eight companies — OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, NVIDIA, SpaceX, Oracle, and Reflection — for deployment on IL-6 and IL-7 classified networks, explicitly keeping Anthropic blacklisted as a "supply chain risk" over a dispute about controls on its Mythos model. The White House is separately drafting an executive action to potentially resolve the Anthropic-Pentagon dispute, with no timeline announced.

Bitcoin reached $78,324 on Saturday morning — up 1.74% from Friday's $76,988 close — after US spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded a $629.9 million net inflow on May 1, reversing a three-day outflow streak. BlackRock's IBIT captured approximately 70% of Friday's flows. April as a whole was the best month for BTC ETF inflows since October 2025, with $2.44 billion flowing in over the first three weeks of the month. The $80,000 psychological level — where a large wall of short-side liquidity has accumulated — is the defining technical test for May: a decisive close above it could trigger a short squeeze toward $84,000. US equity markets are closed for the weekend; Friday's S&P 500 record close of 7,209.01 stands as the benchmark Monday's session will open from, priced against Trump's Iran deal rejection and Lebanon's deterioration.

The largest-ever US longitudinal study of teen cannabis use — 11,000 participants tracked from ages 9 to 17 using biological testing alongside self-reports — confirmed that THC specifically slows memory development in adolescents. Published in Neuropsychopharmacology by UC San Diego, the study found that cannabis users' cognitive growth leveled off while non-using peers continued to improve across memory, attention, language, and processing speed. Separately, clinical trial midpoint results published this week showed that duodenal mucosal resurfacing — a minimally invasive endoscopic "gut reset" that ablates the inner lining of the duodenum — dramatically reduces post-Ozempic weight regain: participants who underwent the procedure regained only 7 pounds after stopping semaglutide, preserving more than 80% of their initial weight loss, compared to the typical pattern of regaining most lost weight within 18 months of stopping GLP-1 drugs.

Largest US teen cannabis study links THC to slower memory development — 11,000 adolescents tracked from ages 9 to 17. A clinical trial shows a gut-reset procedure prevents post-Ozempic weight regain, preserving 80% of initial loss.

A landmark study from UC San Diego — the largest US longitudinal study of teen cannabis use ever conducted — tracked 11,000 participants from ages 9 to 17 using biological testing and confirmed that THC specifically slows memory development in adolescents. Published in Neuropsychopharmacology, the study found that cannabis users' cognitive growth leveled off while non-using peers continued to improve across memory, attention, language, and processing speed. Separately, a published clinical trial showed that duodenal mucosal resurfacing — a minimally invasive endoscopic gut-reset — allows people who stop Ozempic to retain more than 80% of their weight loss, regaining only 7 pounds versus the typical near-total weight regain pattern.
