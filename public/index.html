The Lede · Iran Day 19: US Strikes Ahvaz Petroleum Targets + Khuzestan Water Station · IRGC Disables 2 Tankers in Hormuz · 4 Kuwaiti Sailors Wounded · Microsoft Routes Copilot to MAI Models · BTC $64,810 ▲1.44% · CLARITY Vote Monday · Cyclospora: 34 States · Taylor Farms Named

The US carried out its eighth consecutive strike night against Iran — hitting petroleum-industry targets near Ahvaz and a water pumping station in Khuzestan province, the second consecutive night of civilian-adjacent infrastructure targeting — as the IRGC struck and disabled two commercial tankers in the Strait of Hormuz (the conflict's first direct tanker kills) and wounded four Kuwaiti sailors in an attack on a Kuwaiti naval vessel, with the nuclear compliance deadline 29 days away and analyst Danny Citrinowicz warning of "a conflict that spirals beyond either side's intentions into a much broader regional war"; Microsoft is routing Copilot prompts in Excel and Outlook to its homegrown MAI models at 10× efficiency vs. GPT 5.4, cutting OpenAI and Anthropic dependency at scale, as Meta greenlighted custom AI chip production; Bitcoin rose to $64,810 (▲1.44%) and Ethereum to $1,855 (▲0.77%) ahead of the CLARITY Act's Senate floor vote opening Monday, with Polymarket at 48% passage and an 8-year dormant wallet moving 5,908 BTC in apparent pre-vote repositioning; and the CDC's Cyclospora outbreak expanded to 34 states with 141 hospitalizations after Taylor Farms de Mexico was named as the contaminated iceberg lettuce supplier, the CDC issuing a targeted exclusion for Taco Bell locations in Indiana, Kentucky, Michigan, Ohio, and West Virginia — while new research found nanoplastics in drinking water actively strengthen bacterial biofilms, amplifying pathogen survival against disinfection.

The IRGC's tanker strikes are the weekend's structural event. Near-blockade conditions had been degrading Hormuz transit through deterrence — risk calculations, elevated insurance premiums, commercial operators diverting. Two vessels struck and disabled converts the strait from a deterrence environment into an active combat zone where commercial transit may become uninsurable at any premium, not merely expensive. Combined with the Khuzestan water pumping station and Friday's desalination plant, the conflict now has a dual-track escalation: US civilian-adjacent targeting inviting an international law challenge, IRGC maritime escalation converting the near-blockade to active combat. Both tracks are simultaneous; neither has a resolution mechanism visible.

Monday opens with two simultaneous binary catalysts that pull in different directions. The IRGC tanker strikes add upward pressure to oil and downward pressure to equities through the oil-inflation channel that broke the traditional safe-haven bond bid on Friday. The CLARITY Act floor vote opening is a potential positive catalyst for crypto and crypto-adjacent equities — 48% passage odds, BTC already up 1.44% on pre-vote optimism. Two events, one open, opposite directions by sector. Sunday 5pm ET equity futures are the first read before Monday's 9:30am open.
