Qualcomm is in talks to acquire AI chip startup Tenstorrent for between $8 billion and $10 billion, according to Reuters — in what would be the most significant bid by a major semiconductor company to challenge Nvidia's dominance of AI infrastructure. Tenstorrent is led by Jim Keller, the veteran chip architect behind Apple's A-series silicon and Tesla's autonomous driving chip, and designs scalable AI accelerators using the open RISC-V instruction set, targeting cloud data centers and edge devices. The deal would come as Qualcomm commits roughly $14 billion to cracking Nvidia's AI chip monopoly through RISC-V and an open compiler strategy; Tenstorrent's most recent valuation was $2.6 billion from a December 2024 Series D backed by Jeff Bezos and Samsung, putting the reported acquisition price at a nearly 4× premium.

Eleven major companies — Google, Microsoft, GitHub, Hugging Face, NVIDIA, Salesforce, Cisco, Databricks, GoDaddy, ServiceNow, and Snowflake — published the Agentic Resource Discovery (ARD) specification, an open standard for AI agents to locate, verify, and connect to tools and other agents at runtime. The spec defines an ai-catalog.json manifest and a registry API for natural-language discovery; GitHub shipped Agent Finder for Copilot, and Hugging Face launched a Discover Tool wrapping its Hub in the ARD envelope. The Anthropic–Department of Defense dispute, meanwhile, escalated: the DOD demanded Anthropic allow Claude to be used for lethal autonomous weapons systems and mass domestic surveillance of Americans, litigation that now represents one of the most consequential confrontations between an AI lab and the US government on record.

The United States and Iran agreed to a mutual stand-down Monday, halting the retaliatory exchange cycle that ran from Friday's IRGC drone attack on a Singapore-flagged Hormuz cargo ship through US CENTCOM retaliatory strikes and the IRGC's counter-attacks in the early hours of Sunday. Trump announced a meeting in Qatar after Tehran "requested talks," and a US official confirmed that trade vessels can now "move freely" in the Strait of Hormuz. The diplomatic sessions originally scheduled in Switzerland — focused on enrichment, inspections, and Lebanon — have been relocated to Doha, Qatar, and resequenced to focus first on maritime security before returning to the nuclear agenda. Iran announced that $6 billion of its $12 billion in frozen assets held in Qatar accounts have been released and are returning to the country.

The stand-down's durability is contested. A senior Israeli security official announced that Iranian cyberattacks on Israel have surged to approximately 4,800 hostile incidents in June, compared to roughly 1,600 in June 2025 — a 3× spike that suggests Iran is redirecting aggressive activity from kinetic to cyber channels as the Hormuz stand-down holds. The IRGC's demonstrated operational independence from Iran's government diplomatic negotiators remains the structural accountability gap: the stand-down is an agreement between governments, but the Guards have shown they can conduct drone attacks on Hormuz shipping without coordinating with Tehran's foreign ministry. MOU Day 12 ends today with 48 days remaining and the Doha session tomorrow as the first test of whether a diplomatic framework can hold under active military pressure.

Teen Cannabis: 2× Psychosis + Bipolar Risk · 463K JAMA Study · B12 Compound Crosses Blood-Brain Barrier → Glioblastoma · Fish Oil: No Cognitive Benefits in 2-Year Trial 2× JAMA 2026: 463,396 adolescents ages 13–17 → age 26 · past-year cannabis use → 2× psychotic disorders · 2× bipolar · diagnoses follow use by ~2 years · Kaiser Permanente + UCSF + USC · B12 compound crosses BBB → accumulates in glioblastoma → nitric oxide delivery (animal study) · Fish oil 2-yr RCT: omega-3s reach brain → no memory, cognition, or Alzheimer's benefits

A JAMA Health Forum study of 463,396 adolescents, followed from ages 13–17 through age 26, found that past-year cannabis use during adolescence was associated with roughly doubled risk of developing psychotic and bipolar disorders — and elevated risk of depressive and anxiety disorders. Critically, cannabis use preceded the psychiatric diagnoses by nearly two years in the study sample, a temporal sequence that strengthens the concern about a causal relationship rather than mere correlation with pre-existing conditions. The study was conducted by researchers from Kaiser Permanente, UCSF, the University of Southern California, and the Public Health Institute — representing one of the largest longitudinal examinations of adolescent cannabis use and psychiatric outcomes ever conducted, arriving as recreational legalization has expanded teen access across most US states.

Two additional science findings round out a data-heavy weekend. Researchers identified a vitamin B12–based compound that crosses the blood-brain barrier — one of the primary barriers to treating glioblastoma, one of the deadliest brain cancers — and preferentially accumulates in tumor tissue, delivering sustained nitric oxide in animal studies; the finding points toward a potential targeted delivery vehicle for future glioblastoma treatments. And a two-year randomized controlled trial of fish oil supplements found that while omega-3 fatty acids were successfully delivered to the brain, participants showed no meaningful improvements in memory, cognition, or Alzheimer's-related brain changes compared to placebo — a significant null result for one of the world's most widely used dietary supplements.
