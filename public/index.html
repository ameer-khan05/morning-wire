The Lede · 07:00 ET

US and Iranian negotiators reached a tentative deal Thursday to extend the ceasefire 60 days and reopen the Strait of Hormuz — the text is reportedly on President Trump's desk but unsigned, and VP Vance said Friday morning the US is "not there yet, but very close." WTI fell to $87.20 on the deal news, and the S&P 500 closed at a new record of 7,563.63 (+0.58%) and the Nasdaq at 26,917.47 (+0.91%) Thursday, aided by Snowflake's 36-39% post-earnings surge on a $1.39 billion Q1 beat and $6 billion AWS deal.

The Iran conflict's Day 91 has produced its clearest diplomatic milestone: a tentative memorandum of understanding that would lift constrictions on Strait of Hormuz navigation, require Iran to clear sea mines within 30 days, end the US naval blockade when commercial traffic resumes, and start a 60-day negotiating window on Iran's nuclear program. The deal text is on Trump's desk. VP Vance says the US is "not there yet but very close." Trump has publicly described his choice as a "solid 50/50" between accepting the framework and resuming full military operations — language that is either a genuine expression of presidential indecision or a maximum-pressure endgame tactic designed to extract final Iranian concessions before signing. The oil market is voting at 65% deal probability: WTI at $87.20 (down from Thursday's $90.35 and May 20's $108.59 peak) prices the deal at high probability without pricing it as done. The full resolution — WTI at $82-84, rate-hike probability below 15%, 10-year yield below 4.35% — awaits Trump's signature. The US simultaneously sanctioned eight Iranian oil tankers Thursday evening, maintaining the maximum-pressure track alongside the diplomatic advance. CENTCOM shot down five Iranian attack drones Friday morning. The next 24-48 hours are the most consequential of the 91-day conflict.

Thursday's simultaneous S&P and Nasdaq records — despite April PCE inflation printing at 3.8%, its highest in nearly three years — demonstrate the market's forward-pricing of the Iran deal scenario. The record closes were powered by Snowflake's extraordinary 36-39% single-day surge after reporting Q1 FY2027 revenue of $1.39 billion (beating the $1.32 billion estimate by 5.3%) and announcing a $6 billion, five-year partnership with AWS to build AI data infrastructure — the data-platform complement to Nvidia's chip-layer result from Tuesday. Thursday afternoon deal reports sent WTI lower and rate-hike expectations declining, completing the conditions for record closes. Friday morning futures are edging up: S&P +0.16%, Nasdaq +0.20%, Russell 2000 +0.57%. Bitcoin recovered to $73,381 from Thursday's $72,714 low as Iran deal optimism improved the macro outlook; Ethereum returned above $2,000 to $2,007. Polymarket Iran peace bets hit $213 million. OpenAI published its Frontier Governance Framework, aligning its safety practices with California and EU AI regulations. Apple signed a multi-year deal to build Foundation Models on Google Gemini.

On the science front, Penn Medicine identified GPNMB — a protein produced by microglia near dying neurons — as the molecule that catalyzes the neuron-to-neuron spread of toxic alpha-synuclein clumps in Parkinson's disease: blocking it with monoclonal antibodies arrests the spread, in findings published in Neuron. A University of Bristol trial published in JAMA Psychiatry showed that tocilizumab — an anti-inflammatory drug used for rheumatoid arthritis that blocks the IL-6 pathway — eases treatment-resistant depression in patients with elevated inflammatory markers, targeting the immune system rather than brain chemistry. UC San Diego's Phase 2 trial showed ION224, targeting the DGAT2 liver enzyme, produced 60% histological improvement in MASH fatty liver patients without requiring weight loss. Three conditions — Parkinson's, depression, fatty liver disease — yielding simultaneously to immune and metabolic pathway interventions in a single week.
