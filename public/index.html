The 52nd G7 summit in Évian-les-Bains, France concluded Wednesday with all seven leaders issuing a joint statement voicing support for the US-Iran MOU — the first multilateral endorsement of the deal from the world's major democracies. Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney described the agreement as "a game changer." The G7 statement also called explicitly for an immediate, robust ceasefire in Lebanon and the disarmament of Hezbollah and restoration of the Lebanese government's monopoly on arms — a unified Western demand that neither Israel nor Hezbollah has accepted. The formal MOU signing in Geneva remains confirmed for Thursday, June 19, with VP Vance and Iran's parliamentary speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf scheduled to sign.

The G7 backing of the MOU converts the deal from a US-Iran bilateral arrangement into a commitment with formal support from every major Western democracy. The Lebanon ceasefire demand — while not directly enforceable — adds diplomatic pressure on Israel to engage on the Lebanon-Hezbollah front that enters the 60-day nuclear negotiating window as an unresolved variable. The G7 meeting in Évian, across Lake Geneva from the signing venue, was a deliberate diplomatic sequencing: summit endorsement today, signing ceremony Thursday, with each step building the international architecture around the deal before the 60-day nuclear clock begins.

A Stanford University retrospective cohort study presented at ENDO 2026 — the Endocrine Society's annual meeting in Chicago — found that semaglutide (sold as Ozempic, Wegovy, and Rybelsus) reduced bone fracture risk by 15% in type 2 diabetes patients compared to other anti-obesity medications, while also delivering greater weight loss. The finding directly counters a prior concern in the clinical literature that GLP-1-related weight loss could thin bones. Scientists also announced the discovery of a hidden world of thousands of food chemicals — dubbed "nutritional dark matter" — that go unmeasured by standard nutrition labels and may carry significant implications for disease risk and healthy aging.

Semaglutide's bone protection appears to work counter to expectation: while weight loss can reduce mechanical load on bones and raise fracture risk, semaglutide's GLP-1 receptor agonism may directly support bone metabolism in ways that offset the weight-loss-related bone thinning. The nutritional dark matter research opens a new front in nutritional science: if thousands of bioactive food chemicals are present in diets but unmeasured, the explanatory power of current nutrition research is substantially limited — and dietary interventions based on nutrient-label data are missing a large share of what food actually does in the body. A separate 3-year study of 3,900 adults aged 19 to 94 found that cognitive health can improve at any age, challenging the assumption that brain sharpness must decline with aging.
