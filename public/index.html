Noam Shazeer — co-author of the 2017 "Attention Is All You Need" paper that introduced the Transformer architecture underlying every major AI model today — left Google DeepMind to join OpenAI as Lead for Architecture Research, two years after Google paid $2.7 billion to bring him back from Character.AI. Simultaneously, a coalition of 42 state attorneys general opened a sweeping investigation into OpenAI, with New York already serving subpoenas ahead of the company's anticipated September IPO, covering advertising claims, sycophancy, data handling, and treatment of minors. China unveiled a $295 billion, five-year national AI infrastructure plan — one of the largest government AI commitments in history.

The Shazeer move is a talent-acquisition signal as much as a research hire: the person most directly responsible for the architecture that made modern AI possible is now at OpenAI as it prepares to go public. The 42-state AG investigation introduces legal and reputational complexity into that IPO timeline precisely when OpenAI needs regulatory clarity. China's $295 billion commitment reframes the competitive dynamic from company-to-company to state-versus-state, with scale that no private company can match unilaterally.

T-Rex: 40 Years to Full Size · Alzheimer's + Hormone Therapy · Spinal Cord Therapy First Patients 40 Years T-Rex: new study of 17 fossils → took ~40 years to reach full size of ~8 tons · prior model: faster growth curve · implication: different life history + ecology than assumed · Alzheimer's biomarker: may influence how hormone therapy affects dementia risk · finding: biomarker status modifies treatment outcome → personalized medicine implication · spinal cord/optic nerve therapy: 2 patients first to receive treatment for condition damaging both · Heliconius butterflies: evolved extraordinary lifespan → little sign of physical decline → aging biology implication

A new study of 17 tyrannosaur fossils — the largest sample-based analysis of T-Rex growth rates ever conducted — found the giant predator likely took approximately 40 years to reach its full size of roughly eight tons, far slower than prior models assumed. Researchers separately found that an Alzheimer's-related biomarker may determine whether hormone therapy helps or harms dementia risk, opening a pathway toward personalized treatment approaches. Two patients became the first in the world to receive a new therapy for a condition damaging both the spinal cord and optic nerve. Heliconius butterflies, researchers found, have evolved an extraordinary lifespan with little physical sign of decline — an aging-biology finding with broad implications.

Monday's science redraws the life histories of both extinct and living species. A slower-growing T-Rex implies different ecological dynamics — a predator spending four decades at non-apex sizes faces different competitive pressures than one that scaled quickly. The Alzheimer's biomarker finding is immediately clinically relevant: if a biomarker determines whether hormone therapy accelerates or reduces dementia risk, current guidelines recommending or discouraging hormone therapy across patient populations may need biomarker-stratified revision.
