The Lede · 06:00 ET

Tim Cook exits. John Ternus inherits Apple. Iran hasn't boarded a plane.

Apple announced Monday evening that Tim Cook will step down as CEO effective September 1 — ending a 14-year tenure that turned the company into the world's most valuable enterprise. John Ternus, 50, Apple's senior vice president of hardware engineering and the architect of the M-series chip transition, will take the role. Cook becomes executive chairman. Apple stock slipped less than 1% in after-hours; the bigger question is strategic: Ternus inherits a company that has materially lagged Google, Microsoft, and Anthropic in the generative AI race, and the market is granting him no honeymoon period.

Meanwhile, Day 53 of the US-Iran conflict produced a split screen Tuesday morning: Vice President Vance boarded a flight to Islamabad for a second round of peace talks, while Iranian state television confirmed no delegation had yet left Tehran — casting real doubt on whether Wednesday's ceasefire expiry can be staved off. Separately, Strategy disclosed a $2.54 billion Bitcoin purchase Monday, the largest single corporate buy since 2024, pushing its Bitcoin stack to 815,061 BTC. BTC recovered to $75,450 on Tuesday morning; S&P 500 and Nasdaq futures each gained 0.5% on cautious peace-talk optimism, and oil slipped back below $88 from Monday's 6.5% spike.

Reporting: Apple · CNBC · CNN · CoinAlertNews

AI predicts melanoma in 6 million Swedes — up to five years before diagnosis.

Researchers from the University of Gothenburg and Chalmers analyzed routine health registry data covering Sweden's entire adult population. Their AI model predicted melanoma with 73% accuracy — compared to 64% using age and sex alone. Individuals flagged in the highest-risk tier faced up to a 33% chance of developing melanoma within five years.
