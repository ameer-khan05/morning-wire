Ozempic · EVOKE Trial · Alzheimer's Fails Failed Novo Nordisk · semaglutide · EVOKE & EVOKE+ phase 3 · 3,800 patients · 2 years · no clinical benefit · program ended

Semaglutide — Ozempic's active ingredient — fails the EVOKE and EVOKE+ phase 3 Alzheimer's trials: no slowing of clinical progression in 3,800 patients over two years. Novo Nordisk ends its semaglutide Alzheimer's program. The largest-ever GLP-1 neurological trial closes the therapeutic chapter.

The EVOKE and EVOKE+ trials — the largest GLP-1 drug trials ever conducted in a neurodegenerative disease, enrolling 3,800 patients with early-stage Alzheimer's and following them for two years — failed their primary endpoint. Oral semaglutide showed no statistically significant difference from placebo in the Clinical Dementia Rating Sum of Boxes score at week 104. Semaglutide did improve some Alzheimer's biomarkers — including p-tau181 reduction — but biomarker improvements did not translate into clinical benefit. Novo Nordisk confirmed it is ending the semaglutide Alzheimer's program entirely. GLP-1 observational data still shows a 70% reduced dementia risk in users, keeping the prevention question open; the EVOKE failure closes specifically the therapeutic intervention chapter for established Alzheimer's disease — the market that the industry valued at $30+ billion in hypothetical revenue.
