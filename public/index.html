The Lede · 07:00 ET

The Dow Jones Industrial Average crossed 50,000 for the first time in history on Thursday — then reversed, closing at 49,596.97, down 313 points — as the Iran deal's implied response window passed without resolution and chip stocks digested Wednesday's monster gains. Friday at 8:30 a.m. ET, the April jobs report delivered the number Wall Street had feared: +62,000 nonfarm payrolls, below the floor of the consensus range, confirming that oil-economy stagflation is now visible in the employment data.

Thursday was the market pricing in doubt after Wednesday's dual-catalyst euphoria. With no Iranian formal response to the one-page memo framework arriving in the implied 48-hour window, and AMD and Supermicro giving back 3-4% of their Wednesday surges, the session's character was a systematic giveback of optimism. The Dow's intraday 50,000 crossing — a historic milestone that generated exactly the market reaction one might expect before reversing — captures the psychology precisely: the market wants to cross 50,000 on an Iran peace deal and a confirmed AI earnings cycle, but will not hold the level on peace-deal hope alone. The S&P 500 fell 0.38% to 7,337.11; the Nasdaq fell 0.13% to 25,806.20; the Russell 2000 fell 1.63% to 2,839.63, reversing roughly half of Wednesday's 3.84% small-cap peace-deal surge. WTI oil ticked down to $94.81, holding near the post-deal-hope equilibrium without new directional information from Iran.

Friday morning's NVIDIA-IREN partnership announcement — filed Thursday after hours — is the AI infrastructure sector's most significant supply-side commitment of 2026. NVIDIA and IREN announced a strategic partnership to deploy up to 5 gigawatts of AI infrastructure across IREN's global data center pipeline, with NVIDIA receiving an option to invest up to $2.1 billion in IREN at $70 per share. IREN stock surged 13-19% on the news. The EU Council and Parliament reached a provisional agreement May 7 on Digital Omnibus VII, delaying high-risk AI Act compliance deadlines from August 2026 to December 2027 for Annex 3 AI systems (employment, education, healthcare applications) — the 15-month delay that European industry had been lobbying for. The Trump administration and Chinese government are preparing formal AI governance talks for the May 14-15 Trump-Xi Beijing summit — the first official US-China AI dialogue under Trump — the same week the White House publicly accused China of "industrial-scale" theft of American AI models.

The Iran deal's moratorium duration is the publicly identified sticking point: Iran has proposed 5 years of enrichment suspension, the US has demanded 20 years, and three sources told PBS NewsHour that 12-15 years is the likely landing zone. The US expects Iran's formal counter-proposal within 48 hours. US CENTCOM separately disclosed Friday morning that American naval forces intercepted Iranian attacks on guided missile destroyers in the Strait of Hormuz and responded with defensive strikes — confirming the conflict is militarily active even as diplomatic talks proceed. Lebanon's State Department confirmed that ceasefire renewal talks will be held May 14-15, coinciding with the Trump-Xi summit dates. A major Lancet Psychiatry study — the largest yet on GLP-1 drugs and mental health — found that semaglutide use was associated with 44% lower depression risk, 38% lower anxiety risk, and 42% fewer psychiatric hospital visits compared with periods of non-use in the same individuals.

Semaglutide · Mental Health · Lancet ▼ 44% Depression risk ▼ 44% · anxiety ▼ 38% · psychiatric hospital visits ▼ 42% · substance use disorder care ▼ 47% · Lancet Psychiatry Swedish national cohort · within-person design · GLP-1 mental health benefit confirmed

The Lancet Psychiatry publishes the largest study yet on GLP-1 drugs and mental health: semaglutide users show 44% lower depression risk, 38% lower anxiety risk, and 42% fewer psychiatric hospital visits and sick leave during drug use versus non-use — extending GLP-1's documented benefits far beyond weight loss and diabetes management.

The Swedish national cohort study tracked the same individuals during semaglutide use and non-use periods, a within-person design that controls for baseline health characteristics and reduces confounding. The results are consistent across outcomes: lower depression, lower anxiety, fewer psychiatric hospitalizations, less substance use disorder treatment, and less work absence for psychiatric reasons — all significantly reduced during GLP-1 use periods. The magnitude of the mental health benefit — a 44% relative risk reduction in depression — is comparable to first-line antidepressant medications in some head-to-head meta-analyses. The proposed mechanisms involve GLP-1 receptor expression in the brain's reward circuitry, gut-brain axis signaling, and the independently documented mental health benefits of the metabolic improvements that accompany weight loss. The study does not establish the mental health indication as a licensed use; that would require a randomized controlled trial. But it is the most rigorous observational evidence yet that GLP-1 drugs improve mental health as a consistent secondary effect.
