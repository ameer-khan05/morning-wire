The Lede · 06:00 ET

Ceasefire cracks. Hormuz shuts again. The rally dies.

The 10-day US-Iran truce barely survived the weekend. On Sunday, the USS Spruance intercepted the Iranian cargo vessel Touska in the Gulf of Oman — prompting Tehran to call it "maritime piracy," shut the Strait of Hormuz again, and signal no intention of sending negotiators to the next round of peace talks in Pakistan. Trump told Bloomberg the ceasefire expires Wednesday evening and that extension is "highly unlikely."

Oil responded the only way it knows how: WTI surged 6.5% to $89.29, clawing back most of Friday's ceasefire discount. The Nasdaq's 13-day winning streak — the longest since 1992 — ended right there. Crypto added its own wreckage overnight: North Korea's Lazarus Group exploited a $292 million hole in Kelp DAO's cross-chain bridge, triggering an $8 billion bank run on Aave that turned the broader digital-asset market red.
