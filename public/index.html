Psilocybin: Single 25mg Dose Induces Brain Changes Lasting 1+ Month — Nature Comms · Skin Damage: Hidden Biomarker Found Before Collagen Deteriorates · Plant Compound Reduces RA Inflammation in Rats · Bee Circuits Encode Age-Based Job Assignment 1 mo Psilocybin (25mg): higher brain entropy within 60min → strengthened connections → improved well-being 1+ month (ScienceDaily Jul 19) · skin damage: hidden biomarker precedes visible collagen change · plant compound: RA swelling + joint damage ▼ in rats · bee circuits: age-based job routing in brain

A single 25mg dose of psilocybin produced measurable increases in brain entropy within 60 minutes and strengthened neural connections that lasted more than a month in 28 healthy participants who had never tried psilocybin, per a new Nature Communications study (ScienceDaily, July 19). Researchers described the mechanism as psilocybin "loosening stereotyped patterns of brain activity," with therapeutic implications for depression, addiction, and anxiety. Separately, scientists discovered a hidden biomarker of skin damage that appears before collagen fibers visibly thin or break — enabling earlier intervention — and a plant-derived compound significantly reduced swelling, inflammation, and joint damage in rats with rheumatoid arthritis while rebalancing immune activity.

The psilocybin brain-entropy finding is mechanistically significant: higher entropy means the brain is processing a richer body of information, a measurable correlate of the increased mental flexibility participants reported. The month-long persistence of structural brain changes from a single dose suggests psilocybin's therapeutic window is significantly wider than the 6–8 hour experience itself. The skin damage biomarker, found before collagen visibly deteriorates, shifts the intervention window earlier — the same paradigm shift SORLA represented for Alzheimer's tau.
