The Lede · 07:00 ET

S&P 500 and Nasdaq close at record highs — then Tuesday opens with Brent breaking $111. China blocks Meta's $2B Manus deal. Iran enters Day 60 as the UN warns of its worst supply chain shock since COVID.

Monday's session ended with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite both closing at all-time records — S&P at 7,173.91 (+0.12%) and Nasdaq at 24,887.10 (+0.20%), the first simultaneous record closes since January. Tuesday opens on a different footing: S&P futures are down 0.7% and Nasdaq futures are down 1.2% as Brent crude crosses $111 and the US-Iran conflict enters Day 60 without a deal. UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres warned Tuesday that the Hormuz stalemate risks "the worst supply chain disruption since COVID-19 and the war in Ukraine" — a message backed by more than sixty nations calling for the urgent reopening of the strait. Trump reviewed Iran's Hormuz proposal — reopen the strait, end the war, defer nuclear talks — but US officials confirm he is unlikely to accept without nuclear concessions, which Iran's framework explicitly defers. The week's central market event begins tonight: Alphabet, Meta, and Microsoft all report Q1 earnings after the close.

In tech: China's National Development and Reform Commission issued a one-line notice Monday ordering Meta to cancel its $2 billion acquisition of AI startup Manus — four months after the deal was sealed — citing technology-transfer concerns and national security laws. The block is Beijing's first use of foreign investment law to retroactively unwind a finalized US tech acquisition, a direct escalation of the tech war that raises immediate questions for every US company with China-founded portfolio companies. Separately, OpenAI surpassed $25 billion in annualized revenue — the same week Anthropic publicly crossed $30 billion ARR, marking the first time a rival has publicly exceeded OpenAI's run rate. Google unveiled a $750 million fund for AI agent startups and rolled out its eighth-generation TPUs. Qualcomm and OpenAI's AI smartphone chip partnership — confirmed by analyst Ming-Chi Kuo Monday — continues to ripple through semiconductor markets.

Bitcoin retreated to $77,370 Tuesday morning — down 1.65% from Monday's close — as oil's surge above $111 reintroduced inflation expectations and dampened risk appetite ahead of the FOMC meeting. Despite the price slide, crypto ETFs attracted $933 million in single-day inflows, pushing total crypto fund AUM to $155 billion — the highest since February. Bitcoin 2026 Conference continues in Las Vegas today with the BIP-360 quantum-resistant wallet panel scheduled for Day 3. In science: UC Irvine researchers reversed age-related vision loss in mice by targeting the ELOVL2 gene and restoring vital fatty acids in the retina — findings published in Science Translational Medicine offering a new pathway for macular degeneration. Separately, the gut bacterium Morganella morganii, when it interacts with common environmental pollutants, produces a neuroinflammatory molecule linked to depression — a microbiome-based explanation for treatment-resistant mood disorders.

UC Irvine reverses age-related vision loss in mice — ELOVL2 gene targeting and fatty acid therapy restore retinal function.

Scientists at UC Irvine reversed age-related vision loss in mice by targeting the ELOVL2 gene — a regulator of long-chain fatty acid production that declines with age — and supplementing depleted polyunsaturated fatty acids in the retina. The findings, published in Science Translational Medicine, show lipid therapy can restore visual function and reverse cellular aging signs in the eye, with implications for age-related macular degeneration. Separately, researchers found the gut bacterium Morganella morganii — when interacting with common environmental pollutants — produces a neuroinflammatory molecule linked to depression, offering a new microbiome-based explanation for treatment-resistant mood disorders.
