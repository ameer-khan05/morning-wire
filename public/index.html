The Lede · 09:00 ET

President Trump announced Sunday night on Truth Social that "The deal with the Islamic Republic of Iran is now complete" — authorizing the US naval blockade to end and mine-removal operations in the Strait of Hormuz to begin — as global markets surged Monday morning, WTI crude crashed 5.74% to $79.46, and the formal signing ceremony was set for June 19 in Geneva with VP Vance in attendance.

The US-Iran war, which began February 28 and lasted 107 days, ended with Trump's Sunday night announcement, confirmed by Iran's deputy foreign minister Kazem Gharibabadi. Qatar and Pakistan received credit for the breakthrough mediation. The deal establishes a 60-day ceasefire framework with negotiations on broader sanctions relief and Iran's nuclear program to follow; the Strait of Hormuz will reopen for shipping after mine removal. The S&P 500 surged 1.49% to 7,542 — within 38 points of its May 31 all-time record — as Nasdaq gained 2.38% and oil crashed to its two-month low. Israel said separately it will not withdraw from Lebanon despite the broader agreement.

Separately, the US government issued an export control directive on June 12 suspending all foreign national access to Anthropic's newly launched Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models — the first government-forced AI model recall in US history — citing national security authorities and an alleged jailbreak vulnerability. Anthropic shut both models for all users to comply, publicly disagreeing with the standard. At the American Diabetes Association's 2026 Scientific Sessions, Eli Lilly's oral GLP-1 drug orforglipron posted landmark Phase 3 results in type 2 diabetes, and a second oral GLP-1, elecoglipron, showed strong A1c and weight-loss outcomes — extending the drug class into needle-free formulations for the first time at clinical scale.
