Vancomycin Revived: pghi-4 Molecule Overcomes Bacterial Resistance · SORLA Protein Defends Against Alzheimer's Tau Tangles in Mouse Study · Sleep >8.5 Hours Linked to Tau Biomarkers · Fluoride-Free Gel Rebuilds Tooth Enamel Rx Vancomycin: paired with pghi-4 → blocks resistance mechanism → restores effectiveness against VRE · SORLA: extra copies → less tau, less atrophy, healthier neurons (mice) · sleep >8.5h: linked to tau protein biomarkers in new study · fluoride-free gel: rebuilds enamel body cannot replace

Scientists revived vancomycin — a last-resort antibiotic that vancomycin-resistant enterococci (VRE) had learned to defeat — by pairing it with a small molecule called pghi-4, which blocks the bacterial resistance mechanism and restores the antibiotic's binding effectiveness. Separately, the protein SORLA was identified as a natural defense against tau tangles in Alzheimer's disease: mice with extra SORLA showed less tau accumulation, less brain atrophy, and healthier neuronal connections, pointing toward a preventive therapeutic approach.

VRE bacteria evade vancomycin via D-Ala-D-Lac cell-wall substitution; pghi-4 blocks that step, restoring the antibiotic's mechanism. AMR kills ~700K/year globally; reviving a proven drug is faster than a new class. SORLA as a natural tau limiter represents a preventive Alzheimer's intervention that acts before tangles form — where the field needs to move.
