The Lede · 09:00 ET

Iran's foreign ministry said Sunday that the Islamabad Memorandum will not be signed today — contradicting President Trump, who announced the deal would be signed on his 80th birthday — as Qatari negotiators flew to Tehran to bridge the gap, while the IDF struck a Hezbollah command center in south Beirut, killing at least one, after three Hezbollah drones struck northern Israel.

Iran's FM spokesman Esmaeil Baqaei said the US-Iran memorandum of understanding will not be signed June 14, attributing the delay to "hesitancy on the other side" — meaning Washington — while adding the signing could still occur "in the coming days." Trump had posted Saturday that the deal would be signed on his birthday. Qatar dispatched negotiators to Tehran overnight in a fresh push to close remaining gaps. The deal — committing Iran indefinitely to never developing nuclear weapons, unfreezing Iranian assets, and beginning Hormuz's 30-day reopening — remains on the table but unsigned as Day 106 of the conflict opens.

Separately, the IDF struck what it described as a Hezbollah command center in a south Beirut apartment early Sunday, killing at least one and injuring four, after three Hezbollah drones struck northern Israeli territory. Bitcoin climbed to $64,385 on weekend trading — up 1.1% from Friday's $63,710 close — as Standard Chartered declared that BTC's cycle low is likely already in at $59,000. SpaceX's IPO filing revealed the company holds 18,712 BTC as a strategic reserve asset. Meta cut 600 additional jobs from its Superintelligence Labs division and Zuckerberg admitted the broader AI restructuring included errors. A study of 111,646 women found GLP-1 drugs like Ozempic are associated with a 30% lower risk of developing breast cancer, presented at ASCO 2026.

GLP-1 Drugs Cut Breast Cancer Risk 30% · 111,646 Women · ASCO 2026 · ETH Zurich Alzheimer's Compound 30% GLP-1 drugs (Ozempic · Wegovy · Mounjaro · Zepbound): 30% ↓ breast cancer risk · 111,646 women age 45-80 · BMI ≥ 25 · Penn Medicine · presented ASCO 2026 Annual Meeting · published JCO Oncology Practice · mechanism: reduces inflammation · alters cancer pathways · caveat: observational · ETH Zurich: experimental Alzheimer's compound · blocks novel pathway · reduces nerve cell loss in mice · extends survival · targets process existing drugs do not address

A study of 111,646 women presented at the 2026 ASCO Annual Meeting found that GLP-1 drugs — including Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, and Zepbound — are associated with a 30% lower likelihood of developing breast cancer compared with non-users, adding the drug class's most significant cancer prevention signal yet to its growing list of benefits beyond obesity and diabetes management. ETH Zurich researchers separately published an experimental compound that blocks an Alzheimer's-related pathway untargeted by existing treatments, reducing nerve cell loss and extending survival in mice — a potential complement to recently approved anti-amyloid therapies.

The GLP-1 breast cancer finding carries immediate clinical implications: tens of millions of current GLP-1 users may be receiving incidental cancer protection, and the finding — published in JCO Oncology Practice and presented at ASCO — is likely to accelerate dedicated breast cancer prevention trials. The ETH Zurich Alzheimer's work targets a different biological pathway from amyloid and tau, offering a potential complement to lecanemab and donanemab rather than a competitor to them.
