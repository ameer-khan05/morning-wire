The Lede · 07:00 ET

Intel tops its year-2000 high and Nasdaq hits a record. DeepSeek drops V4 to challenge the frontier at $0.14 per million tokens.

Friday reversed Thursday's Iran-driven selloff decisively: Intel's blockbuster Q1 earnings sent shares surging more than 25% — the stock crossed its year-2000 all-time high for the first time in a quarter century — while Nvidia reclaimed its $5 trillion market-cap crown, together driving the Nasdaq 1.63% higher to a record 24,836.60 and the S&P 500 up 0.80% to a record 7,165.08, the 18th consecutive session of semiconductor-index gains. Markets are closed Saturday; Friday close values are shown. The same session brought DeepSeek's V4 launch: V4 Flash and V4 Pro are mixture-of-experts models with 1-million-token context windows that the Chinese startup claims "close the gap" with GPT-5.5 and Gemini 3.1 Pro on reasoning benchmarks — at $0.14/$0.28 per million tokens for Flash, a fraction of US lab pricing — sharpening the global AI cost war exactly one year after DeepSeek upended Silicon Valley.

The Israel-Lebanon ceasefire extension announced Thursday did not survive the weekend intact: Israel launched airstrikes in southern Lebanon on Friday targeting Hezbollah rocket launchers in Yater and Kafra, killing at least 6, as Hezbollah returned fire and called the three-week extension "meaningless." The US naval blockade of Iran continued tightening — Defense Secretary Hegseth said Tehran faces a "stark choice" between a nuclear deal and economic collapse — while Iran's foreign ministry confirmed no talks with US negotiators are planned. Bitcoin sits at $77,510 Saturday morning as crypto leverage unwinds after the failed $80,000 test; open interest fell 6% in 24 hours and BTC dominance ticked to 60.14%.

In science: UC Irvine researchers published evidence in Science Translational Medicine that supplementing the retina with specific polyunsaturated fatty acids can reverse age-related vision decline by restoring the ELOVL2 aging gene's enzymatic function — the first targeted genetic reversal of age-related macular decline in animal models, with AMD implications. A separate study profiles "SuperAgers," adults over 80 maintaining memory equivalent to people three decades younger, challenging conventional assumptions about cognitive aging's inevitability. The Oracle–Related Digital–Blackstone $16 billion Michigan Stargate data center closed financing Friday, adding concrete infrastructure to OpenAI's capacity plans.

Science & Health ELOVL2 Vision reversal · UC Irvine · aging gene

UC Irvine reverses age-related vision loss by targeting the ELOVL2 aging gene in retinal tissue.

Researchers at UC Irvine published evidence in Science Translational Medicine that targeted polyunsaturated fatty acid supplementation can reverse age-related vision decline by restoring ELOVL2 gene function in the retina — the first targeted reversal of this aging mechanism in animal models, with implications for AMD treatment. Genetic variants in ELOVL2 are also linked to faster AMD progression, enabling risk stratification. Separately, new "SuperAger" research — adults over 80 with memory comparable to 50-year-olds — is rewriting what we thought possible for the aging brain.
