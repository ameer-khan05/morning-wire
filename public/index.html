The Lede · 07:00 ET

Iran submits a 14-point peace plan — Trump says he "can't imagine" it is acceptable, warns strikes could resume — as the IRGC issues a deadline for the US blockade to end and Israel's IDF expands evacuation orders north of the Litani River for the first time, with 11 days to Lebanon's ceasefire expiry.

Iran presented a formal 14-point peace proposal through Pakistan on Saturday, offering to negotiate termination of the war within a 30-day window in exchange for the lifting of the US naval blockade, reparations, the release of frozen assets, guarantees against future military aggression, and new Strait of Hormuz governance terms. President Trump confirmed he was briefed on the "concept of the deal" but said Sunday he "can't imagine" the proposal would be acceptable, adding that air strikes could resume if Iran "misbehaves." The IRGC simultaneously issued a statement characterizing the US position as offering either "an impossible military operation or a bad deal," declaring Iran "fully prepared" for resumed conflict — a coordinated dual-track signal: negotiate through Pakistan while publicly hardening the regime's posture. The IDF issued evacuation warnings for towns north of the Litani River for the first time since the ceasefire took effect in April — a geographic expansion of its Lebanon operational posture that signals preparation for ground movement into new territory as the May 14 ceasefire expiry approaches with 11 days remaining. Lebanon's health ministry reports 73 killed and 163 injured since its last casualty update on April 30. Iran's parliament advanced a 12-article Hormuz sovereignty law to full chamber debate — the IRGC already deposited the first $2 million toll from diverted shipping traffic into Iran's Central Bank on April 23.

Microsoft reported Q3 FY2026 earnings of $82.9 billion in revenue — Azure cloud grew 40%, the company's AI business surpassed a $37 billion annual revenue run rate, and Copilot reached 20 million enterprise seats. Simultaneously, OpenAI announced a restructured partnership with Amazon Web Services, accelerating its drift from Microsoft toward Amazon infrastructure in what CNBC described as "an aggressive move" after months of subtle repositioning — a significant signal about the reliability of the OpenAI-Microsoft relationship that Microsoft shares will price at Monday's open. Berkshire Hathaway held its first annual meeting under CEO Greg Abel in Omaha on Saturday — the first since Buffett's announcement without him chairing proceedings in more than six decades; Warren Buffett (95) sat in the front row as an observer while Abel presided over Q1 2026 operating earnings of $11.3 billion, doubled from the prior year's $9.6 billion, in a meeting that replaced Buffett's trademark folksy wisdom with detailed business discussions and drew a noticeably smaller crowd. Spirit Airlines grounded all operations on Saturday — the first major US airline shutdown in 25 years — reflecting the aviation sector's mounting fuel-cost stress from Brent at $116.90.

Bitcoin consolidated at $78,281 on Sunday morning — down slightly from Saturday's $78,324, holding above $78,000 for a second consecutive day without testing $80,000. The SEC formally scheduled a roundtable on the CLARITY Act, the digital asset market structure legislation whose Senate markup remains unscheduled with 75 days to the GENIUS Act implementing deadline. The Bhutan sovereign wealth fund's government-associated wallet transferred $287 million in BTC on May 1, raising near-term selling pressure questions at an already technically sensitive level below $80,000. Baylor College of Medicine published findings in Nature Neuroscience showing that boosting Sox9 — a protein that regulates astrocyte activity — activates the brain's own star-shaped support cells to physically engulf and remove amyloid plaques from Alzheimer's disease mouse models while preserving memory function, pointing toward a potential endogenous treatment pathway distinct from the direct pharmacological approach that has dominated and largely failed in Alzheimer's drug development for three decades.

Alzheimer's · Sox9 · Brain Self-Cleaning Sox9 Baylor College of Medicine · Nature Neuroscience · astrocytes clear plaques · memory preserved · endogenous mechanism

Baylor College of Medicine: boosting Sox9 protein activates astrocytes to physically clear Alzheimer's amyloid plaques from mouse brains while preserving memory. Nature Neuroscience. The brain's own support system — not an external drug — as the clearing agent.

Researchers at Baylor College of Medicine published findings in Nature Neuroscience showing that increasing Sox9 — a protein that regulates astrocyte activity during aging — activates those star-shaped brain support cells to engulf and remove amyloid plaques from Alzheimer's mouse models while preserving memory and cognitive function. Lower Sox9 accelerated plaque buildup; higher Sox9 enhanced astrocyte structural complexity and plaque clearance at a scale the researchers described as "vacuum cleaner"-level. The finding points toward a potential treatment strategy that harnesses the brain's own endogenous support system, distinct from the direct pharmacological attack on plaques that has dominated and largely disappointed in Alzheimer's drug development for three decades.
