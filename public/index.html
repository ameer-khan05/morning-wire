The Lede · 09:00 ET

Trump claimed Thursday that the United States has "ended the war" with Iran — saying both sides agreed to a memorandum of understanding to stop the fighting — and cancelled further planned strikes, but Iran's official spokesman denied any deal had been finalized and Tehran accused Washington of being "greedy" and changing the text of the peace agreement, leaving the ceasefire's status contested as of Friday morning.

Markets responded as though the deal were real: the Dow surged 923 points Thursday to close at 50,841 — its largest single-day gain of the 104-day conflict era — while the S&P 500 advanced 1.75% and the Nasdaq jumped 2.54%, recovering all of Wednesday's Iran-driven losses and then some. Oil fell hard: WTI crude dropped more than 4% as traders priced out the Hormuz closure risk premium, while gold slid 2.07% as defensive positioning unwound and the UST 10-year yield fell 11 basis points. The draft deal's reported terms — the US commits to lifting oil sanctions on Iran, Iran pledges to reopen the Strait of Hormuz within 30 days — would, if signed Sunday as Trump suggested, represent the most consequential geopolitical resolution since the Abraham Accords.

Against that backdrop, SpaceX begins trading today on the Nasdaq under ticker SPCX at $135 per share — the company raised $75 billion in its IPO, making it the largest initial public offering in history, more than 2.5 times Saudi Aramco's prior record of $29.4 billion. SpaceX's $1.78 trillion valuation at listing means the combined Anthropic ($965B), OpenAI ($852B), and SpaceX IPO pipeline now exceeds $3.6 trillion in stated value — the most concentrated week of technology capital market activity in history. Bitcoin climbed to $63,718 (+3.4%) Friday morning as crypto markets priced the Iran deal optimism; analysts estimate a confirmed ceasefire could push BTC above $78,000. In science, GLP-1 drugs — the medication class behind Ozempic, Wegovy, and Mounjaro — were found to reduce breast cancer risk by approximately 30% in a large study presented at the ASCO Annual Meeting and published in JCO Oncology Practice.

President Trump claimed Thursday that the United States has "ended the war" with Iran, asserting that both sides agreed to a "very strong memorandum of understanding" to stop the fighting, and that Iran has committed to never pursuing a nuclear weapon — the core US demand throughout the 104-day conflict. Trump cancelled planned further strikes and said a signing ceremony could occur "soon," possibly in Europe with Vice President Vance attending. Iran's official spokesman contradicted him directly: "Iran has not reached a final decision regarding any agreement," with Tehran additionally accusing Washington of being "greedy" and unilaterally changing the text of the peace agreement.

The gap between Trump's announcement and Iran's denial is the defining uncertainty of Friday morning. The draft deal terms — as reported by Iranian state media — include a US commitment to lift oil sanctions on Iran and Iran's pledge to reopen the Strait of Hormuz within 30 days. Those terms, if true, would satisfy Iran's two core economic demands and represent a significant US concession on the sanctions front. Whether Iran's supreme leadership has approved them is the question markets are watching.

A large study presented at the 2026 American Society of Clinical Oncology Annual Meeting and published in JCO Oncology Practice on June 3 found that women taking GLP-1 drugs — the medication class that includes Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, and Zepbound — were approximately 30% less likely to develop breast cancer, with researchers hypothesizing the effect is linked to the metabolic improvements and immune system enhancement associated with the drug class. Separately, researchers identified a new Alzheimer's disease target and created an experimental compound that blocks a damaging process inside brain cells; in mice, the treatment slowed nerve cell loss and reduced Alzheimer's-related pathological changes. A third study reported the first successful human trial of an AI-designed universal coronavirus vaccine, finding it safe and well tolerated.

The GLP-1 breast cancer finding adds to a rapidly expanding body of evidence suggesting GLP-1 drugs' benefits extend far beyond weight loss and blood sugar control — into cancer prevention, cardiovascular protection, and potentially neurological health. The ASCO presentation marks the highest-profile clinical oncology conference, giving the finding immediate visibility across the oncology field. The AI-designed coronavirus vaccine is a proof-of-concept for computational vaccine design that could compress development timelines for future pandemic threats.
