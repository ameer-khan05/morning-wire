Lead Story

Meta & YouTube Found Negligent in Landmark Social Media Addiction Trial

A Los Angeles jury found Alphabet's YouTube and Meta liable for harm done to a young user, ordering the companies to pay $3 million in compensatory damages. The plaintiff said Instagram and YouTube contributed to depression and suicidal thoughts. A separate New Mexico jury delivered a similar verdict. These rulings could set a sweeping legal precedent for how tech platforms are held accountable for their design choices — particularly features engineered for engagement and retention among young users.
