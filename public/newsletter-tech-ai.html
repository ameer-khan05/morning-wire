OpenAI Hits $2B Monthly Revenue, Plans IPO

OpenAI has crossed a major milestone, now generating $2 billion per month in revenue while advancing toward a public offering. The company's new GPT-5.4 model features an unprecedented 1 million-token context window and scores 75% on OSWorld-V benchmarks, demonstrating significant gains in reasoning and task completion. With total funding now reaching $122 billion, OpenAI continues to dominate the AI landscape at a valuation of $730 billion.

Fujitsu Engineering 1.4nm AI Processor for Edge Inference

Fujitsu is advancing on-device AI with a specialized neural processing unit (NPU) built at the 1.4nm node at its Rapidus fabrication facility in Hokkaido. The chip is engineered specifically for edge AI inference, enabling real-time processing without relying on cloud connectivity. This move represents Japan's strategic push to compete in advanced semiconductor manufacturing for AI workloads.

Americans Increasingly Concerned About AI Harms

A Quinnipiac poll reveals that more than half of U.S. adults believe artificial intelligence will cause significant harm to society, underscoring growing public skepticism about AI deployment. In response to these concerns, the 2026 Responsible AI Symposium is convening industry leaders, researchers, and policymakers to address ethical frameworks and governance standards. The gathering reflects mounting pressure on tech companies to demonstrate safety and accountability in their AI systems.

AI-First Dominance at Y Combinator Demo Day

Eighty-five percent of startups pitching at Y Combinator's Winter 2026 Demo Day are building AI-first products, marking the highest proportion ever recorded. This trend underscores the venture capital community's conviction that artificial intelligence is reshaping every sector from healthcare to finance to creative industries. The concentration of AI talent and capital in the startup ecosystem signals accelerating innovation at the edges of the industry.

AMD Launches Ryzen AI 400 Series for Laptops

AMD unveiled its Ryzen AI 400 series processors at CES 2026, bringing specialized AI acceleration to consumer laptops and mobile workstations. The new chips integrate dedicated neural engines capable of handling local AI inference, enabling users to run large language models and generative AI applications without cloud dependencies. This processor family represents AMD's effort to capture market share in the rapidly growing on-device AI segment.

Anthropic's Claude Mythos Model Surfaced in Data Leak

A significant data breach has exposed details of Anthropic's unreleased Claude Mythos model, which reportedly surpasses the current Opus 4.6 in capability and reasoning performance. The leak raises serious questions about AI model security and the risks of storing sensitive intellectual property in vulnerable environments. Industry experts are calling for stronger cybersecurity protocols across AI research organizations as model weights and training techniques become increasingly valuable targets for competitors and malicious actors.

Source: Fortune

Apple Rebuilds Siri with Google Gemini Technology

Apple has overhauled its voice assistant, integrating Google's Gemini model as the underlying engine for Siri in iOS 26.4. The updated assistant runs on Apple's Private Cloud Compute infrastructure, keeping user interactions on-device and encrypted while leveraging Gemini's advanced natural language understanding. This partnership reflects Apple's decision to adopt best-in-class LLM technology rather than develop its own, signaling a pragmatic shift in the company's AI strategy.
