★ Lead Story

Anthropic's Mythos: The Most Powerful AI Model Ever Built — Leaked, Feared, and Coming Soon

A configuration error in Anthropic's content management system inadvertently exposed the existence of Claude Mythos (internally codenamed "Capybara"), a model that the company describes as a "step change" in AI capability and "the most capable we've built to date." Compared to Claude Opus 4.6, Mythos delivers dramatically higher scores on benchmarks for software coding, academic reasoning, and — most controversially — cybersecurity penetration testing.

The cybersecurity implications are what have Washington most alarmed. Anthropic is privately briefing top government officials that Mythos could enable AI agents to independently penetrate corporate, government, and municipal networks with "wild sophistication and precision." The company acknowledges the model makes large-scale cyberattacks significantly more feasible and is working to implement robust safeguards before any general release.

Mythos is described as a new tier above Opus — larger, more capable, and substantially more expensive to run. Anthropic says it is prioritizing efficiency improvements to bring costs down before wider availability, but early access customers are already testing the model in controlled environments. The leak has reignited debates about the pace of frontier AI development and whether safety protocols can keep up.

Consumer & Enterprise AI

Apple's Reimagined Siri: The Largest AI Assistant Deployment in History

Apple's completely rebuilt Siri launches with iOS 26.4 this week, powered by Google's Gemini models for complex reasoning while Apple retains control over privacy through its Private Cloud Compute infrastructure. The new Siri can read and act on screen content, chain multiple actions from a single request, and maintain natural multi-turn conversations. With 2.2 billion active Apple devices, this is the largest single deployment of advanced AI assistant capabilities ever attempted.

Google Opens Personal Intelligence to All U.S. Users

Google's "Personal Intelligence" feature — allowing Gemini to draw on data from Gmail, Photos, YouTube, and other connected apps for context-aware responses — is now available to free-tier users across Search, Chrome, and the Gemini app. Previously limited to paid subscribers, the expansion signals Google's aggressive strategy to establish Gemini as the default AI layer across its ecosystem.

AI Infrastructure Capital Surge: Nscale Raises $2B, Ayar Labs $500M

The physical backbone of AI is attracting massive capital. Nscale secured a $2B Series C at a $14.6B valuation to build GPU cloud infrastructure, while MIT spinout Ayar Labs raised $500M Series E led by NVIDIA and AMD for its silicon-photonic chips that promise to dramatically reduce data center energy consumption. Meanwhile, Mistral raised $830M in debt financing to acquire 13,800 Nvidia chips for its Paris data center.
