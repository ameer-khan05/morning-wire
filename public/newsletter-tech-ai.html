OpenAI Closes $122B Funding Round, Reaches $852B Valuation

In what may be the most consequential funding round in startup history, OpenAI has closed a $122 billion Series C, valuing the company at $852 billion post-money. The raise reflects the company's extraordinary growth trajectory: OpenAI is now generating $2 billion in monthly recurring revenue and approaching 1 billion weekly active users across its products.

The valuation makes OpenAI one of the most valuable private companies in history—surpassing even the largest sovereign wealth funds in capitalization. The capital will fund the next generation of AI research and infrastructure, including training of increasingly capable frontier models and expansion of inference capacity to meet global demand.

The Stories

Q1 2026 Shatters Venture Funding Records

Global startup investment reached $300 billion in Q1 2026 across 6,000 funded startups—a stunning 150% quarter-over-quarter increase and the highest quarterly total in venture history. More remarkably, four of the five largest venture rounds ever were announced in just the first three months of 2026: OpenAI's $122B Series C, Anthropic's $30B funding round, xAI's $20B raise, and Waymo's $16B investment. The concentration of mega-rounds reflects the market's acute focus on AI infrastructure and capability.

Source: Crunchbase

Google Launches Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite

Google released Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite, an efficiency-focused variant of its flagship model designed for developers and enterprises prioritizing speed and cost. The new model delivers 2.5x faster response times and 45% faster output generation compared to previous versions, while maintaining strong reasoning capabilities. Priced at $0.25 per million input tokens, Flash-Lite targets high-volume inference workloads where latency and economics matter as much as capability.

Source: Google

NVIDIA Partners with Alpamayo, Invests $2B in Marvell

NVIDIA announced a strategic partnership with Alpamayo for autonomous vehicle development using advanced digital twin technology, while simultaneously committing $2 billion in investment to Marvell Technology for next-generation AI networking hardware and silicon photonics. The dual move signals NVIDIA's expanded ambitions beyond consumer and enterprise AI—positioning the company at the center of autonomous systems infrastructure and the underlying semiconductor components powering the next wave of AI deployment.

Source: NVIDIA

Elon Musk Announces SpaceX-xAI Merger

In a move that consolidates AI and space exploration, Elon Musk announced plans to merge SpaceX and xAI, aiming to embed Grok AI models directly into SpaceX's operational systems. The integration envisions fully autonomous spacecraft and robotic Mars colonies powered by advanced agentic AI—combining xAI's frontier models with SpaceX's hardware and launch capabilities. The merger represents one of the most ambitious AI-hardware integrations announced to date.

Source: Various

Software Sector Rallies on AI Monetization

Enterprise software stocks surged as legal and policy barriers to AI deployment continued falling. ServiceNow gained 5.5%, Salesforce rose 3%, and Adobe climbed 2.7% following developments in AI copyright policy and government support. Enterprise legal departments, once cautious about AI liability, are now green-lighting massive software deployments. The shift reflects growing confidence that the regulatory environment will support—rather than hinder—AI monetization.

Source: Financial Markets Data

U.S. Judge Rules Trump Admin Violated Free Speech by Banning Anthropic AI

A federal judge ruled that a Trump administration directive banning Anthropic's AI models from government systems violated First Amendment protections. The decision sets a precedent that government restrictions on specific AI systems may constitute unconstitutional speech suppression. The ruling opens questions about the limits of government authority in regulating private AI companies' market access and signals that courts may scrutinize AI policy through a free speech lens.

Source: Council on Foreign Relations

Alibaba Pledges $100B AI and Cloud Revenue Target

Chinese tech giant Alibaba announced an ambitious five-year plan to reach $100 billion in annual revenue from its AI and cloud business. The commitment reflects Alibaba's strategic bet on becoming a dominant player in enterprise AI infrastructure serving the Asia-Pacific region. The pledge signals heightened competition for AI infrastructure dominance beyond the U.S. market.

Source: Various

AI Startups Command Record Seed Valuations

Seed-stage AI startups are commanding unprecedented valuations and investment checks at the earliest funding stages. Beyond the mega-deals capturing headlines, more than 10 AI startups have raised Series A rounds exceeding $1 billion—a threshold previously reserved for later-stage companies. The phenomenon reflects investor appetite for early-stage AI ventures and suggests the ecosystem is shifting earlier in the capital stack.

Source: TechCrunch
