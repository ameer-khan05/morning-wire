Q1 Venture Funding Shatters All Records at $300 Billion

Investors poured $300 billion into 6,000 startups globally in Q1 2026, up 150% quarter-over-quarter and year-over-year. Four of the five largest venture rounds ever were closed this quarter: OpenAI ($122B), Anthropic ($30B), xAI ($20B), and Waymo ($16B)—collectively representing $188 billion in funding. Artificial intelligence claimed 80% of all venture capital, underscoring the market's overwhelming focus on AI infrastructure and capabilities.

Source: Crunchbase News

DeepSeek V4 Launches With Open Weights, Rivaling US Frontier Models

DeepSeek released V4, a one-trillion-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model released under the Apache 2.0 open-source license. Remarkably, the model was trained for just $5.2 million, achieving performance competitive with US frontier models. The release underscores the democratization of frontier AI and the emerging efficiency advantages in model training.

Source: DevFlokers AI News

Google DeepMind's Gemini 3.1 Adds Real-Time Voice and Image Analysis

Google DeepMind released Gemini 3.1 with advanced real-time voice and image analysis capabilities, enabling seamless multimodal interactions. The release also introduced a compression algorithm that slashes AI inference costs by reducing memory requirements six-fold, a critical breakthrough for cost-effective deployment at scale.

Anthropic Blocks Chinese Labs From Using Claude, Identifies IP Theft

Anthropic announced it has blocked Chinese-controlled companies from accessing Claude and identified DeepSeek, Moonshot, and MiniMax as illicitly extracting model capabilities from Claude without authorization. The action reflects growing tensions in the global AI race and Anthropic's commitment to protecting its intellectual property.

Microsoft committed $10 billion to Japan's AI infrastructure and development ecosystem, signaling continued global expansion of AI compute and a strategic push into Asian markets. The investment underscores the strategic importance of diversifying AI infrastructure beyond North America.

Utah Becomes First State to Allow AI to Renew Drug Prescriptions

Utah granted AI systems autonomous authority to renew drug prescriptions for patients, marking a historic milestone in healthcare automation and the growing regulatory trust in AI decision-making. The policy change enables faster prescription renewals while maintaining safety standards.
