Nvidia's Rubin Platform Launches — Six New Chips, One AI Supercomputer

At GTC 2026, Nvidia made a bold strategic pivot that redefines the future of AI infrastructure. The company unveiled the Rubin platform, a comprehensive ecosystem featuring six new architectural innovations designed to dominate AI inference and agentic workflows. The lineup includes the Vera CPU for general compute, the Rubin GPU as the flagship accelerator, NVLink 6 Switch for interconnect, ConnectX-9 SuperNIC for networking, BlueField-4 DPU for data plane acceleration, and the Spectrum-6 Ethernet Switch for fabric connectivity.
Equally significant, Groq unveiled its new 3 language processing unit, positioning itself as a serious contender in the specialized AI chip space. Major cloud providers—AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft, and Oracle Cloud Infrastructure—have already committed to deploying Vera Rubin systems in the second half of 2026. This signals a fundamental shift in how enterprises will architect their AI infrastructure.
