Lead Story

Gemini 3 Deep Think Goes Live: AI Enters the Age of Specialized Reasoning

Google has officially launched Gemini 3 Deep Think, its most advanced reasoning model to date, now available in the Gemini app for Ultra subscribers with early API access for researchers and enterprises. This release marks a fundamental shift in how AI models are positioned and deployed—moving away from the generalist chatbot paradigm toward specialized tools engineered for demanding technical work.

Unlike its predecessors, Gemini 3 Deep Think is explicitly designed for professional-grade use cases: scientific research, complex engineering problems, mathematical proofs, and multi-step logical reasoning. The model excels at tasks that require sustained, deliberate computation over casual conversation. For enterprises and technical teams, this represents a watershed moment—access to reasoning-first AI that can handle the kind of work that previously required specialized expertise or painstaking manual analysis.

The rollout strategy reveals Google's confidence in the model's capabilities and its commitment to iterative deployment. By targeting Ultra subscribers and technical researchers first, Google is effectively conducting a large-scale beta test while simultaneously capturing early revenue. This approach echoes OpenAI's strategy with GPT-5.4 but with a distinctive emphasis on reasoning over raw scale, positioning Gemini 3 Deep Think as the thinking person's AI—not the fastest, but the most thorough.

Stories

OpenAI Surpasses $25B Revenue, Eyes Late 2026 IPO

OpenAI's annualized revenue has crossed the $25 billion threshold, making it one of the fastest companies to reach this milestone. Industry sources indicate the company is preparing for a potential IPO in late 2026, which would value the organization significantly higher and unlock investment returns for early stakeholders. The revenue surge reflects aggressive enterprise adoption and consumer subscription growth.

Source: TechStartups

Apple Partners with Google to Power Next-Gen Siri with Gemini

Apple has finalized a deal to integrate Google's 1.2 trillion parameter Gemini model into Siri, running on Apple Private Cloud Compute for on-device processing. The partnership underscores a broader strategic shift: rather than building its own foundational AI models from scratch, Apple is leveraging existing leaders while maintaining user privacy through proprietary infrastructure. iOS 27 will also open Siri to third-party AI services via the App Store, creating an ecosystem play.

Source: TechStartups

GPT-5.4 Launches for Professional Tasks

OpenAI's GPT-5.4 is now available for enterprise customers, featuring a record-breaking 1.05 million token context window. The model is optimized for complex multi-step projects including document analysis, code generation, and creative work across longer contexts. Early adopters report substantial productivity gains in technical domains.

Source: BuildFastWithAI

Google's TurboQuant Disrupts Memory Chip Market

Google has unveiled TurboQuant, a compression technology that allows AI models to run using a fraction of their original memory footprint. The breakthrough is creating ripple effects across the hardware market—DDR5 memory prices have begun declining as demand projections are revised downward. Bloomberg reports the technology is causing a strategic split in the memory chip sector, with some vendors doubling down on AI compute while others pivot to consumer and edge devices.

Source: TechStartups

NVIDIA Launches Enterprise Agent Toolkit

NVIDIA has released an open-source platform for building and deploying autonomous AI agents in enterprise environments. The toolkit abstracts away infrastructure complexity, allowing developers to focus on agent orchestration and task design. Early partners include major cloud providers and consulting firms.

Source: DevFlockers

Alibaba Introduces Wukong Enterprise AI Platform

Alibaba has launched Wukong, a multi-agent platform designed for enterprise workflows including document editing, approval processes, and research coordination. The platform competes directly with similar offerings from other large cloud providers and represents China's push into enterprise AI infrastructure.
